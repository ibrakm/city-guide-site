# Lesson 9: Giving Directions & Explaining Locations

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Giving and understanding directions, explaining locations, prepositions of place

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning how to give directions and explain locations in business situations. This is very useful when you meet clients, when you visit other companies, or when people visit you!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Have you ever given directions to someone in English? Was it easy or difficult?
2. Have you ever been lost in a new city? What did you do?
3. When business visitors come to your company, do you give them directions?
4. What's difficult about giving or understanding directions in a foreign language?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. You have 4 minutes. Share your experiences!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear some stories! Who has given directions in English? How did it go?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now, I'm writing 'DIRECTIONS & LOCATIONS' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm: What words do you use to give directions? What phrases do you know? Think about landmarks, movements, positions. 2 minutes!"

*Write "DIRECTIONS & LOCATIONS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Let's collect your vocabulary. Give me direction words!"

*Collect on board. Expected: turn left/right, go straight, walk, cross, opposite, next to, near, far, block, corner, traffic light, building, entrance, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you say when someone needs to turn?"
- "How do you describe where something is?"
- "What landmarks help people find places?"

---

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now I'm going to read two conversations. In both, someone is giving directions. Listen carefully. I'll read each one twice."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Conversation 1: Giving Directions to Your Office**

*A client calls your office*

**Receptionist:** Good morning, Global Solutions. How can I help you?

**Client:** Hello, I have a meeting at your office at 2 PM today. I'm at the train station now. Could you tell me how to get there?

**Receptionist:** Of course! From the train station, it's very easy. When you leave the station, turn right onto King Street. Walk straight for about 5 minutes until you see a large bank on your left.

**Client:** Okay, turn right, walk straight, bank on the left. Got it.

**Receptionist:** Good. At the bank, turn left onto Park Avenue. Walk for about 2 minutes. Our building is on the right side, just after the coffee shop. It's a tall glass building. You can't miss it!

**Client:** So that's left at the bank onto Park Avenue, and your building is on the right after the coffee shop?

**Receptionist:** Exactly! The entrance is on the ground floor. Come to reception and they'll direct you to the meeting room.

**Client:** Perfect! Thank you so much!

**Receptionist:** You're welcome. See you at 2!

---

**Conversation 2: Explaining Location of a Conference Room**

*A visitor arrives at your company*

**Visitor:** Excuse me, I'm looking for Conference Room B. Could you help me?

**Employee:** Of course! Conference Room B is on the third floor. Take the elevator over there – it's opposite the reception desk.

**Visitor:** Okay, third floor.

**Employee:** Yes. When you get out of the elevator, turn right. You'll see a long corridor. Walk along the corridor, past the marketing department.

**Visitor:** Past marketing, okay.

**Employee:** At the end of the corridor, you'll see two rooms. Conference Room A is on the left, and Conference Room B is on the right, next to the water cooler.

**Visitor:** Great! So elevator to third floor, turn right, walk to the end, room B is on the right?

**Employee:** Exactly! It takes about 2 minutes. The meeting starts at 10, right? You have plenty of time.

**Visitor:** Yes, perfect. Thank you very much!

**Employee:** You're welcome. Enjoy your meeting!

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. In Conversation 1, where is the client starting from?
2. What are the landmarks the receptionist mentions?
3. Where is the company building?
4. In Conversation 2, which floor is Conference Room B on?
5. What does the visitor pass on the way to the room?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. Were these directions clear? Why?
2. What did the people do to check they understood?
3. How do you make sure people understand your directions?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's learn key language for giving directions and describing locations."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**GIVING DIRECTIONS:**

**1. Starting Point:**
- "From [place], [directions]"
- "When you leave [place], [directions]"
- "Start at [place]"

**Examples:**
- "From the station, turn right."
- "When you leave the hotel, walk straight."

**2. Movement Verbs:**
- "Go/Walk straight (ahead)"
- "Turn left/right"
- "Cross [street/road]"
- "Pass [landmark]"
- "Walk along [street]"
- "Go past [landmark]"
- "Take the [first/second] left/right"

**Examples:**
- "Walk straight for 5 minutes."
- "Turn left at the traffic lights."
- "Go past the bank."

**3. Describing Distance:**
- "It's about [number] minutes walk."
- "Walk for about [number] minutes."
- "It's [number] blocks from here."
- "It's very close/nearby."

**4. Using Landmarks:**
- "You'll see [landmark] on your left/right."
- "It's just after/before [landmark]."
- "It's opposite [landmark]."
- "It's next to [landmark]."
- "Look for [landmark]."

**DESCRIBING LOCATION:**

**5. Prepositions of Place:**
- "on the [floor number] floor"
- "on the left/right (side)"
- "at the end of [corridor/street]"
- "in front of / behind [landmark]"
- "between [A] and [B]"
- "opposite / across from [landmark]"
- "next to / beside [landmark]"
- "near / close to [landmark]"

**Examples:**
- "The office is on the fifth floor."
- "It's opposite the bank."
- "The room is at the end of the corridor."

**6. Checking Understanding:**
- "Did you get that?"
- "Is that clear?"
- "Do you understand?"
- "Would you like me to repeat that?"

**7. Confirming Directions:**
- "So I turn left at the bank?"
- "Let me confirm – the building is on the right?"
- "Can you repeat the last part?"

### **Common Mistakes:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Be careful:
- 'Turn left/right' NOT 'turn to left'
- 'On the third floor' NOT 'in the third floor'
- 'Go straight' NOT 'go direct'"

### **Practice Dialogues:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll read Person A, you all read B."

**Dialogue 1:**
**A:** Excuse me, how do I get to the meeting room?  
**B:** It's on the second floor. Take the elevator and turn left.  
**A:** Okay, second floor, turn left.  
**B:** Yes, then walk to the end of the corridor. The meeting room is on the right.  
**A:** Perfect, thank you!

**Dialogue 2:**
**A:** Could you tell me how to get to your office from the subway?  
**B:** Sure! When you exit the subway, turn right onto Main Street.  
**A:** Turn right onto Main Street, okay.  
**B:** Walk straight for about 10 minutes. Our building is on the left, just after the post office.  
**A:** Great! After the post office, on the left. Got it!

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs, then create your own. Give directions from this classroom to another place in the building!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Preposition**

**Instructions:**
Choose: *on / at / next to / opposite / between / past*

1. The office is _______ the third floor.
2. The bank is _______ the post office and the café.
3. Walk _______ the supermarket – don't go inside!
4. The hotel is _______ the train station, on the other side of the road.
5. The meeting room is _______ the elevator.
6. Turn left _______ the traffic lights.

**Answers:** 1. on, 2. between, 3. past, 4. opposite, 5. next to, 6. at

**Teacher Script:**
"Work with your partner. Choose the right preposition."

*Give 3 minutes. Check together.*

### **Exercise 2: Put the Directions in Order**

**Instructions:**
Number these directions 1-6 in the correct order:

___ a) The building is on the left, opposite a bank.  
___ b) Walk straight for about 5 minutes.  
___ c) From the hotel, turn right onto Market Street.  
___ d) You'll see our office. Come to the reception on the ground floor.  
___ e) Then turn left at the traffic lights.  
___ f) Walk for another 2 minutes.

**Correct Order:** c, b, e, f, a, d

**Teacher Script:**
"Put them in logical order, then practice giving these directions to your partner!"

*Give 4 minutes.*

### **Exercise 3: Draw and Direct**

**Teacher Script:**
"Student A: Draw a simple map with 5 locations (your office, a bank, a café, a hotel, a subway station). Don't show your partner!

Student B: Ask for directions to reach the office from the subway. Student A gives directions. Student B draws the route.

Then compare your maps! Are they the same?"

*Give 3 minutes for this activity.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Perfect! Now realistic practice! You'll work in pairs. I'll give you situation cards. Some of you will give directions, some will ask for them. Let's practice!"

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Receptionist**
A visitor is calling. They're at City Center Hotel. They need directions to your office for a 3 PM meeting. Your office is:
- 10 minutes walk from the hotel
- Directions: Exit hotel, turn left on Oxford Street, walk straight 5 minutes, turn right at McDonald's onto Park Lane, walk 3 minutes, office building on right (tall blue building), next to a bookstore.

**Situation 1B: Visitor**
You're at City Center Hotel. You have a meeting at 3 PM at a company office. You don't know where their office is. Call them and ask for directions. Ask them to repeat if needed. Confirm the directions.

---

**Situation 2A: Office Worker**
A visitor has arrived at your company. They need to find:
- Conference Room 3 (4th floor)
- Directions from ground floor reception: Take elevator to 4th floor, turn right, walk past IT department and sales department, at end of corridor turn left, Conference Room 3 is second door on left, opposite the kitchen.

**Situation 2B: Visitor**
You've arrived at a company for a meeting in Conference Room 3. You're at the ground floor reception. Ask someone for directions. The building is big and confusing! Make sure you understand – ask them to repeat or clarify.

---

**Situation 3A: Local Employee**
Your client is visiting from abroad. They're staying at the Grand Hotel. You're taking them to lunch at "The Garden Restaurant." The restaurant is:
- 15 minutes walk from the hotel
- Directions: From hotel, turn right, walk along River Street, cross the bridge, turn left onto Garden Avenue, walk past shopping mall, restaurant is on right, between a flower shop and a pharmacy.

**Situation 3B: Visiting Client**
You're visiting from abroad, staying at the Grand Hotel. Your business contact is taking you to lunch at a restaurant. You want to know how to walk there (you like walking). Ask for directions. You might go there again, so remember the route!

---

**Situation 4A: Conference Organizer**
A conference attendee is lost! They need to find:
- Exhibition Hall B (where their presentation is)
- They're currently at the main entrance
- Directions: From entrance, go straight through main lobby, take escalator to Level 2, turn right, walk past Hall A (on left), Hall B is at the end, on the right. It's a 5-minute walk. Their presentation starts in 10 minutes!

**Situation 4B: Conference Attendee**
You're at a big conference center. You have a presentation in Exhibition Hall B in 10 minutes! You're at the main entrance and lost. Ask someone for help. You're a bit stressed – you can't be late!

### **Additional Challenge Role-plays:**

**Situation 5A: Employee**
Give directions from your company to the nearest:
1. Subway/train station
2. Good restaurant
3. ATM/Bank
(Use real or imaginary locations)

**Situation 5B: Visitor**
You've just finished a meeting. You need to:
1. Get to the train station (you have a train in 30 minutes)
2. Find a place to eat lunch
3. Get cash from an ATM
Ask for directions to all three!

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair: take your situation cards. Don't look at your partner's card! Person A gives directions, Person B asks and confirms. Try to be clear and helpful! You have 12 minutes – do at least 2 situations."

*Monitor. Listen for clear directions and good use of prepositions.*

*After 12 minutes:*

"Great work! Who got lost? Who gave very clear directions? Let's see one example. Pair 4, can you show us Situation 2?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Excellent! What made the directions clear? What helped the person understand?"

*Discuss: landmarks, confirmation, repetition, clear language.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful directions today! I heard great language. Marco said 'Walk past the bank on your left' – very clear! Yuki said 'Let me confirm – I turn right at the traffic lights?' – perfect checking!

Three important tips for giving directions:

1. **Use landmarks** – don't just say 'turn left in 200 meters.' Say 'turn left at the big bank' or 'turn left at the traffic lights.' Landmarks are easier to see!

2. **Check understanding** – after giving directions, ask 'Is that clear?' or 'Did you get that?' Make sure they understood!

3. **Be patient** – if someone doesn't understand, repeat using different words. Don't get frustrated!

Think: When was the last time you needed directions? What helped you understand? 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: when visiting a new place, don't be afraid to ask! Most people are happy to help!"

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

### Teacher Script:
"For homework, choose one:

**Option 1 (Writing):** Write directions (100-120 words):
- From your home to your office/workplace
- From your office to the nearest train station
Use at least 6 direction phrases from today!

**Option 2 (Speaking):** Record yourself (2-3 minutes) giving directions:
- From a famous landmark in your city to another famous place
- From your company entrance to your desk/office
Speak clearly and use landmarks!

**Option 3 (Drawing):** Draw a simple map of the area around your office. Label important places in English (bank, restaurant, station, etc.). Bring it to class and practice giving directions using your map!

Questions? See you next lesson!"
