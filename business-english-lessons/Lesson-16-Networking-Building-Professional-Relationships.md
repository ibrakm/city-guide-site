# Lesson 16: Networking & Building Professional Relationships

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Advanced networking skills, conference introductions, exchanging business cards, building lasting connections

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome to Lesson 16! We're now moving into advanced workplace communication. Today we're focusing on networking and building professional relationships. This is about more than just introducing yourself – it's about creating connections that last!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Have you ever attended a networking event or professional conference? What was it like?
2. What's the difference between meeting someone once and building a professional relationship?
3. Do you find networking easy or difficult? Why?
4. How do you stay in touch with professional contacts?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your networking experiences – both successes and challenges!"

"Let's hear from you! Who enjoys networking? Who finds it challenging?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

**Teacher adds:**
"Many people find networking difficult, even in their own language! But it's one of the most important business skills. Today we'll learn how to network confidently and build real relationships."

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read about a successful networking experience at an international conference. Notice how the person doesn't just introduce themselves – they build a real connection. Read carefully."

### **Reading Text:**

**Building a Valuable Connection at the Asia-Pacific Trade Conference**

Last month, I attended the Asia-Pacific Trade Conference in Singapore. On the first day, during the coffee break, I was standing near the refreshments when I noticed a woman reading the conference program intently.

I approached her with a smile. "Excuse me, are you trying to decide which session to attend next? I'm having the same problem – there are so many interesting topics!"

She laughed. "Exactly! I'm Anna Chen, by the way. I work for Global Textiles in Taiwan."

"Nice to meet you, Anna. I'm Marco Rossi from Fashion Direct in Italy. We import clothing and textiles from Asia." We shook hands and exchanged business cards.

"Oh, interesting!" Anna said. "What kind of textiles are you looking for?"

"Mainly sustainable and eco-friendly fabrics. We're trying to move toward more environmentally conscious products. What about your company?"

"We actually specialize in sustainable cotton and bamboo fabrics! That's why I'm at this conference – to meet potential partners like you."

We both realized this could be a valuable connection. We talked for another 15 minutes about our companies, our challenges, and our goals. I learned that Anna had been in the textile industry for 12 years and knew a lot about sustainable production.

Before the next session started, I said, "Anna, I'd really like to continue this conversation. Would you be interested in having dinner tonight? I'd love to learn more about your products."

"That would be great! Let me give you my direct phone number and email."

That evening, over dinner, we discussed a potential partnership. We discovered we had similar business philosophies and could help each other. Anna introduced me to sustainable fabric suppliers, and I connected her with Italian fashion brands looking for sustainable materials.

Six months later, we've done three successful orders together, and Anna has become not just a supplier, but a trusted business partner and friend.

**Key Lessons from This Experience:**
1. Start with a friendly, open approach (commenting on the conference program)
2. Find common ground quickly (both interested in textiles)
3. Listen actively and show genuine interest
4. Exchange detailed contact information (not just business cards)
5. Follow up promptly (dinner invitation same day)
6. Think long-term (building partnership, not just one transaction)
7. Add value to each other (mutual introductions and connections)

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**

1. How did Marco start the conversation with Anna?
2. What common interest did they discover?
3. What did they do to continue the relationship beyond the conference?
4. How has their relationship developed over six months?

*Give 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent analysis! Now let's learn advanced language for networking and relationship building. This goes beyond basic introductions!"

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**STARTING CONVERSATIONS (Beyond Basic Introductions):**

**1. Opening Lines at Networking Events:**
- "Excuse me, are you [doing/interested in] ...?"
- "I couldn't help noticing [observation]. I'm interested in that too."
- "This is a great event, isn't it? Is this your first time attending?"
- "That was an interesting presentation. What did you think?"
- "I see you're from [company/country]. I'd love to hear about..."

**Examples:**
- "Excuse me, are you here for the technology sessions?"
- "I couldn't help noticing your company badge. You're in the textile industry too!"

**2. Finding Common Ground:**
- "What brings you to this conference/event?"
- "What are you hoping to get out of today?"
- "Are you working on any interesting projects?"
- "What's your main focus at the moment?"
- "We seem to have similar interests in..."

**BUILDING THE CONNECTION:**

**3. Showing Genuine Interest:**
- "That sounds really interesting. Tell me more."
- "How did you get started in [industry/role]?"
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
- "I'd love to hear your perspective on..."
- "How long have you been working in [field]?"

**4. Identifying Potential Collaboration:**
- "It sounds like we might be able to help each other."
- "There could be some interesting synergies between our companies."
- "I think we should explore this further."
- "This could be a valuable partnership."
- "We might have complementary expertise."

**5. Exchanging Business Cards Professionally:**
- "Here's my business card. I'd love to stay in touch."
- "Let me give you my card. Please feel free to contact me anytime."
- "May I have your card?"
- "I'll send you an email with [information/details]."
- **When receiving:** "Thank you. [Look at card] Oh, you're the [title]. That's impressive!"

**IMPORTANT:** In many Asian cultures, receive business cards with both hands and look at them respectfully before putting them away!

**CONTINUING THE RELATIONSHIP:**

**6. Suggesting Next Steps:**
- "Would you like to continue this conversation over coffee/lunch/dinner?"
- "I'd love to schedule a proper meeting to discuss this further."
- "Can I send you some information about [topic]?"
- "Would you be interested in [activity/meeting/collaboration]?"

**7. Keeping in Touch:**
- "I'll follow up with you next week."
- "Let me send you that article/contact/information I mentioned."
- "I'll connect with you on LinkedIn."
- "I'll be in touch soon about [topic]."

**8. Following Up After the Event:**
- "It was a pleasure meeting you at [event]."
- "I wanted to follow up on our conversation about [topic]."
- "As promised, I'm sending you [information]."
- "I've been thinking about what we discussed..."

**BUILDING LONG-TERM RELATIONSHIPS:**

**9. Adding Value:**
- "I came across this article and thought of you."
- "I'd like to introduce you to [person] – I think you'd have a lot to discuss."
- "I thought you might be interested in [opportunity/event]."
- "Let me know if there's anything I can help you with."

**10. Staying Connected:**
- "How are things going with [project they mentioned]?"
- "I hope your [event/launch/project] went well!"
- "It's been a while – shall we catch up?"
- "I'd love to hear how things are developing for you."

### **Networking Do's and Don'ts:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember these networking rules:

**DO:**
- ✅ Be genuinely interested in others (not just selling)
- ✅ Listen more than you talk (70% listening, 30% talking)
- ✅ Follow up within 48 hours
- ✅ Add value to your connections
- ✅ Remember details about people (take notes after!)
- ✅ Be yourself and authentic

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Immediately try to sell your products
- ❌ Monopolize someone's time
- ❌ Only talk about yourself
- ❌ Collect cards without building connections
- ❌ Forget to follow up
- ❌ Be fake or overly pushy"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice a networking conversation. I'll read Person A, you all read Person B."

**Person A:** Excuse me, I couldn't help noticing you're from TechSolutions. I'm very interested in technology for retail. What brings you to this conference?  

**Person B:** Hi! Yes, I'm here to learn about AI applications in business. I'm Sarah Chen, Product Manager. And you?  

**Person A:** I'm David Park from Retail Innovations. We're looking into AI for customer service. It sounds like we have similar interests!  

**Person B:** Definitely! What specific applications are you exploring?  

**Person A:** Mainly chatbots and customer analytics. How about you?  

**Person B:** Similar areas! We've just launched a new AI customer service platform. I'd be happy to tell you more about it.  

**Person A:** That sounds really interesting! Would you like to grab a coffee after this session?  

**Person B:** I'd love to! Let me give you my card. Here's my direct email and phone number.  

**Person A:** Great! Here's mine. I'll send you a calendar invite for coffee at 3 PM?  

**Person B:** Perfect! Looking forward to it.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs. Then create your own networking conversation. Remember to: start naturally, find common ground, show interest, and suggest next steps!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic networking today! You all did a great job building connections, not just collecting business cards!

Let's remember the FIVE KEYS to successful networking:

1. **Be genuinely interested** – Ask questions and listen! Don't just talk about yourself.

2. **Find common ground** – Look for shared interests, challenges, or goals.

3. **Add value** – Think: 'How can I help this person?' not just 'What can they do for me?'

4. **Suggest next steps** – Don't just say 'Let's stay in touch.' Be specific: 'Can I send you that information?' 'Would you like to meet for coffee?'

5. **Follow up within 48 hours** – Send that email! Make that introduction! Take action!

One more important point: Networking is about building relationships, not making sales. The best networkers help others first, and business follows naturally.

Think: What will you do differently at your next networking event? Discuss 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: Your network is one of your most valuable business assets. Invest in it!"
