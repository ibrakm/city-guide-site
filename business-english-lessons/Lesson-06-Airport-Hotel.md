# Lesson 6: At the Airport & Hotel

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Travel vocabulary, check-in procedures, handling problems at airport and hotel

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Hello everyone! Today we're learning about business travel – specifically at airports and hotels. Let me ask you some questions."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Do you travel for business? How often?
2. What do you do when you arrive at the airport?
3. Have you ever had a problem at an airport or hotel? What happened?
4. What's the difference between business travel and holiday travel?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your travel experiences!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear some stories! Who has traveled for business? Where did you go?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now, I'm writing two headings: 'AIRPORT' and 'HOTEL' on the board. In your pairs, brainstorm words and phrases for each place. Think about: what you do there, what you say, what problems can happen. 2 minutes!"

*Write "AIRPORT" and "HOTEL" on the board with a line between them.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me airport words first."

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: check-in, boarding pass, gate, flight, delayed, luggage, baggage claim, customs, security, passport, etc.*

"Good! Now hotel words?"

*Expected: reception, check in/out, reservation, room key, Wi-Fi password, wake-up call, breakfast included, single/double room, etc.*

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read about a business traveler's experience. Read both parts."

### **Reading Text:**

**Part 1: At the Airport**

James Chen is traveling from Singapore to London for a business conference. He arrives at the airport three hours before his flight. First, he goes to the check-in counter.

"Good afternoon. I have a flight to London at 6 PM," James says.

The agent asks, "May I see your passport and booking confirmation, please?"

James shows his documents. The agent checks them and asks, "Are you checking any baggage?"

"Yes, one suitcase," James replies.

"Would you prefer a window or aisle seat?"

"Aisle, please."

The agent prints his boarding pass. "You're all set. Your gate is B12. Boarding starts at 5:30 PM. Have a good flight!"

James goes through security and waits at the gate. Unfortunately, the flight is delayed by 30 minutes due to bad weather.

**Part 2: At the Hotel**

James arrives in London late at night. He takes a taxi to his hotel. At the reception desk, he says, "Good evening. I have a reservation under the name James Chen."

The receptionist checks the computer. "Yes, Mr. Chen. We have a single room for you for three nights. Could I see your passport and credit card, please?"

James gives his documents. The receptionist continues, "You're in room 305 on the third floor. Breakfast is served from 6:30 to 10 AM in the restaurant on the ground floor. Here's your room key. The Wi-Fi password is on this card."

"Thank you. What time is checkout?" James asks.

"Checkout is at 11 AM. If you need anything, just call reception. Enjoy your stay!"

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. Where is James traveling to?
2. What does the airline agent ask James?
3. Why is the flight delayed?
4. What documents does the hotel receptionist ask for?
5. What information does the receptionist give James?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. Is James a good traveler? Why?
2. What questions would you ask at a hotel reception?
3. What do you do when your flight is delayed?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's learn key phrases for airports and hotels."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**AT THE AIRPORT:**

**1. At Check-in:**
- "I have a flight to [destination] at [time]."
- "I'd like to check in, please."
- "I'm checking [number] bag(s)."
- "Could I have a window/aisle seat?"

**Examples:**
- "I have a flight to Tokyo at 3 PM."
- "I'm checking two bags."

**2. Asking for Information:**
- "Where is gate [number]?"
- "What time is boarding?"
- "Has the flight been delayed?"
- "Where is baggage claim?"

**AT THE HOTEL:**

**3. At Reception (Checking In):**
- "I have a reservation under [name]."
- "I'd like to check in, please."
- "Is breakfast included?"
- "What time is checkout?"

**4. Asking for Help:**
- "Could I have a wake-up call at [time]?"
- "What's the Wi-Fi password?"
- "Is there a business center?"
- "Could you call a taxi for me?"

**5. Reporting Problems:**
- "There's a problem with [item]."
- "The [item] isn't working."
- "Could you send someone to fix [problem]?"

**Examples:**
- "The air conditioning isn't working."
- "There's a problem with the shower."

### **Useful Vocabulary:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember these important words:"
- boarding pass (NOT ticket – ticket is what you buy, boarding pass is what you get at check-in)
- baggage/luggage (both are correct)
- check in (verb) vs. check-in (noun): "I need to check in" / "Where is check-in?"

### **Practice Dialogues:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! Dialogue 1 – at the airport. Listen first."

**Dialogue 1:**
**Passenger:** Good morning. I have a flight to Paris at 11 AM.  
**Agent:** May I see your passport, please? Are you checking any bags?  
**Passenger:** Yes, one suitcase.  
**Agent:** Here's your boarding pass. Gate C7. Boarding is at 10:30.  
**Passenger:** Thank you. Where is the gate?  
**Agent:** Go through security and turn left.

**Dialogue 2:**
**Guest:** Good evening. I have a reservation under Sarah Kim.  
**Receptionist:** Yes, Ms. Kim. A single room for two nights. May I have your credit card?  
**Guest:** Here you are. What time is breakfast?  
**Receptionist:** From 7 to 10 AM. Your room is 412. Here's your key.  
**Guest:** Thank you. Is there Wi-Fi?  
**Receptionist:** Yes, the password is on this card.

**Teacher Script:**
"Practice both dialogues in pairs. Then create your own using different destinations and questions!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good work! Now some exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Match Questions and Answers**

**Questions:**
1. Where is gate B12?
2. What time is checkout?
3. Are you checking any bags?
4. Is breakfast included?
5. Has the flight been delayed?
6. What's the Wi-Fi password?

**Answers:**
a) Yes, it's included in the room rate.  
b) It's on the card with your room key.  
c) Yes, one suitcase.  
d) Go through security and turn right.  
e) Checkout is at 11 AM.  
f) Yes, by 20 minutes.

**Answers:** 1-d, 2-e, 3-c, 4-a, 5-f, 6-b

**Teacher Script:**
"Match the questions and answers with your partner. Then practice asking and answering!"

*Give 3 minutes. Check together.*

### **Exercise 2: Complete the Conversations**

**Instructions:**
Fill in the blanks with appropriate words.

**Conversation 1 (Airport):**
A: I have a _______ to New York at 2 PM.  
B: May I see your _______, please?  
A: Here it is. Could I have an _______ seat?  
B: Certainly. Here's your _______ pass.

**Conversation 2 (Hotel):**
A: I have a _______ under Tom Wilson.  
B: Yes, Mr. Wilson. Could I see your _______?  
A: What time is _______ served?  
B: From 6:30 to 10 AM. Here's your room _______.

**Answers:**
Conv. 1: flight, passport, aisle, boarding
Conv. 2: reservation, passport/credit card, breakfast, key

**Teacher Script:**
"Complete and practice these conversations!"

*Give 4 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now for realistic practice. You'll do two role-plays: one at the airport, one at a hotel. Work in pairs."

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Passenger**
You're checking in for a flight to Frankfurt at 9:30 AM. You have:
- 1 suitcase and 1 carry-on bag
- You want an aisle seat
- Ask: where is the gate? what time is boarding?
- Your flight is in 2 hours

**Situation 1B: Airline Agent**
A passenger is checking in for Frankfurt flight at 9:30 AM. You need:
- Their passport
- Ask about baggage
- Ask seat preference
- Give boarding pass
- Gate: A15, boarding at 9:00 AM

---

**Situation 2A: Hotel Guest**
You're checking into the Grand Business Hotel. You have:
- Reservation for 2 nights under your name
- You need to wake up at 6 AM tomorrow
- Ask about: Wi-Fi, breakfast time, business center
- You need a taxi to a conference center at 8 AM tomorrow

**Situation 2B: Hotel Receptionist**
A guest is checking in. You need:
- Passport and credit card
- Give room 308 (3rd floor)
- Breakfast: 6:30-10 AM (included)
- Wi-Fi password: Business2024
- Business center: 2nd floor
- You can arrange a taxi

---

**Situation 3A: Passenger (Problem)**
You're at the airport. Your flight is delayed by 3 hours! You have an important meeting. Ask the agent:
- Why is it delayed?
- Is there an earlier flight?
- Can you get compensation?
- Where can you wait?

**Situation 3B: Airline Agent (Problem)**
The flight is delayed 3 hours (bad weather). The passenger is upset. You can:
- Explain the delay (safety reasons)
- Check for other flights (only one flight available – in 5 hours)
- Offer meal vouchers ($15)
- Direct to business lounge (gate B area)

---

**Situation 4A: Hotel Guest (Problem)**
You're in room 205. Problems:
- Air conditioning not working (very hot!)
- No towels in bathroom
- TV remote doesn't work
- You need these fixed quickly – you have a conference call in 1 hour

**Situation 4B: Hotel Receptionist (Problem)**
A guest from room 205 has problems. You can:
- Send maintenance for AC (will come in 20 minutes)
- Send housekeeping with towels (immediately)
- Bring new remote (5 minutes)
- Apologize and offer: free breakfast tomorrow

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair: do two different situations. One airport, one hotel. Each person plays both roles in different situations. You have 12 minutes. Go!"

*Monitor and note good language and errors.*

*After 12 minutes:*

"Great work! Let's see one difficult situation. Can a pair show us a problem situation?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Excellent! How did they handle the problem politely?"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic role-plays! I heard great language. Yuki said 'May I see your passport?' – very professional! Juan said 'The air conditioning isn't working' – clear and direct!

Two important points:

1. At check-in (airport or hotel), they will ALWAYS ask for your passport. Have it ready!

2. When there's a problem, be polite but clear: 'Excuse me, there's a problem with my room. The air conditioning isn't working.' Don't just say 'It's broken!' – say exactly what the problem is.

Think: What will you do differently on your next business trip? Discuss for 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Great! Remember: prepare your documents, ask questions clearly, and report problems politely. You'll have less stress when you travel!"
