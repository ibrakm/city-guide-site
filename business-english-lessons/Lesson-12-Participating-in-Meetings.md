# Lesson 12: Participating in Meetings (Opinions, Agreeing/Disagreeing)

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Expressing opinions, agreeing/disagreeing politely, participating actively in meetings

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning how to participate actively in business meetings. This means giving your opinion, agreeing or disagreeing with others, and making suggestions. Let's start!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. How often do you attend meetings in English?
2. Do you find it easy or difficult to speak up in meetings? Why?
3. What's the difference between meetings in your language and meetings in English?
4. Have you ever disagreed with someone in a meeting? How did you express it?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Be honest about your experiences!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share! Who finds it difficult to speak up in meetings? Why?"

*Take 3-4 responses. Validate that this is a common challenge.*

**Teacher adds:**
"Many people find this difficult! Today we'll learn how to confidently share your ideas and disagree politely."

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now, I'm writing 'BUSINESS MEETINGS' on the board. Think about meetings you've attended. In pairs, brainstorm: What phrases do people use to give opinions? To agree? To disagree? What meeting vocabulary do you know? 2 minutes!"

*Write "BUSINESS MEETINGS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me your phrases and words!"

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: I think, in my opinion, I agree, I disagree, good point, maybe, however, suggestion, agenda, minutes, chair, vote, decision, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you say when you have an idea?"
- "How do you agree with someone?"
- "What about when you don't agree?"

---

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now I'm going to read a meeting conversation. Five people are discussing where to have their company's annual conference. Listen carefully to how they express opinions and agree or disagree. I'll read it twice."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Meeting: Choosing a Conference Location**

**Chair (Anna):** Good morning, everyone. Thank you for coming. Today we need to decide where to hold our annual conference in September. We have three options: Singapore, Bangkok, or Hong Kong. Let's hear everyone's thoughts. David, what do you think?

**David:** Thanks, Anna. In my opinion, Singapore would be the best choice. It has excellent conference facilities and easy transportation. Most of our clients are in Asia, so it's convenient for them.

**Maria:** I see your point, David, but I'm not sure I agree. Singapore is quite expensive. Bangkok would be more cost-effective, and the hotels there are also very good.

**Chen:** That's a good point, Maria. However, I think we should also consider Hong Kong. It's somewhere in the middle – not as expensive as Singapore, but with better international connections than Bangkok.

**David:** You're right about the international connections, Chen. But doesn't Hong Kong have limited conference space? I heard it's difficult to book venues there in September.

**Chen:** Actually, I checked, and there are several venues available. It might be worth investigating further.

**Lisa:** If I could add something – I completely agree with Chen about Hong Kong. I attended a conference there last year, and the facilities were excellent. Plus, it's a very convenient location for our European partners too.

**Maria:** That's true, Lisa. I hadn't thought about our European partners. Maybe Hong Kong is a better option than I initially thought.

**Anna:** Good discussion! So it seems like Hong Kong might be our preferred option. Let me suggest this: Chen, could you research Hong Kong venues in more detail and send us a report by Friday? Then we can make a final decision next week.

**All:** Agreed!

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What are the three location options?
2. What reasons does David give for choosing Singapore?
3. Why does Maria prefer Bangkok?
4. What advantage does Chen mention for Hong Kong?
5. Who makes the final suggestion? What is it?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. Who agreed with whom? Who disagreed?
2. Were the disagreements polite or rude? How do you know?
3. Did anyone change their opinion? Who?
4. What makes this a good meeting?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great analysis! Now let's learn key phrases for participating in meetings – giving opinions, agreeing, and disagreeing politely."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**GIVING OPINIONS:**

**1. Stating Your Opinion:**
- I think (that)...
- In my opinion,...
- I believe (that)...
- From my perspective,...
- I feel that...
- It seems to me that...

**Examples:**
- "I think Singapore is the best option."
- "In my opinion, we should wait."

**2. Making Suggestions:**
- I suggest that we...
- How about...?
- What if we...?
- Perhaps we could...
- Why don't we...?
- I'd like to propose...

**Examples:**
- "I suggest that we postpone the decision."
- "How about meeting again next week?"

**AGREEING:**

**3. Strong Agreement:**
- I completely agree (with [person]).
- Exactly!
- That's a good/excellent point.
- I think you're absolutely right.
- I couldn't agree more.

**4. Partial Agreement:**
- I agree to some extent, but...
- That's true, but...
- You have a point, however...
- I see what you mean, but...

**Examples:**
- "That's true, but we also need to consider the cost."
- "I agree to some extent, but there are other factors."

**DISAGREEING (POLITELY!):**

**5. Soft Disagreement:**
- I'm not sure I agree...
- I see your point, but...
- I understand what you're saying, but...
- I'm afraid I don't agree...
- I have a different opinion.

**6. Respectful Disagreement:**
- With respect, I think...
- I appreciate your point, but...
- That's one way to look at it, but...
- Actually, I think...

**Examples:**
- "I see your point, but I think Bangkok would be better."
- "With respect, I think we need more information."

**IMPORTANT NOTE:**

**Teacher Script:**
"NEVER say just 'I disagree' or 'You're wrong' in a meeting! This is rude!

ALWAYS soften your disagreement:
- ❌ 'I disagree.'
- ✅ 'I see your point, but I'm not sure I agree.'

And ALWAYS give a reason:
- 'I'm not sure I agree because [reason].'
- 'I have a different opinion. I think [reason].'"

**OTHER USEFUL MEETING PHRASES:**

**7. Asking for Opinions:**
- What do you think, [name]?
- Does anyone else have thoughts on this?
- [Name], would you like to add anything?

**8. Managing the Discussion:**
- Let's hear from everyone.
- We're running out of time. Let's decide.
- Can we come back to this point later?
- Let's move on to the next topic.

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll read Person A, B, and C. You all repeat after me."

**Person A:** I think we should hire more staff for the project.  
**Person B:** I see your point, but I'm not sure we have the budget.  
**Person C:** Actually, I agree with A. We need more people to finish on time.  
**Person A:** Exactly! If we don't hire more staff, we'll miss the deadline.  
**Person B:** That's a good point. Perhaps we could hire temporary staff instead of permanent employees?  
**Person C:** That's an excellent idea! That would save money.  
**Person A:** I completely agree. Let's do that.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now, in groups of 3-4, practice this dialogue, then create your own. Choose a topic like: 'Should we work from home or return to the office?' Have a meeting discussion!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent discussions! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Polite or Too Direct?**

**Instructions:**
Mark each sentence P (polite) or D (too direct/rude):

1. ___ "You're wrong."
2. ___ "I'm not sure I agree with that."
3. ___ "That's a stupid idea."
4. ___ "That's a good point, but have we considered other options?"
5. ___ "I disagree completely."
6. ___ "I see what you mean, but I have a different opinion."
7. ___ "No, that won't work."
8. ___ "I understand your concern, but perhaps we could try a different approach."

**Answers:** 1-D, 2-P, 3-D, 4-P, 5-D, 6-P, 7-D, 8-P

**Teacher Script:**
"Check with your partner. Then rewrite the 'too direct' sentences to make them polite!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Sample answers:**
1. "I'm afraid I have a different opinion."
3. "I'm not sure that would work because..."
5. "I see your point, but I don't think I agree."
7. "I'm not sure that will work. What if we..."

### **Exercise 2: Complete the Meeting Conversation**

**Instructions:**
Choose the correct phrase: *I think / I see your point / good point / suggest / I completely agree / not sure I agree*

**Person A:** _______ we should launch the product in March.  
**Person B:** _______, but don't you think April would give us more time?  
**Person A:** That's a _______, but we might lose the market opportunity.  
**Person C:** _______ with Person A. March is better.  
**Person B:** I'm _______ _______. The product isn't ready yet.  
**Person A:** Okay, let me _______ a compromise. How about late March?  
**Person B:** That could work!

**Answers:** I think, I see your point, good point, I completely agree, not sure I agree, suggest

**Teacher Script:**
"Complete the conversation with your partner, then practice it!"

*Give 4 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Meeting Simulation (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Perfect! Now it's time for a real meeting! You'll work in groups of 4. Each group will have a meeting to discuss a business problem and make a decision. Everyone must participate – give your opinion, agree or disagree with others!"

### **Meeting Scenarios:**

**Divide class into 2 groups of 4. Give each group a scenario card:**

**Scenario 1: Choosing a Company Phone System**

**Meeting Topic:** Your company needs a new phone system. You must decide between 3 options:

- **Option A:** Traditional desk phones (reliable, easy to use, but expensive and not flexible)
- **Option B:** Mobile phones for everyone (flexible, modern, but expensive and some staff aren't comfortable with technology)
- **Option C:** Computer-based phone system (Skype/Teams) (cheap, flexible, but requires good internet and training)

**Role Cards for Each Person:**

**Person 1 – IT Manager:**
You prefer Option C (computer-based). It's modern, cheap, and easy to update. You think everyone should learn new technology.

**Person 2 – Finance Manager:**
You prefer Option C (computer-based). It's the cheapest option. The company needs to save money right now.

**Person 3 – Operations Manager:**
You prefer Option A (traditional phones). They're reliable and all staff know how to use them. You're worried about technology problems with other options.

**Person 4 – HR Manager:**
You prefer Option B (mobile phones). Staff want flexibility to work from home. Mobile phones allow this. Cost is worth it for employee satisfaction.

**Your task:** Discuss all opinions. Everyone must speak! Make a decision that everyone can accept.

---

**Scenario 2: Planning a Team-Building Event**

**Meeting Topic:** Your department wants to organize a team-building event for 30 employees. You must decide:

- **Option A:** Weekend outdoor activity (hiking, camping) – fun, healthy, team bonding, but some people might not like outdoor activities
- **Option B:** Cooking class and dinner – everyone can participate, relaxed, but might be boring for some
- **Option C:** Escape room challenge and drinks – fun, modern, good for teamwork, but limited space (need 3 groups)

**Role Cards for Each Person:**

**Person 1 – Team Leader:**
You prefer Option A (outdoor). You think physical activity is good for health and team spirit. Office workers sit too much!

**Person 2 – Organizer:**
You prefer Option B (cooking class). It's easy to organize, everyone can participate (including people with health issues), safe and enjoyable.

**Person 3 – Young Employee:**
You prefer Option C (escape room). It's modern and exciting. Cooking class sounds boring. You think young staff want something different.

**Person 4 – Senior Employee:**
You're worried about Option A (outdoor) because not everyone is physically fit. You could support Option B or C, but want something inclusive for all ages.

**Your task:** Discuss all opinions. Make sure everyone participates! Decide together.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each group: sit together like you're in a meeting. Choose one person to be the meeting chair (they guide the discussion and help people take turns speaking).

Everyone else: Give your opinion! Agree or disagree with others! Use the phrases we learned!

You have 10 minutes to discuss and make a decision. I'll be listening!"

*Groups have their meetings. Monitor and take notes on good language use and errors.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Stop! What did you decide? Group 1, what phone system did you choose? Why?"

*Each group reports their decision.*

"Great! Did anyone change their opinion during the meeting? Why?"

*Discuss briefly.*

"Let's listen to good phrases I heard. I heard someone say 'I see your point, but...' – excellent! I heard 'That's a good point' – perfect!"

*Share 3-4 good examples you heard.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic meetings! You all participated actively and used great phrases!

Let's remember the FIVE KEY RULES for participating in meetings:

1. **Always give reasons** – Don't just say 'I think Option A.' Say 'I think Option A because...'

2. **Be polite when disagreeing** – Never say 'You're wrong.' Say 'I see your point, but...' or 'I'm not sure I agree because...'

3. **Listen actively** – Show you heard others: 'That's a good point' / 'I understand what you're saying'

4. **Build on others' ideas** – 'I agree with Maria, and I'd like to add...'

5. **Participate!** – Meetings need everyone's ideas. Don't be silent!

Think: What will you do differently in your next meeting in English? 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: Your opinion is valuable! Don't be afraid to speak up – just remember to be polite and give reasons!"

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

### Teacher Script:
"For homework, choose one:

**Option 1 (Writing):** Write a meeting dialogue (150-200 words) with 4 people. The topic: 'Should the company allow employees to work from home 2 days per week?' Include:
- At least 2 people giving opinions
- At least 2 examples of agreement
- At least 2 examples of polite disagreement
- A final decision

**Option 2 (Speaking):** Record yourself (2-3 minutes) giving your opinion on a business topic. Choose one:
- Should companies ban smartphones in meetings?
- Is it better to work in an office or from home?
- Should companies give longer holidays to employees?
Give your opinion, give reasons, and also mention one opposing viewpoint (and why you still disagree).

**Option 3 (Real Practice):** In your next real meeting (in any language), notice how people give opinions, agree, and disagree. What phrases do they use? Are they direct or indirect? Bring notes to class.

Questions? See you next lesson!"
