# Lesson 8: Shopping & Negotiating Prices

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Shopping for business, negotiating prices, making purchases, discussing discounts

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Hello everyone! Today we're learning about shopping and negotiating prices in business contexts. This is very useful when you buy products for your company or when you sell to customers!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Do you ever buy things for your company? What kinds of things?
2. Do you negotiate prices in your job? When?
3. In your country, is it common to negotiate prices? In what situations?
4. What's the difference between shopping for yourself and shopping for your company?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. You have 4 minutes. Think about your work experience."

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear from you! Who negotiates prices in their job? What do you negotiate?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now, I'm writing 'BUSINESS SHOPPING & NEGOTIATION' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm: What do you say when you buy things for your company? What words relate to prices and negotiation? 2 minutes!"

*Write "BUSINESS SHOPPING & NEGOTIATION" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me your words and phrases."

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: price, discount, bulk order, quantity, cheaper, wholesale, retail, quote, offer, deal, negotiate, supplier, terms, delivery, payment, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you ask when you want a lower price?"
- "What about buying large quantities?"
- "What payment words do you know?"

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read about a purchasing manager buying products. Read both emails carefully."

### **Reading Text:**

**Email 1: Requesting a Quote**

From: michael.brown@officeworld.com  
To: sales@techsupplies.com  
Subject: Quote Request for Office Equipment

Dear Sir/Madam,

I'm writing to request a quote for office equipment for our company. We are looking to purchase the following items:

- 20 office chairs (model: ErgoComfort 3000)
- 10 standing desks (adjustable height)
- 5 laser printers (color, multi-function)

Could you please provide:
1. Your best price for these items
2. Delivery time to our warehouse in Manchester
3. Payment terms
4. Any discounts for bulk orders

We are planning to make this purchase within the next two weeks. If your prices are competitive, we would be interested in becoming a regular customer.

I look forward to hearing from you soon.

Best regards,  
Michael Brown  
Purchasing Manager  
OfficeWorld Ltd.

---

**Email 2: Negotiating a Better Price**

From: michael.brown@officeworld.com  
To: sales@techsupplies.com  
Subject: RE: Quote for Office Equipment

Dear Ms. Johnson,

Thank you for your quote. Your prices are reasonable, and we're very interested in your products.

However, I've received quotes from two other suppliers, and their prices are slightly lower. Your quote for the office chairs is £150 each, but another supplier quoted £135.

We prefer to work with you because of your excellent reputation. Would it be possible to offer a better price? We're ordering 20 chairs, 10 desks, and 5 printers – a total value of approximately £8,000.

If you could offer a 10% discount on the total order, we would place the order immediately. We would also be interested in a long-term partnership, with monthly orders of similar size.

Could you let me know if this is possible?

Best regards,  
Michael Brown

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What products does Michael want to buy?
2. What information does he request in the first email?
3. In the second email, what problem does Michael mention?
4. What does Michael offer to do if they get a discount?
5. How much discount does Michael ask for?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. Is Michael's negotiation strategy good? Why?
2. What reasons does he give for wanting a discount?
3. Would you negotiate the same way? What would you say?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's learn key language for shopping and negotiating in business."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**REQUESTING QUOTES/INFORMATION:**

**1. Asking for Prices:**
- "Could you give me a quote for [products]?"
- "What's your price for [product]?"
- "How much do you charge for [product/service]?"
- "Could you send me your price list?"

**Examples:**
- "Could you give me a quote for 50 laptops?"
- "What's your price for bulk orders?"

**2. Asking for Details:**
- "What are your payment terms?"
- "What's the delivery time?"
- "Do you offer discounts for large orders?"
- "Is shipping included in the price?"

**NEGOTIATING:**

**3. Mentioning Competitor Prices:**
- "I've received a quote from another supplier."
- "Another company quoted [price]."
- "Your price is higher than [competitor]."
- "We found a cheaper price elsewhere."

**4. Asking for Discounts:**
- "Would it be possible to offer a discount?"
- "Could you give us a better price?"
- "Is there any flexibility on the price?"
- "What discount can you offer for bulk orders?"

**5. Making Offers/Conditions:**
- "If you can offer [discount], we'll place the order immediately."
- "We're willing to pay [price] if you can deliver by [date]."
- "We'd be interested in a long-term partnership if the price is right."

**6. Showing Interest (but not committing):**
- "Your prices are reasonable."
- "We're very interested in your products."
- "Your quote is competitive."
- "We're considering several suppliers."

**ACCEPTING/DECLINING:**

**7. Accepting an Offer:**
- "That sounds good. We'll take it."
- "Yes, that price works for us."
- "We accept your offer."
- "Please go ahead and process the order."

**8. Declining Politely:**
- "I'm afraid that's still too expensive for us."
- "Unfortunately, we can't accept that price."
- "We'll need to think about it."
- "We'll keep your quote and contact you if needed."

### **Useful Vocabulary:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Important words:
- **Quote**: the price a supplier gives you (noun and verb)
- **Bulk order**: large quantity
- **Competitive**: price is good compared to others
- **Terms**: conditions (payment terms, delivery terms)"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice a negotiation. I'll read the Buyer, you all read the Seller first."

**Buyer:** Hello, I'm interested in ordering 100 units of your Model X. What's your best price?  
**Seller:** For 100 units, the price is $50 per unit.  
**Buyer:** I see. Another supplier quoted $45 per unit. Could you offer a better price?  
**Seller:** Well, for orders over 100 units, we can offer $47 per unit.  
**Buyer:** What if I order 150 units? Could you do $45?  
**Seller:** For 150 units, yes, we can do $45 per unit.  
**Buyer:** Great! And what are your payment terms?  
**Seller:** 50% deposit, 50% on delivery.  
**Buyer:** Perfect. We accept. Please send me the order form.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs. Then create your own negotiation for different products!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

### Teacher Script:

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Perfect! Now for realistic negotiation practice! Work in pairs. You'll negotiate as buyer and supplier. I'll give you role cards with your targets. Try to get the best deal!"

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Buyer (Office Manager)**
You need to buy:
- 30 office desks
- 30 office chairs
- 10 filing cabinets

Your budget: Maximum $15,000 total
You've received another quote: $14,500 for everything
You need delivery within 2 weeks
You want 30-day payment terms (pay after delivery)
Try to negotiate the best price!

**Situation 1B: Seller (Furniture Supplier)**
Your standard prices:
- Office desk: $400 each = $12,000 (30 desks)
- Office chair: $150 each = $4,500 (30 chairs)
- Filing cabinet: $200 each = $2,000 (10 cabinets)
- Total: $18,500

You can offer:
- 5% discount for orders over $15,000
- 10% discount for orders over $20,000
- Delivery: 3 weeks (or 2 weeks with $500 extra charge)
- Payment: 50% deposit normally, but flexible for good customers

---

**Situation 2A: Buyer (Purchasing Manager)**
You want to buy:
- 100 laptop computers (for your company)

Your information:
- Your budget: $700 per laptop maximum
- You've had quotes: $750 from Supplier A, $720 from Supplier B
- You need them in 3 weeks
- You want a warranty (guarantee) for 2 years
- This is a test order – if satisfied, you'll order 200 more next quarter

**Situation 2B: Seller (Computer Supplier)**
Your product:
- Standard price: $800 per laptop
- Good quality, 1-year warranty included
- 2-year warranty: add $50 per laptop

You can offer:
- 50-99 laptops: 5% discount
- 100+ laptops: 10% discount
- You really want this customer! (They might buy more)
- You can offer extra discount for guaranteed future orders
- Delivery: 4 weeks (or 3 weeks + $500 rush fee)

---

**Situation 3A: Buyer (Restaurant Owner)**
You need:
- Fresh vegetables delivered weekly for 1 year
- Estimated: $2,000 per month = $24,000 per year

Your situation:
- Your current supplier charges $2,000/month
- You're unhappy with current supplier (quality problems)
- You want good quality and reliable delivery
- You can pay monthly (prefer not to pay in advance)
- Willing to sign 1-year contract for good price

**Situation 3B: Seller (Vegetable Supplier)**
Your offer:
- Standard price: $2,200 per month
- Your vegetables are top quality (better than competitors)
- You deliver 3 times per week

You can offer:
- 6-month contract: 5% discount
- 1-year contract: 10% discount
- Payment in advance (3 months): extra 5% discount
- You need contracts (guaranteed business)
- You can be flexible on payment terms for the right price

---

**Situation 4A: Buyer (Hotel Manager)**
You need to buy:
- 50 new TVs for hotel rooms
- 50 mini fridges

Your budget: $500 per room = $25,000 total
Another supplier quoted: $28,000 but poor quality
You need installation included
Delivery needed: within 1 month
You might renovate another hotel next year (100 rooms)

**Situation 4B: Seller (Hotel Equipment Supplier)**
Your prices:
- TV (50-inch): $400 each = $20,000
- Mini fridge: $250 each = $12,500
- Total: $32,500
- Installation: $2,000 extra

You can offer:
- Orders over $30,000: free installation
- 10% discount for orders over $40,000
- If they commit to future order, you can give better price now
- Delivery: 2 weeks included

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair: take your role cards. Read your information carefully. Think about: What's your goal? What can you offer? What's your limit? Then negotiate! Try to reach an agreement that works for both sides. You have 12 minutes!"

*Monitor. Listen for negotiation language and strategies.*

*After 12 minutes:*

"Stop! Did everyone reach an agreement? What was difficult?"

*Discuss briefly.*

"Let's see one negotiation. Pair 3, can you show us your negotiation?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Excellent! What negotiation phrases did you hear? What strategy did they use?"

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic negotiations! I heard great language. Li said 'Would it be possible to offer a discount?' – very polite! Carlos said 'If you can do $45, we'll order immediately' – good strategy!

Three key tips for negotiating:

1. **Do your research** – know competitor prices, know your budget. Michael in the email mentioned another quote – this gives you power!

2. **Be polite but firm** – don't say 'That's too expensive!' Say 'I'm afraid that's outside our budget. Could you offer a better price?'

3. **Offer something in return** – 'If you can give 10% discount, we'll order 200 units' or 'If you can deliver in 2 weeks, we'll pay 50% deposit.'

Think: What will you do differently next time you negotiate? Discuss 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: negotiation is normal in business! Don't be afraid to ask for a better price – the worst they can say is no!"
