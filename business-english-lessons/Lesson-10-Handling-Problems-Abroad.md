# Lesson 10: Handling Problems Abroad

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Solving travel problems, reporting issues, asking for help, staying calm in difficult situations

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning how to handle problems when you travel for business. We all know that problems happen – lost luggage, cancelled flights, wrong hotel bookings. Let's talk about it!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Have you ever had a problem while traveling for business? What happened?
2. What kinds of problems can happen when you travel?
3. How do you feel when something goes wrong in a foreign country?
4. What's the most important thing to do when there's a problem?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your stories – we can learn from each other's experiences!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear some problem stories! Don't worry – everyone has them! Who wants to share?"

*Take 3-4 responses. Validate that problems are normal.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good stories! Now, I'm writing 'TRAVEL PROBLEMS' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm all the problems that can happen when you travel for business, and what you need to say. Think about flights, hotels, taxis, meetings, documents. 2 minutes!"

*Write "TRAVEL PROBLEMS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me travel problems and solutions!"

*Collect vocabulary on board. Expected: delayed, cancelled, lost, stolen, broken, overbooked, wrong, missed, late, refund, replacement, emergency, help, problem, complaint, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What problems happen at hotels?"
- "What about luggage?"
- "What if you're late for a meeting?"
- "What do you ask for when there's a problem?"

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read about three businesspeople dealing with problems. Read all three situations."

### **Reading Text:**

**Problem 1: Lost Luggage**

David arrives in Shanghai for an important conference. He goes to baggage claim at the airport, but his suitcase doesn't arrive. All his business clothes and presentation materials are in that suitcase! The conference starts tomorrow morning.

David goes to the airline's baggage service desk. "Excuse me, I have a problem. My luggage hasn't arrived on Flight BA238 from London."

The agent asks for his baggage claim tag and passport. "I'm very sorry, Mr. Johnson. It seems your bag was put on the wrong flight. It will arrive tomorrow evening."

"Tomorrow evening? But I have a conference presentation tomorrow morning! I need my clothes and documents," David explains.

The agent says, "I apologize for the inconvenience. We can offer you $100 for emergency purchases. Please keep your receipts. Your bag will be delivered to your hotel tomorrow evening."

David accepts the offer and immediately goes shopping for emergency clothes and copies his presentation from his email.

---

**Problem 2: Double Booking**

Maria arrives at her hotel in Berlin at 10 PM after a long flight. She's exhausted and just wants to sleep.

"Good evening. I have a reservation under Maria Santos," she says to the receptionist.

The receptionist checks the computer, looking worried. "I'm very sorry, Ms. Santos, but we have a problem. Your booking was for yesterday, not today. And we're completely full tonight – there are no rooms available."

Maria is shocked. "But I confirmed last week! I have the email right here. It clearly says March 15th – today!"

The receptionist looks at the email. "You're absolutely right. This is our mistake. Please wait a moment."

After making several phone calls, the receptionist says, "Ms. Santos, I sincerely apologize. We've found you a room at our sister hotel, three blocks from here. It's a superior room at no extra cost. We'll also refund tonight's payment and arrange a taxi there immediately."

Maria accepts, though she's frustrated. At least they found a solution.

---

**Problem 3: Meeting Delay**

Chen has an important client meeting at 9 AM in downtown Tokyo. Unfortunately, his taxi gets stuck in terrible traffic due to an accident on the highway. It's already 8:50 AM, and they're not moving.

Chen immediately calls his client: "Good morning, Mr. Yamamoto. This is Chen Wei. I'm very sorry, but I'm stuck in traffic. There's been an accident on the highway. I'm going to be about 30 minutes late. I sincerely apologize."

Mr. Yamamoto responds, "Don't worry, Mr. Chen. These things happen. We'll reschedule for 9:30. Take your time and travel safely."

When Chen finally arrives at 9:25, he apologizes again and thanks them for their patience. He's prepared and professional during the meeting, and everything goes well. Mr. Yamamoto appreciates that Chen called immediately to inform them.

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What was David's problem? What solution did the airline offer?
2. What was wrong with Maria's hotel booking? What did the hotel do?
3. Why was Chen late for his meeting? What did he do?
4. Who handled their problem well? Why?
5. What would you do in each situation?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. What's the most important thing these people did when they had problems?
2. How would you feel in these situations?
3. Can you think of better solutions to any of these problems?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great discussion! Now let's learn key language for handling problems professionally and calmly."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**REPORTING A PROBLEM:**

**1. Stating the Problem:**
- "I have a problem."
- "There's a problem with [item/situation]."
- "Something is wrong with [item]."
- "I'm afraid there's been a mistake."
- "[Item] is missing/lost/broken/not working."

**Examples:**
- "There's a problem with my reservation."
- "My luggage is missing."
- "I'm afraid there's been a mistake with my booking."

**2. Explaining the Situation:**
- "I was supposed to [action], but [problem]."
- "My flight was cancelled/delayed."
- "I've lost my [item]."
- "Someone stole my [item]."
- "The [item] isn't working properly."

**Examples:**
- "I was supposed to check in today, but my reservation is missing."
- "Someone stole my wallet."

**ASKING FOR HELP:**

**3. Requesting Assistance:**
- "Could you help me with this?"
- "What should I do?"
- "Can you help me solve this problem?"
- "I need to [action]. Can you help?"

**4. Asking for Solutions:**
- "What can you do about this?"
- "Is there another option?"
- "Can you offer me a replacement/refund?"
- "How soon can this be fixed?"

**EXPRESSING URGENCY:**

**5. Showing It's Important:**
- "This is urgent."
- "I need this as soon as possible."
- "I have an important meeting in [time]."
- "This is very important."

**Examples:**
- "I have an important presentation tomorrow."
- "This is urgent – my flight leaves in two hours."

**STAYING CALM AND POLITE:**

**6. Being Polite Under Pressure:**
- "I understand, but [problem still exists]."
- "I appreciate your help, but [need more]."
- "This is very inconvenient. What can we do?"
- "I need to speak to a manager, please."

**7. Accepting Apologies/Solutions:**
- "Thank you for your help."
- "I appreciate your assistance."
- "Thank you for resolving this quickly."
- "I understand. Thank you for the solution."

**APOLOGIZING FOR YOUR LATENESS/PROBLEM:**

**8. When YOU Cause a Problem:**
- "I'm very sorry, but I'm going to be late."
- "I apologize – I've had an emergency."
- "I'm afraid I won't be able to [action] because [reason]."
- "Please accept my sincere apologies."

**Examples:**
- "I'm very sorry, but I'm stuck in traffic."
- "I apologize – I've had a family emergency."

### **Important Tip:**

**Teacher Script:**
"When you have a problem:
1. **Stay calm** – getting angry doesn't help
2. **Be clear** – explain exactly what's wrong
3. **Be polite** – people want to help polite customers
4. **Suggest solutions** – 'Could you...?' 'Is it possible to...?'
5. **Document everything** – keep emails, receipts, names"

### **Practice Dialogues:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice problem situations. I'll read A, you read B."

**Dialogue 1: Lost Item**
**A:** Excuse me, I have a problem. I left my laptop in a taxi.  
**B:** Oh no! Do you have the taxi receipt?  
**A:** Yes, here it is. What should I do?  
**B:** Let me call the taxi company for you. Please wait.  
**A:** Thank you so much. This is urgent – I have a presentation this afternoon.  
**B:** I understand. I'll do my best to help.

**Dialogue 2: Late for Meeting**
**A:** *Phone call* Hello, this is Sarah Chen. I'm very sorry, but I'm going to be late for our 2 PM meeting.  
**B:** What happened?  
**A:** My earlier meeting ran over, and I'm still 20 minutes away. I apologize for the inconvenience.  
**B:** I understand. Could you be here by 2:30?  
**A:** Yes, definitely. Thank you for your patience.  
**B:** No problem. See you at 2:30.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs. Then create your own problem situations!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great work! Now some exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Match Problems with Solutions**

**Problems:**
1. Your flight is cancelled.
2. Your hotel room has no hot water.
3. You lost your passport.
4. Your credit card was stolen.
5. You're going to be late for a meeting.

**What should you do?**
a) Call the meeting organizer immediately.  
b) Go to your embassy or consulate.  
c) Ask to change rooms or request maintenance.  
d) Call your bank to cancel the card.  
e) Ask the airline for the next available flight.

**Answers:** 1-e, 2-c, 3-b, 4-d, 5-a

**Teacher Script:**
"Match the problems with the best solution. Then discuss with your partner: What would you SAY in each situation? Practice!"

*Give 4 minutes.*

### **Exercise 2: Polite or Too Direct?**

**Teacher Script:**
"Look at these sentences. Which are polite? Which are too direct for a business situation? Mark P (polite) or D (too direct)."

1. ___ "This is terrible! I want my money back!"
2. ___ "I'm afraid there's been a problem with my booking."
3. ___ "Your hotel is terrible!"
4. ___ "Could you help me resolve this issue?"
5. ___ "This is your fault! Fix it now!"
6. ___ "I understand this is difficult, but is there another option?"
7. ___ "Give me a refund immediately!"
8. ___ "I'd appreciate it if you could help me with this."

**Answers:** 1-D, 2-P, 3-D, 4-P, 5-D, 6-P, 7-D, 8-P

**Teacher Script:**
"Discuss with your partner. Then rewrite the 'too direct' sentences to make them polite!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now realistic problem-solving practice! You'll work in pairs. One person has a problem, the other person helps. Switch roles for different situations. Try to stay calm and professional!"

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Business Traveler (Problem)**
You're at the airport. Problems:
- Your flight to London was cancelled (bad weather)
- You have a very important client meeting tomorrow at 9 AM
- The next flight is in 24 hours – too late!
- You MUST get to London today
Ask the airline agent for help. Be polite but show urgency!

**Situation 1B: Airline Agent**
A passenger's flight was cancelled. You can:
- Offer a flight via Paris (different airline, arrives at 11 PM tonight)
- Or full refund
- Or hotel + next flight tomorrow
Try to help, but explain what's possible.

---

**Situation 2A: Hotel Guest (Problem)**
You checked into your hotel room. Problems:
- No Wi-Fi (you need it for work!)
- Air conditioning not working (very hot!)
- Very noisy – there's construction outside
- You need to work tonight – you have a presentation tomorrow
Complain politely and ask for solutions.

**Situation 2B: Hotel Manager**
A guest is complaining about their room. You can offer:
- Different room on higher floor (quieter, working AC)
- Portable Wi-Fi device
- Upgrade to business suite (free)
- 20% discount on their bill
Apologize and find a solution.

---

**Situation 3A: Business Person (Late!)**
You're in a taxi going to an important meeting with a client at their office. You're supposed to be there at 10 AM. Problems:
- Terrible traffic – accident on the road
- It's 9:55 AM and you're still 15 minutes away
- This is a very important client
- You can't reschedule (they're only available today)
Call them and explain. Apologize and suggest solution.

**Situation 3B: Client**
You're waiting for a meeting with a supplier at 10 AM. They're calling you – they're late. You:
- Have another meeting at 11 AM
- Could wait until 10:30 AM maximum
- Are a bit annoyed (this is unprofessional)
- But you understand that problems happen
Listen and decide what to do.

---

**Situation 4A: Conference Attendee (Problem)**
You're at an international conference. Problems:
- You registered and paid, but your name isn't on the list!
- You have a presentation in 1 hour in Room 3B
- You need your conference badge to enter
- You have your confirmation email and receipt
Explain the problem and get this fixed quickly!

**Situation 4B: Conference Registration Desk**
An attendee says they registered but aren't on your list. You:
- Can't find their registration in the system
- But they have a confirmation email (it looks real)
- Can issue a temporary badge
- Can check with your manager
- The presentation is soon – they're stressed!
Help them professionally.

---

**Situation 5A: Business Traveler (Lost Luggage)**
You arrived for a 3-day business trip. Your luggage is lost (the airline lost it). In your luggage:
- All your business clothes
- Product samples for client meetings
- Important documents (you have copies on email)
- You have meetings starting tomorrow
You need compensation and help!

**Situation 5B: Airline Baggage Service**
A passenger's luggage is lost. You can offer:
- $150 for emergency purchases (clothes, toiletries)
- Luggage will arrive in 2 days
- Delivered to hotel for free
- Keep receipts for possible further compensation
Apologize and help them.

---

**Situation 6A: Business Person (Sick!)**
You woke up feeling very sick (bad food yesterday). You have:
- Important client presentation at 2 PM today
- Dinner with client tonight
- You're too sick to attend
- This client is very important (big contract)
Call your client and your colleague. Apologize and reschedule.

**Situation 6B: Client AND Colleague**
Your supplier (partner) calls – they're sick and can't make today's meeting/dinner. You (as client):
- Are disappointed (you flew here to meet them!)
- Could meet tomorrow morning instead
- Or do a video call today

You (as colleague):
- Could do the presentation instead (but you're not as prepared)
- Or help reschedule
Help find a solution!

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair: you'll do 2-3 different situations in 12 minutes. Take your role cards. Remember:
- The person with the problem: be polite but clear!
- The person helping: be professional and offer solutions!
- Both: stay calm!

Go!"

*Monitor. Listen for problem-solving language and professionalism.*

*After 12 minutes:*

"Great problem-solving! These situations were difficult, but you handled them well! Let's see one. Pair 5, can you show us a difficult situation?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Excellent! What language did they use? How did they stay professional?"

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic work handling difficult problems! I heard great language. Sofia said 'I'm afraid there's been a mistake' – very polite! Marco said 'This is urgent – I have a meeting in one hour' – clear and firm but professional!

Five KEY TIPS for handling problems abroad:

1. **Stay calm** – panic makes everything worse
2. **Be polite** – angry customers don't get good help
3. **Be clear** – explain exactly what's wrong and what you need
4. **Suggest solutions** – don't just complain! Say 'Could you...?'
5. **Document everything** – keep emails, receipts, names, times

Think about this: What will you do differently next time you have a travel problem? Discuss 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: problems happen to everyone! The important thing is HOW you handle them. Professional, calm, polite = best results!"
