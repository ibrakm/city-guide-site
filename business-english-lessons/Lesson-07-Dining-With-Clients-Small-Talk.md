# Lesson 7: Dining with Clients & Small Talk

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Restaurant language, making small talk, socializing with business contacts

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning about dining with clients and making small talk. This is a very important business skill! Let's start."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Have you ever had a business lunch or dinner? With who?
2. What topics do you talk about during a business meal?
3. What topics should you NOT talk about in business situations?
4. Is dining with clients common in your country? What's the culture?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Think about your experiences and your country's business culture."

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share! Who has experience with business dinners? What did you talk about?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

**Teacher adds:**
"Good answers! Business meals are important because you build relationships, not just do business. Today we'll learn how!"

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now, I'm writing 'BUSINESS MEALS & SMALL TALK' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm: What do you say at a restaurant? What small talk topics are safe? What restaurant vocabulary do you know? 2 minutes!"

*Write "BUSINESS MEALS & SMALL TALK" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Let's hear your ideas. Give me restaurant phrases first."

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: menu, order, recommend, starter, main course, dessert, bill, tip, reservation, table for..., waiter, etc.*

"Good! Now, what topics can you talk about with clients?"

*Expected: weather, travel, hobbies, sports, food, weekend plans, family (general), local area, etc.*

**Teacher adds prompts:**
- "What about politics or religion?" (Generally avoid!)
- "What about the weather?" (Very safe!)
- "What about sports?" (Usually safe!)

---

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's listen to a business dinner conversation. I'll read it twice. Listen carefully to what they talk about and how they order food."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Business Dinner at an Italian Restaurant**

*Sarah (host) is dining with Mr. Tanaka (client from Japan) at an Italian restaurant in London.*

**Sarah:** Thank you for meeting me this evening, Mr. Tanaka. How was your flight to London?

**Mr. Tanaka:** It was very comfortable, thank you. And please, call me Kenji.

**Sarah:** Wonderful, Kenji. And please call me Sarah. Have you been to London before?

**Kenji:** Yes, twice. But this is my first time in this part of the city. It's very nice.

**Sarah:** I'm glad you like it. So, shall we look at the menu?

**Waiter:** Good evening. Can I get you something to drink first?

**Sarah:** Kenji, what would you like? Wine? Water?

**Kenji:** Just mineral water, please.

**Sarah:** Two mineral waters, please. And could we see the menu?

**Waiter:** Of course. I'll be back in a moment to take your order.

**Sarah:** The pasta here is excellent. I can recommend the seafood linguine. Or do you prefer meat dishes?

**Kenji:** I love seafood! The linguine sounds perfect. What are you having?

**Sarah:** I think I'll have the chicken risotto. So, Kenji, how long are you staying in London?

**Kenji:** Until Friday. I have meetings all week. On Saturday I'm going to visit Oxford before I fly home.

**Sarah:** Oh, Oxford is beautiful! Have you ever been there before?

**Kenji:** No, this will be my first time. I'm really looking forward to it.

**Sarah:** You'll love it! The university buildings are amazing. Do you enjoy visiting historical places?

**Kenji:** Yes, very much. In my free time, I like to visit museums and old buildings. Do you have any hobbies?

**Sarah:** I enjoy photography, especially when I travel. And I play tennis on weekends.

**Kenji:** That's interesting! I play golf. It's a good way to relax after work.

*The waiter returns.*

**Waiter:** Are you ready to order?

**Sarah:** Yes, I'll have the chicken risotto, please.

**Kenji:** And I'll have the seafood linguine.

**Waiter:** Excellent choices. Would you like any starters?

**Sarah:** Kenji, would you like to share a starter?

**Kenji:** Sure, that sounds nice.

**Sarah:** Let's have the mixed salad, please.

**Waiter:** Very good. Your food will be ready shortly.

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, answer these questions with your partner:"

1. Where are Sarah and Kenji having dinner?
2. What small talk topics do they discuss?
3. What does Sarah recommend?
4. What do they order?
5. What are their hobbies?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss these questions:"
1. Was this a good business dinner conversation? Why?
2. Did they only talk about business?
3. What makes Kenji feel comfortable?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's learn key language for dining with clients and making small talk."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**AT THE RESTAURANT:**

**1. Making Recommendations:**
- "I can recommend the [dish]."
- "The [dish] here is excellent/very good."
- "Have you tried [dish]?"
- "I usually have the [dish]."

**Examples:**
- "I can recommend the pasta."
- "The steak here is excellent."

**2. Ordering:**
- "I'll have the [dish], please."
- "Could I have the [dish]?"
- "What would you like?"
- "Would you like a starter/dessert?"

**3. Offering:**
- "What would you like to drink?"
- "Would you like to share a starter?"
- "Can I get you anything else?"

**SMALL TALK TOPICS:**

**4. Safe Topics (Good for business):**
- Travel: "How was your flight?" / "Have you been to [place] before?"
- Work/Schedule: "How long are you staying?" / "Do you have a busy week?"
- Hobbies: "What do you do in your free time?" / "Do you have any hobbies?"
- Food: "Do you like Italian food?" / "What kind of food do you prefer?"
- Weather: "The weather is nice today, isn't it?"
- Sports: "Do you follow any sports?"
- Weekend plans: "Do you have any plans for the weekend?"

**5. Starting Small Talk:**
- "How was your [journey/flight/morning]?"
- "Is this your first time in [city]?"
- "Have you had a chance to see [local attraction]?"

**6. Showing Interest:**
- "That sounds interesting!"
- "Really? Tell me more."
- "I'd love to visit there someday."
- "How interesting!"

**7. Topics to AVOID:**
- Religion
- Politics
- Personal finances/salary
- Very personal family problems
- Age
- Controversial topics

### **Grammar Note:**

**Teacher Script:**
"When we make recommendations, we use:
- 'I can recommend' + noun: 'I can recommend the fish.'
- 'The [food] here is excellent' – this is a safe way to suggest something.
- Questions are polite: 'Would you like...?' is more polite than 'Do you want...?'"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll read Person A, you read Person B."

**Person A:** Thank you for joining me for lunch, David. How was your morning?  
**Person B:** It was very productive, thank you. I had a good meeting with the sales team.  
**Person A:** Excellent! So, shall we look at the menu?  
**Person B:** Yes, good idea. What do you recommend?  
**Person A:** The sandwiches here are very good. I usually have the chicken sandwich.  
**Person B:** That sounds perfect. I'll have that too. So, how long have you been with this company?  
**Person A:** About five years. I really enjoy it. What about you?  
**Person B:** I've been in my current position for three years. Do you have any hobbies outside of work?  
**Person A:** Yes, I enjoy cycling on weekends. What about you?

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs, then create your own restaurant conversation. Choose different food and different small talk topics!"

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now realistic practice! You'll role-play business dinners. Work in pairs. You'll do this at a restaurant table, so arrange your chairs like you're dining together!"

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Host**
You're having dinner with a new supplier from Germany (your partner). You're at a French restaurant. You want to:
- Make them feel comfortable
- Make small talk (travel, hobbies, plans)
- Recommend food (the steak and fish are excellent)
- Build a good relationship
You enjoy running and traveling.

**Situation 1B: Guest (Supplier from Germany)**
You're having dinner with a potential client (your partner) in France. You're a bit nervous. You:
- Are visiting for 3 days
- Love French food, especially cheese
- Enjoy photography as a hobby
- Plan to visit the Eiffel Tower tomorrow
Be polite and friendly!

---

**Situation 2A: Host**
You're having lunch with an important client from Brazil (your partner). You're at an Italian restaurant. You want to:
- Thank them for meeting you
- Discuss their trip and stay
- Make small talk about sports, food, culture
- Recommend the pasta dishes
You love football/soccer.

**Situation 2B: Guest (Client from Brazil)**
You're having lunch with your supplier (your partner) in Italy. You:
- Had a long flight (12 hours) but it was okay
- Are staying for 5 days
- Love Italian food, especially pizza
- Play football/soccer on weekends
- Are interested in Italian culture and history

---

**Situation 3A: Host**
You're having dinner with a partner from South Korea (your partner). You're at a steakhouse. You want to:
- Make them comfortable (it's their first visit to your city)
- Recommend the restaurant's specialties
- Talk about their hobbies and interests
- Suggest places they should visit in your city
You enjoy cooking and hiking.

**Situation 3B: Guest (Partner from South Korea)**
You're having dinner with a business contact (your partner). This is your first time in their city. You:
- Are very interested in local culture
- Enjoy hiking and outdoor activities
- Are staying for 1 week
- Want recommendations for places to visit
- Are a little worried about language, but trying your best!

---

**Situation 4A: Host**
You're having lunch with a potential client from India (your partner). You're at a seafood restaurant. You want to:
- Create a friendly atmosphere
- Find common interests
- Recommend dishes (seafood is the specialty)
- Talk about business travel experiences
You travel a lot for work and enjoy it.

**Situation 4B: Guest (Client from India)**
You're having lunch with a supplier (your partner). You:
- Travel internationally often (you've been to 15 countries!)
- Enjoy trying local food in every country
- Like the seafood, but also vegetarian options
- Enjoy reading and yoga
- Are interested in learning about their company culture

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair, take your cards. Arrange your chairs like you're at a restaurant table. Start with greetings, have a conversation, order food (imagine the waiter), and make small talk. Remember: be polite, friendly, and show interest! You have 12 minutes."

*Monitor. Listen for natural conversation flow and use of phrases.*

*After 12 minutes:*

"Excellent dinner conversations! Let's see one. Pair 2, can you show us part of your conversation?"

*One pair demonstrates a section.*

"Great! What small talk topics did you hear? What made the conversation natural and friendly?"

*Discuss.*

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful work! I heard great conversations. Ana said 'How was your flight?' – perfect opener! Tom said 'That sounds interesting!' – showing interest, excellent!

Three important tips for business dining:

1. **Start with safe topics** – travel, weather, food, hobbies. Avoid personal or controversial topics!

2. **Show interest** – ask follow-up questions, say 'That's interesting!' or 'Tell me more.'

3. **Be a good host** – if you invite someone, make recommendations, make sure they're comfortable, and don't talk only about business!

Think: What makes you comfortable when you eat with new people? What can you do to make others comfortable? 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Exactly! Good business relationships are built on personal connections, not just contracts. Remember this!"
