## ⏱️ Standard Lesson Structure (60-75 min)

| Stage | Time | Teacher Role | Student Activity |
|-------|------|--------------|------------------|
| **Warm-up** | 10 min | Facilitate discussion | Discuss warm-up questions in pairs |
| **Vocabulary Brainstorm** | 5-7 min | Elicit & write on board | Brainstorm topic vocabulary |
| **Reading/Listening** | 10-12 min | Read text/Ask questions | Read/listen, answer comprehension Qs |
| **Language Focus** | 15 min | Present & explain | Practice dialogues, note phrases |
| **Guided Practice** | 10 min | Monitor & correct | Complete exercises in pairs |
| **Role-play** | 15 min | Set up & monitor | Perform role-plays (70%+ speaking!) |
| **Wrap-up** | 5 min | Summarize & correct | Reflect on learning |

**Total: 70-76 minutes**

## 🎯 Essential Teacher Phrases for Each Stage

### Warm-up:
- "Let's start with some questions about..."
- "Discuss these questions with your partner. You have 4 minutes."
- "Let's hear from some of you. Who wants to share?"

### Vocabulary Brainstorm:
- "In pairs, you have 2 minutes to brainstorm words related to..."
- "What words or phrases did you think of? Give me one."
- "Good! Any other ideas?"

### Reading/Listening:
- "Now let's read/listen to..."
- "Answer these questions with your partner."
- "Let's check your understanding..."

### Language Focus:
- "Now let's look at some key phrases for..."
- "Notice how we say..."
- "Let's practice this dialogue together first."

### Guided Practice:
- "Work with your partner to complete..."
- "Let's check the answers together. Number 1...?"
- "Good! Now practice saying these with your partner."

### Role-play Setup:
- "Now it's time for realistic practice!"
- "Each pair, take your role cards."
- "You have [X] minutes. Go!"
- "I'll be listening and taking notes."

### Monitoring:
- *Walk around quietly*
- *Note good language use and errors*
- *Help struggling students with whispered hints*
- *Don't interrupt fluency activities!*

### Wrap-up:
- "Great work today! I heard excellent language..."
- "Let me correct a few things I noticed..."
- "Think about this question: How can you use today's language in your work?"
- "For homework, choose one option..."

## ✅ Pre-Lesson Checklist

**24 Hours Before:**
- [ ] Read through entire lesson plan
- [ ] Prepare all role cards (print or write)
- [ ] Prepare handouts (reading texts, exercises)
- [ ] Practice reading listening texts aloud
- [ ] Think about your students' specific needs

**Before Class Starts:**
- [ ] Arrange chairs for pair/group work
- [ ] Write warm-up questions on board
- [ ] Lay out all materials in order
- [ ] Test any technology (if using)
- [ ] Have timer ready
- [ ] Prepare board with lesson structure outline

**During Class:**
- [ ] Stick to time limits (use timer!)
- [ ] Monitor student speaking time (70%+ goal)
- [ ] Take notes on errors during role-plays
- [ ] Note examples of good language use
- [ ] Be flexible if activities run long/short

**After Class:**
- [ ] Reflect: What worked well? What to improve?
- [ ] Note students who need extra support
- [ ] Prepare materials for next lesson

## 🚨 Common Teacher Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**
- Talk too much (Remember: students should speak 70%!)
- Interrupt fluency activities to correct every error
- Skip the warm-up (it activates prior knowledge!)
- Rush through role-plays (this is where real learning happens!)
- Forget to give clear time limits
- Let strong students dominate discussions

✅ **Do:**
- Use pair work extensively
- Monitor and note errors for later correction
- Give clear instructions for every activity
- Demonstrate role-plays before students start
- Praise good language use
- Keep energy high and positive

## 💡 Quick Fixes for Common Problems

**Problem: Activity finishes too quickly**
- Add a "now create your own dialogue/situation" extension
- Ask follow-up questions to pairs
- Have early finishers help others
- Add depth: "Now do it again, but more formally/informally"

**Problem: Activity taking too long**
- Give a 1-minute warning
- Move to next stage (can return if time permits)
- Shorten guided practice section
- Reduce number of role-play scenarios

**Problem: Students not speaking enough**
- Reduce teacher talking time
- Use pair work instead of whole-class questions
- Don't accept one-word answers (ask "Why?" "Can you explain?")
- Make role-plays longer and more complex

**Problem: Mixed abilities**
- Pair stronger with weaker students (sometimes)
- Give differentiated role cards (some more complex)
- Offer multiple homework options
- Provide extra support to struggling students during monitoring

**Problem: Students speaking L1 during activities**
- Set clear English-only expectations
- Make activities challenging and engaging
- Provide sufficient language input before activities
- Monitor closely and gently remind students

**Problem: Low energy/participation**
- Use energizers between activities (stand up, move around)
- Vary activity types (individual→pair→group→whole class)
- Make scenarios humorous or dramatic
- Connect to students' real work experiences

## 📊 Student Speaking Time Tracker

Use this mental checklist during each lesson:

**High Speaking Time Activities (70%+ student talk):**
- ✅ Pair discussions
- ✅ Role-plays
- ✅ Group brainstorming
- ✅ Student presentations
- ✅ Peer teaching/explaining

**Medium Speaking Time (50% student talk):**
- ⚠️ Whole-class discussions (only a few speak at once)
- ⚠️ Question-answer with teacher
- ⚠️ Guided reading aloud

**Low Speaking Time (Less than 30% student talk):**
- ❌ Teacher explaining for extended periods
- ❌ Teacher reading texts to class
- ❌ Individual silent work
- ❌ Teacher-fronted grammar lectures

**Goal:** Maximize ✅ activities, minimize ❌ activities!

## 🎭 Role-play Management Tips

### Before Role-play:
1. **Set up clearly:** Explain the scenario, show example if needed
2. **Distribute role cards:** Give time to read
3. **Check understanding:** "What's your role?" "What's your goal?"
4. **Set time limit:** "You have 5 minutes for this role-play"

### During Role-play:
1. **Monitor without interrupting**
2. **Listen and take notes** (good language + errors)
3. **Help struggling pairs** with whispered hints
4. **Extend if students are engaged** and using language well

### After Role-play:
1. **Get feedback:** "How did it go?" "What was difficult?"
2. **Praise good language:** "I heard Maria say '[phrase]' - excellent!"
3. **Correct key errors:** Pick 2-3 common mistakes to address
4. **Optional: Demo:** Have one strong pair demonstrate for class

## 🌟 Motivation Techniques for Adult Learners

**Connect to Real Work:**
- "Imagine this is a real client..."
- "When might you need this language in your job?"
- "Who has experienced this situation at work?"

**Acknowledge Progress:**
- "Look how much better your [skill] is than Lesson 1!"
- "You used that phrase perfectly!"
- "Your confidence has really grown!"

**Make It Relevant:**
- Use students' company names in examples (if appropriate)
- Invite students to share work experiences
- Adapt scenarios to their industries

**Keep It Positive:**
- "Mistakes are how we learn!"
- "That was a good try. Let's practice it once more."
- "Everyone's improving!"

**Provide Clear Value:**
- "This phrase will help you in client meetings."
- "You can use this email structure immediately at work."
- "This skill is essential for [their job]."

## 📝 Error Correction Strategy

### During Fluency Activities (Role-plays):
- **DON'T** interrupt to correct
- **DO** note errors for later
- **Focus on:** Communication success, not perfect accuracy

### During Accuracy Activities (Guided Practice):
- **DO** correct immediately
- **Use techniques:** 
  - Repeat with question intonation ("He go? Or he goes?")
  - Gesture (show past tense with hand motion)
  - Elicit from other students ("Who can correct this?")

### In Wrap-up:
- **Pick 2-3 common errors** from the lesson
- **Write on board** (without naming who made the error!)
- **Explain and practice** the correct form
- **Move on** (don't dwell on mistakes)

**Remember:** Adult learners need error correction, but in a supportive, non-embarrassing way!

## 🏆 Success Indicators for Each Lesson

**You know the lesson was successful when:**

✅ Students were speaking 70%+ of the time  
✅ All students participated (not just the confident ones)  
✅ Students used the target language in role-plays  
✅ Energy remained high throughout  
✅ Students asked to continue practicing after time was up  
✅ Students connected the language to their real work  
✅ Students laughed, engaged, and enjoyed themselves  
✅ Weaker students showed improvement  
✅ Students can recall key phrases in wrap-up  
✅ Students leave feeling more confident  

**Red flags that need addressing:**

⚠️ Only 2-3 students talking (same students always)  
⚠️ Long periods of silence or L1 use  
⚠️ Students looking confused or bored  
⚠️ Role-plays finishing in 30 seconds (too easy)  
⚠️ Teacher talking more than 30% of time  
⚠️ Students not using target language in activities  
⚠️ Running significantly over/under time

## 📞 Emergency Lesson Plans

**If you finish 10+ minutes early:**
- Extended role-plays with role reversal
- Student presentations about their companies
- "Create your own scenario" activity
- Review quiz game (teams competing)
- Open discussion on the topic
- Extra homework explanation time

**If you're running late:**
- Skip or shorten guided practice
- Reduce number of role-play scenarios
- Assign some exercises as homework
- Do abbreviated wrap-up
- Save one activity for start of next lesson

**If materials are missing:**
- Students can write their own role cards
- Use board instead of handouts
- Teacher reads texts aloud
- Students act out scenarios without written prompts
- Adapt activities to available materials

## 🎯 Homework Assignment Guide

### Types of Homework:

**1. Writing (For students who want writing practice):**
- Emails
- Descriptions
- Reports
- Dialogues

**2. Speaking (For students who want fluency):**
- Record yourself
- Practice with colleague/friend
- Repeat role-plays from class
- Present on a topic

**3. Real-World Application (Most valuable!):**
- Use phrases at work this week
- Write a real business email
- Have a real business conversation
- Bring an example to class

### Homework Principles:
- ✅ Always optional (adults are busy!)
- ✅ Provide 2-3 options (different learning styles)
- ✅ Keep it realistic (15-20 minutes max)
- ✅ Connect to real work when possible
- ✅ Check/discuss in next lesson's warm-up

## 🌍 Cultural Awareness Tips

**Remember:**
- Business customs vary by culture
- Directness levels vary
- Formality expectations differ
- Punctuality norms vary
- Small talk topics vary

**What to do:**
- Discuss cultural differences openly
- Ask students about their business culture
- Explain Western business norms (without imposing)
- Adapt scenarios to students' contexts
- Celebrate diversity in approaches
