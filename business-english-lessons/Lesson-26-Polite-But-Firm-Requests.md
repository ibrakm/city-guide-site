# Lesson 26: Polite but Firm Requests

**Level:** B2 (Upper Intermediate - Advanced)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Being assertive while remaining professional, making non-negotiable requests politely, setting boundaries

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome to Module 4's integration lessons! Today: being polite but firm. Sometimes you need to be assertive – you can't accept 'no' – but you must stay professional. Let's explore this balance!"

**Warm-up Questions:**
1. Have you ever needed to insist on something at work? What happened?
2. Is it difficult for you to be firm with suppliers or colleagues?
3. What's the difference between being firm and being rude?
4. In your culture, how direct can you be in business?

*Discuss 4 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 2-8: [Following standard structure]**

### **Key Language:**

**FIRM BUT POLITE:**

**1. Non-negotiable Requests:**
- "I need this by [date]. This is non-negotiable."
- "I must insist that..."
- "This is essential for..."
- "I'm afraid I can't accept anything later than..."

**2. Setting Clear Boundaries:**
- "I understand your position, but I need..."
- "While I appreciate your concerns, I must..."
- "I respect that, however..."
- "That won't work for us. We need..."

**3. Escalating Politely:**
- "I've requested this twice already..."
- "This is now urgent..."
- "I'll need to speak with your manager if..."
- "I'd prefer to resolve this with you, but if not possible..."

**4. Being Direct Without Being Rude:**
- "To be clear: we need X by Y."
- "Let me be direct about our requirements..."
- "I want to be upfront about what we need..."
- "Let me clarify our expectations..."

**Practice Scenario:**
You ordered fabric. Delivery is now 2 weeks late. Supplier keeps making excuses. You need to be firm.

**Poor:** "This is unacceptable! You're terrible!"
**Good:** "I understand you've had challenges. However, we've now been waiting 3 weeks beyond the agreed deadline. We have customers depending on this delivery. I need a definite delivery date today, or I'll need to source elsewhere and discuss compensation for the delay."

*Role-play practice in pairs – 15 minutes total.*
