# Lesson 11: Writing Professional Emails

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Email structure, formal vs. informal tone, common business email types

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning about writing professional emails in English. How many emails do you write in English every day? Let's discuss!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. How many work emails do you send per day? Per week?
2. What's difficult about writing emails in English?
3. Have you ever sent an email and regretted it? What happened?
4. What's the difference between a work email and a personal email to a friend?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your email experiences!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear from you! What's difficult about business emails in English?"

*Take 3-4 responses. Validate concerns.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now, I'm writing 'BUSINESS EMAILS' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm: What are the parts of an email? What phrases do you use? What kinds of business emails do you write? 2 minutes!"

*Write "BUSINESS EMAILS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me your ideas!"

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: subject line, greeting, opening, closing, signature, Dear, attachment, forward, CC, inquiry, request, complaint, thank you, regards, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you write at the beginning?"
- "What do you write at the end?"
- "What types of emails do you send?"

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's look at three different business emails. Read all three and notice the structure and language."

### **Reading Text:**

**Email 1: Formal Inquiry**

I would appreciate it if you could send this information by the end of this week.

Thank you for your assistance.

Best regards,  
John Park  
Finance Manager  
Global Trading Co.  
Tel: +82-2-555-0123

**Email 2: Semi-Formal Request**

From: sarah.chen@marketing.com

I hope this email finds you well.

I'm writing to ask if we could arrange a meeting next week to discuss the new marketing campaign. I'd like to get your input on the design concepts we've been working on.

Please let me know your availability.

Best regards,  
Sarah Chen  
Marketing Director

**Email 3: Following Up**

From: maria.santos@exports.com  
To: client@international.jp

Dear Mr. Tanaka,

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What is the purpose of each email?
2. Which email is the most formal? Which is least formal? How do you know?
3. What information does each person request or provide?
4. What closing phrases do they use?
5. What's different about the greetings in each email?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. When should you use 'Dear Sir/Madam' vs. 'Hi [Name]'?
2. Why are subject lines important?
3. Which email style do you use most in your job?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great analysis! Now let's learn the structure and key language for professional emails."

### **Email Structure:**

Write on board:

**EMAIL STRUCTURE:**

1. **Subject Line** – Clear and specific
2. **Greeting** – Dear/Hi + name
3. **Opening** – Why you're writing
4. **Body** – Main message (details, requests, information)
5. **Closing** – Action needed or next steps
6. **Sign-off** – Best regards, etc.
7. **Signature** – Name, title, contact info

### **Key Expressions:**

**GREETINGS:**

**Formal:**
- Dear Mr./Ms./Dr. [Last Name],
- Dear Sir/Madam, (when you don't know the name)

**Semi-formal/Informal:**
- Hi [First Name],
- Hello [First Name],

**OPENING LINES:**

**Formal:**
- I am writing to [inquire/request/inform/complain]...
- I am writing with regard to...
- I would like to inquire about...
- Thank you for your email dated [date].

**Semi-formal:**
- I hope this email finds you well.
- Thank you for your email about...
- I'm writing to ask/tell you about...

**REQUESTING:**

**Formal:**
- I would appreciate it if you could...
- Could you please...?
- I would be grateful if you could...

**Semi-formal:**
- Could you...?
- Would you mind...?
- Can you please...?

**PROVIDING INFORMATION:**

- I'm pleased to inform you that...
- I'm attaching [document].
- Please find attached...
- As requested, I'm sending you...

**CLOSING LINES:**

**Formal:**
- I look forward to hearing from you.
- Thank you for your attention to this matter.
- Please do not hesitate to contact me if you need further information.

**Semi-formal:**
- Looking forward to your reply.
- Let me know if you have any questions.
- Thanks for your help!

**SIGN-OFFS:**

**Formal:**
- Yours sincerely, (when you know the name)
- Yours faithfully, (when you don't know the name)
- Best regards,

**Semi-formal:**
- Best regards,
- Kind regards,
- Thanks,
- Best,

### **Important Tips:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember these rules:
1. **Subject line**: Be specific – 'Meeting Request' is better than just 'Meeting'
2. **First email to someone**: Use formal language. Later emails can be less formal.
3. **Keep it brief**: Business people are busy!
4. **One topic per email**: Don't mix different topics
5. **Proofread**: Check spelling and grammar before sending!"

### **Common Mistakes:**

- DON'T write 'Dear Mr. John' – use 'Dear Mr. Smith' or 'Dear John'
- DON'T write 'Hi Sir/Madam' – it's either 'Hi John' or 'Dear Sir/Madam'
- DON'T use casual language in formal emails: 'wanna', 'gonna', 'btw', emojis
- DON'T forget the subject line!

### **Practice:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll give you situations. With your partner, decide: What greeting, opening, and closing would you use?"

**Situations:**
1. First email to a new supplier (you don't know them)
2. Email to your colleague in another office (you know them well)
3. Thank you email to a client after a meeting

*Give 3 minutes. Check answers together.*

**Sample answers:**
1. Greeting: Dear Sir/Madam, Opening: I am writing to inquire about..., Closing: I look forward to hearing from you. / Best regards,
2. Greeting: Hi [Name], Opening: I hope you're well. I'm writing to..., Closing: Let me know! / Thanks,
3. Greeting: Dear Mr./Ms. [Name], Opening: Thank you for meeting with me yesterday..., Closing: I look forward to our continued cooperation. / Best regards,

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Formal or Informal?**

**Instructions:**
Mark each phrase F (formal) or I (informal/semi-formal):

1. ___ Hi there!
2. ___ I am writing to inquire about...
3. ___ Thanks a lot!
4. ___ I would be grateful if you could...
5. ___ Can you send me the file?
6. ___ Please do not hesitate to contact me.
7. ___ Let me know!
8. ___ Yours faithfully,
9. ___ See you soon!
10. ___ I look forward to hearing from you.

**Answers:** 1-I, 2-F, 3-I, 4-F, 5-I, 6-F, 7-I, 8-F, 9-I, 10-F

**Teacher Script:**
"Check with your partner. When would you use formal vs. informal?"

*Give 2 minutes.*

### **Exercise 2: Fix the Mistakes**

**Instructions:**
These emails have mistakes. Find and correct them.

**Email A:**
Subject: (no subject)  
Hi Mr. John,  
I wanna ask about your products. Can u send me info?  
Thx,  
Maria

**Problems:**
- No subject line
- 'Mr. John' should be 'Mr. Smith' or 'Dear John'
- Too informal: 'wanna', 'u', 'Thx'
- Too brief/casual for a first email to a supplier

**Email B:**
Subject: URGENT!!!!!!  
Dear Sir,  
SEND ME THE REPORT NOW. I NEED IT.  
Sarah

**Problems:**
- Too many exclamation marks
- All capitals = shouting
- Too direct/rude: 'SEND ME' → 'Could you please send me'
- No polite closing

**Teacher Script:**
"Work in pairs. Find the mistakes and rewrite these emails properly!"

*Give 4 minutes. Check answers.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Writing Activity (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now you'll write real emails! Work individually first, then share with your partner for feedback. You'll write two emails."

### **Writing Tasks:**

**Teacher distributes task cards:**

**Task 1: Choose ONE (write 70-100 words):**

**A) Request Email:**
You need to order 50 new office chairs for your company. Write an email to a supplier (Office Furniture Plus) requesting:
- A quote for 50 ergonomic office chairs
- Delivery time
- Payment terms
You don't know the person's name.

**B) Thank You Email:**
You had a successful meeting with a client (Mr. David Wilson from ABC Corporation) yesterday. Write an email to thank him and:
- Thank him for the meeting
- Confirm the next steps you discussed
- Attach the document you promised

**C) Inquiry Email:**
You're planning a business trip to London next month. Write an email to a hotel (The Business Hotel London) to ask about:
- Availability for 3 nights (dates: your choice)
- Room price
- Whether breakfast is included
- Business facilities (Wi-Fi, meeting rooms)

---

**Task 2: Choose ONE (write 70-100 words):**

**D) Follow-up Email:**
You sent a quote to a potential client (Ms. Anna Lee) two weeks ago, but she hasn't replied. Write a polite follow-up email to:
- Remind her about the quote
- Ask if she needs any additional information
- Offer to answer any questions

**E) Apology Email:**
You were supposed to attend a meeting yesterday with a colleague (Tom Chen) but you couldn't attend because of an emergency. Write an email to:
- Apologize for missing the meeting
- Explain briefly (family emergency)
- Ask for a summary of what was discussed
- Suggest rescheduling

**F) Confirmation Email:**
A supplier (Ms. Sofia Martinez) is visiting your office next Thursday at 2 PM to present new products. Write an email to confirm:
- The date and time
- The location (your office address)
- Who will attend (you and your manager)
- How long the meeting will take (1 hour)

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Choose TWO different tasks from the list. Write two emails (70-100 words each). Remember:
1. Write a clear subject line
2. Use appropriate greeting
3. Be clear and polite
4. Use proper closing and sign-off
5. Check your spelling!

You have 10 minutes to write. Then you'll share with your partner."

*Students write individually. Monitor and help.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, exchange emails with your partner. Read their emails and check:
- Is the structure correct?
- Is the tone appropriate (formal/semi-formal)?
- Is the message clear?
- Any spelling/grammar mistakes?

Give helpful feedback!"

*Give 4 minutes for peer review.*

**Teacher Script:**
"Who wants to share their email? Let's read one good example."

*Choose 1-2 students to read their emails aloud or project them.*

"Excellent work! I can see you've learned the structure and phrases!"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful emails today! You've all improved your business email writing!

Let's remember the FIVE KEY RULES for professional emails:

1. **Clear subject line** – Tell them what the email is about
2. **Appropriate greeting** – Formal for new contacts, less formal for colleagues
3. **Clear opening** – State why you're writing in the first sentence
4. **Brief and polite body** – Get to the point, but be polite
5. **Professional closing** – 'Best regards' is always safe!

One more important tip: Before sending any email, read it out loud. Does it sound polite? Clear? Professional? If yes, send it!

Think: What will you do differently when you write emails now? Discuss 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: email is often the first impression you make. A well-written email shows professionalism and respect!"
