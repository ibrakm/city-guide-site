# Lesson 24: Explaining Company Policies

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Explaining rules, processes, and instructions clearly in simple English

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome! Today we're learning how to explain company policies and procedures. Whether you're in HR, management, or customer service, you need to explain rules clearly. Let's discuss!"

**Warm-up Questions:**
1. Have you ever had to explain company rules to someone? Was it easy or difficult?
2. Have you ever been confused by unclear rules or procedures?
3. What makes a good policy explanation?
4. What policies are common in most companies?

*Discuss 4 minutes, share responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

*Brainstorm: policy, procedure, regulation, requirement, guideline, mandatory, optional, comply, violate, exception, etc.*

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### **Reading Text:**

**Good vs. Poor Policy Explanations**

**POOR Explanation (Too Complex):**

"Pursuant to company regulations section 4.2.3, all employees must submit expense reimbursement documentation within the prescribed timeframe of fifteen calendar days subsequent to expenditure occurrence, accompanied by appropriate substantiation as delineated in appendix B..."

**Problem:** Too formal, too complex, unclear.

---

**GOOD Explanation (Clear & Simple):**

"Our expense reimbursement policy works like this:

**What:** If you spend your own money for business purposes, we'll reimburse you.

**When:** Submit your claim within 15 days of spending the money.

**How:** Fill out the expense form and attach receipts.

**What happens next:** Finance reviews your claim within 5 business days. If approved, you'll receive payment by direct deposit within 10 days.

**Important:** Without receipts, we cannot reimburse amounts over $25.

Do you have any questions?"

**Why this works:** Simple language, clear structure, numbered steps, addresses consequences.

---

**Example: Return Policy Explanation**

**Poor:** "Items may be returned subject to conditions."

**Good:** "We accept returns within 30 days if:
- The item is unused and in original packaging
- You have your receipt
- The item is not customized or personalized

To return an item:
1. Bring it to our store or mail it to us
2. Include your receipt
3. We'll refund you within 7 business days

Note: Shipping costs are not refundable unless the item was defective."

### **Comprehension:**

1. What makes the good explanations better?
2. What structure do they follow?
3. Why are bullet points helpful?

*Discuss 3 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### **Key Expressions:**

**INTRODUCING POLICIES:**

**1. Opening:**
- "Let me explain our policy on..."
- "Our policy works like this..."
- "Here's how our [policy] works..."
- "The procedure for [X] is..."

**2. Stating Requirements:**
- "You must..."
- "You're required to..."
- "It's mandatory to..."
- "You need to..."
- "We ask that you..."

**3. Stating Options:**
- "You can/may..."
- "You have the option to..."
- "You're welcome to..."
- "It's optional to..."

**4. Stating Prohibitions:**
- "You cannot/may not..."
- "It's not permitted to..."
- "We don't allow..."
- "It's against policy to..."

**EXPLAINING PROCEDURES:**

**5. Sequencing Steps:**
- "First,... Then,... Finally,..."
- "Step 1:... Step 2:... Step 3:..."
- "To begin with,... Next,... After that,..."

**6. Explaining Timeframes:**
- "Within [X] days..."
- "No later than..."
- "By [date/time]..."
- "During business hours..."

**7. Explaining Conditions:**
- "If..., then..."
- "Provided that..."
- "As long as..."
- "Unless..."

**CLARIFYING & CHECKING UNDERSTANDING:**

**8. Ensuring Clarity:**
- "Does that make sense?"
- "Do you have any questions?"
- "Is anything unclear?"
- "Let me give you an example..."

**9. Providing Examples:**
- "For example..."
- "For instance..."
- "Let's say..."
- "In practice, this means..."

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Employee:** Can I work from home?

**Manager:** Let me explain our work-from-home policy. You can work from home up to 2 days per week. Here's how it works:

First, you need to request approval from your manager at least 24 hours in advance.

Second, you must be available during normal business hours (9 AM - 5 PM).

Third, you need to have a reliable internet connection and a quiet workspace.

On work-from-home days, you're required to attend all scheduled meetings via video call and respond to emails within 2 hours.

This policy doesn't apply during busy periods – for example, during our end-of-quarter rush, everyone must be in the office.

Does that answer your question?

*Practice in pairs – 5 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### **Exercise: Simplify These Policies**

Rewrite these in clear, simple English:

1. **Complex:** "Vehicular parking is prohibited in unauthorized zones during operational hours."  
   **Simple:** "Don't park in unauthorized areas during business hours (9 AM - 6 PM)."

2. **Complex:** "Personnel are obligated to adhere to prescribed break duration parameters."  
   **Simple:** "Lunch breaks are 1 hour. Coffee breaks are 15 minutes."

*Students practice with more examples – 5 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Policy Explanations (15 min)**

### **Policy Explanation Scenarios:**

**Students work in pairs. Each explains a policy:**

1. **Vacation Policy:** 15 days per year, must request 2 weeks in advance, max 5 consecutive days, cannot carry over to next year

2. **Dress Code:** Business casual Monday-Thursday, casual Friday, no shorts/sandals, clients visiting = business professional

3. **Meeting Room Booking:** Book via online system, max 2 hours, cancel if not using, clean up after

4. **Company Laptop Policy:** For work use only, no personal software installation, return when leaving company, report problems to IT

*Students prepare, then explain to partner – 12 minutes total.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great policy explanations!

**RULES FOR EXPLAINING POLICIES:**

1. **Use simple language** – Not legal jargon
2. **Break into steps** – Number them
3. **Give examples** – "For instance..."
4. **Check understanding** – "Does that make sense?"
5. **Be friendly** – Policies don't have to sound harsh

Remember: Your job is to help people understand and follow the rules, not to confuse them!"
