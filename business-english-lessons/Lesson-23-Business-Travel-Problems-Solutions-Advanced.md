# Lesson 23: Business Travel Problems & Solutions (Advanced)

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Complex travel problems, negotiating solutions, travel insurance claims, emergency situations

**Note:** This builds on Lesson 10 with more advanced scenarios and language.

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome! Today we're tackling advanced business travel problems. We covered basic issues in Lesson 10. Now we'll handle more complex situations like visa problems, insurance claims, and emergency situations."

**Warm-up Questions:**

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### **Reading Text:**

**Complex Travel Emergencies: Three Case Studies**

**Case 1: Medical Emergency Abroad**

James developed severe food poisoning in Bangkok during a business trip. He needed hospitalization.

**What he did right:**
- Called his travel insurance company immediately
- Went to a hospital on their approved list
- Kept all medical receipts and reports
- Informed his company and rescheduled meetings
- Filed insurance claim within 30 days

**Language he used:**
"I have travel insurance with [company], policy number [X]. I need emergency medical treatment. Can you direct me to an approved facility?"

**Result:** Full reimbursement, $12,000 in medical costs covered.

---

**Case 2: Visa Denial at Border**

Maria arrived in Russia for a conference but was denied entry – her visa had a small error (wrong date).

**What she did:**
- Stayed calm and polite with immigration officers
- Contacted her company's travel department immediately
- Called the conference organizers
- Contacted the Russian embassy
- Rebooked flight and hotel (non-refundable but claimed through insurance)

**Language she used:**
"There appears to be an error on my visa. I have a business conference starting tomorrow. Who can I speak with to resolve this? Is there an emergency visa process?"

**Result:** Missed the conference but resolved visa issue. Insurance covered change fees ($800). Lesson learned: always double-check visa details!

---

**Case 3: Lost Passport with Connecting Flight**

Chen realized his passport was stolen 3 hours before his connecting flight from London to New York.

**What he did:**
- Reported theft to local police immediately (got police report)
- Contacted his embassy (Chinese Embassy in London)
- Got emergency travel document within 2 hours
- Contacted airline, explained situation, changed flight (paid change fee)
- Informed his US-based company
- Filed insurance claim for stolen passport and change fees

**Critical language:**
"My passport has been stolen. I need an emergency travel document. I have a business commitment in New York tomorrow. This is urgent."

**Result:** Got emergency travel document, arrived 1 day late, insurance reimbursed $600 in expenses.

### **Key Lessons:**
1. Always have digital copies of important documents
2. Know your insurance coverage before traveling
3. Save emergency contact numbers
4. Stay calm – problems can be solved
5. Document everything for insurance claims

### **Comprehension Questions:**

1. What did James do immediately when he got sick?
2. Why was Maria denied entry to Russia?
3. How did Chen get a replacement travel document quickly?
4. What should you always do for insurance claims?

*Discuss 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### **Key Expressions:**

**DESCRIBING EMERGENCIES:**

**1. Medical Emergencies:**
- "I need urgent medical attention."
- "I have travel insurance with [company]."
- "Where is the nearest hospital/clinic?"
- "I need to see a doctor immediately."
- "Can you direct me to an English-speaking doctor?"

**2. Document Problems:**
- "My passport has been lost/stolen."
- "There's an error on my visa."
- "I need to report a theft."
- "Where is the [country] embassy/consulate?"
- "I need an emergency travel document."

**3. Stating Urgency:**
- "This is urgent/critical."
- "I have a business meeting/flight in [time]."
- "I need this resolved immediately."
- "Time is very limited."
- "Is there an expedited/emergency process?"

**NEGOTIATING SOLUTIONS:**

**4. With Airlines:**
- "Due to [problem], I need to change my flight."
- "What are my options?"
- "Can you waive the change fee given these circumstances?"
- "Is there a compassionate exemption for emergencies?"
- "I'd like to speak with a supervisor."

**5. With Hotels:**
- "I need to extend/cancel my stay due to [emergency]."
- "What's your policy on emergency cancellations?"
- "Can you accommodate me given the situation?"
- "I'm willing to pay a reasonable fee, but..."

**6. With Insurance:**
- "I need to file a claim for [problem]."
- "My policy number is [X]."
- "What documentation do you need?"
- "What's covered under my policy?"
- "How long does the claims process take?"

**DOCUMENTING FOR INSURANCE:**

**7. What to Say/Do:**
- "Could I have a receipt/report for insurance purposes?"
- "I need documentation of this incident."
- "Can you provide an official statement?"
- "Please include the date, time, and nature of the problem."

**8. Following Up:**
- "I'm following up on my insurance claim, reference number [X]."
- "What's the status of my claim?"
- "What additional information do you need?"
- "When can I expect reimbursement?"

**COMMUNICATING WITH YOUR COMPANY:**

**9. Informing Your Company:**
- "I'm writing to inform you of a travel emergency."
- "Unfortunately, I've encountered a serious problem."
- "I need to postpone/cancel the meeting due to [emergency]."
- "I'm taking all necessary steps to resolve this."
- "I'll keep you updated on the situation."

### **Practice Dialogues:**

**Emergency 1 (Medical):**
**Traveler:** I need urgent medical attention. I have severe abdominal pain. I have travel insurance with Global Insurance, policy number 123456. Can you direct me to the nearest approved hospital?  
**Insurance:** Yes, the nearest approved facility is City Hospital, 10 minutes away. I'm sending you the address now. Show them your insurance card upon arrival.

**Emergency 2 (Lost Passport):**
**Traveler:** My passport was stolen this morning. I have a flight to New York tomorrow. I need an emergency travel document urgently.  
**Embassy:** I understand. We can issue an emergency travel document, but you'll need to come to the embassy with a police report, photos, and proof of citizenship. Can you come this afternoon?

*Practice in pairs – 5 minutes.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### **Exercise 1: What Would You Say?**

**Scenario 1:** You're at the airport. Your flight is in 3 hours but you realize your visa has expired.  
**Your response:** "Excuse me, I've discovered my visa has expired. I have an urgent business meeting. Is there anyone who can help me resolve this? Is there an emergency visa process available?"

**Scenario 2:** You fell and injured your ankle. You need to see a doctor.  
**Your response:** "I've injured my ankle and need medical attention. I have travel insurance with [company]. Where is the nearest hospital or clinic? Do you have English-speaking doctors?"

**Scenario 3:** Your luggage with all your business materials was lost.  
**Your response:** "My luggage has been lost. It contains important business materials for a presentation tomorrow. What are my options? When can I expect it to be found? Can the airline provide compensation for essential items I need to purchase?"

*Students practice similar scenarios in pairs – 4 minutes.*

### **Exercise 2: Insurance Claim Preparation**

"What information/documentation would you need for these insurance claims?"

1. Medical emergency abroad
2. Lost/stolen luggage
3. Missed flight due to emergency
4. Hotel cancellation

*Discuss in pairs – 3 minutes.*

**Answers:**
1. Medical receipts, doctor's reports, diagnosis, policy number
2. Police report, list of items, receipts, baggage claim ticket
3. Original ticket, reason for missing flight, rebooking costs
4. Original booking, cancellation notice, reason, policy terms

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Emergency Scenarios (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now realistic emergency practice! Work in pairs. Handle these complex travel emergencies!"

### **Emergency Scenario Cards:**

**Scenario 1A: Traveler in Crisis**
You're in Dubai for business. You discovered your wallet was pickpocketed – lost credit cards, cash, and ID. Your company meetings start tomorrow. You need to: report theft, get emergency funds, contact your bank, inform your company. Call the hotel concierge (Partner B) for help.

**Scenario 1B: Hotel Concierge**
Help the guest handle this crisis. You can: contact police, recommend emergency money transfer services, help contact their bank, provide information about embassy location.

---

**Scenario 2A: Business Traveler**
You're flying to an important negotiation meeting tomorrow. The airline just cancelled your flight (mechanical problems). Next available flight arrives 1 day late. Your client is expecting you tomorrow. Handle: negotiate with airline for compensation/alternatives, call client to reschedule, contact your boss to inform.

**Scenario 2B: Airline Agent**
The flight is cancelled. You can offer: rebooking on next flight (24 hours later), or routing through another city (arrives 6 hours late with 2 connections), or full refund. Policy: no cash compensation, but can offer hotel voucher and meal vouchers.

---

**Scenario 3A: Traveler Filing Claim**
You had a medical emergency abroad. You paid $8,000 in medical costs (you have receipts). Call your travel insurance (Partner B) to file a claim. You need to: explain what happened, find out what's covered, understand the process, know when you'll be reimbursed.

**Scenario 3B: Insurance Agent**
Review the traveler's claim. You need: policy number, medical receipts, doctor's report, details of treatment. Coverage: up to $50,000 medical. Process: 15-30 days for claims processing. Ask clarifying questions.

---

**Scenario 4A: Traveler at Embassy**
Your passport was stolen. You have a critical business meeting in 2 days in another country. You need emergency travel documents. Explain your situation to the embassy staff (Partner B). You have: police report, photos, copy of old passport, flight details.

**Scenario 4B: Embassy Staff**
You can issue emergency travel documents but need: police report, proof of citizenship, 2 photos, application form, fee ($140). Process takes 3-4 hours if everything is complete. Interview the traveler to verify their identity and urgency.

*Students do 2 scenarios each – 12 minutes.*

"How did you handle the stress? What language was most useful?"

*Debrief – 3 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent emergency management!

**TRAVEL EMERGENCY PROTOCOL:**

1. **Stay calm** – Panic doesn't help
2. **Assess priority** – What needs immediate attention?
3. **Contact right people** – Insurance, embassy, company, airline
4. **Document everything** – Photos, receipts, reports
5. **Follow up** – Especially for insurance claims

**BEFORE YOUR NEXT TRIP:**
- Copy important documents (passport, visa, insurance)
- Save emergency contacts in phone
- Know your insurance coverage
- Register with your embassy (if traveling to risky areas)
- Have backup credit card and cash

Remember: Most problems can be solved. Stay professional and persistent!

What's one thing you'll do to prepare better for travel? 30 seconds."

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

**Option 1:** Create a "Travel Emergency Kit" document: List all important contacts, document copies you need, insurance info, embassy addresses. Make it specific for your next trip.
