# Lesson 22: Trade Fairs & Exhibitions

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Starting conversations at exhibitions, asking questions, exchanging information, following up

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome! Today we're focusing on trade fairs and exhibitions – important events for making business connections and finding new customers or suppliers. Have you attended trade shows before?"

**Warm-up Questions:**
1. Have you ever attended a trade fair or exhibition? Which one?
2. What do people do at trade fairs?
3. What's the purpose of exhibiting at a trade show?
4. What's challenging about approaching strangers at exhibitions?

*Discuss 4 minutes, then share responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"I'm writing 'TRADE FAIRS' on the board. Brainstorm: What vocabulary is specific to trade shows? What do you see, do, and say there? 2 minutes!"

*Collect: booth/stand, exhibitor, visitor, brochure, sample, catalog, demonstration, lead, business card, etc.*

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### **Reading Text:**

**Strategies for Success at Trade Fairs**

**As an Exhibitor (Selling):**

Marco works for an Italian textile company. At the Shanghai Textile Fair, he's exhibiting their new fabric collection. His strategy:

**Before the fair:**
- Prepared product samples and brochures
- Practiced his "elevator pitch" (30-second company introduction)
- Set goals: collect 50 qualified leads, make 3 solid connections

**At the stand:**
- Greeted visitors warmly: "Good morning! Welcome to Italian Textiles. Are you looking for any particular type of fabric?"
- Qualified visitors: "What kind of products do you work with?" "What's your company's focus?"
- Demonstrated products: "Feel this fabric – 100% organic cotton. Notice the quality?"
- Collected contact information: "May I have your business card? I'll send you our complete catalog."
- Scheduled follow-ups: "I'll be in touch next week with pricing information."

**After the fair:**
- Followed up within 48 hours with personalized emails
- Connected on LinkedIn
- Sent requested information promptly

**Result:** 60 leads collected, 5 promising potential customers, 2 orders within a month.

---

**As a Visitor (Buying/Sourcing):**

Sarah is a purchasing manager looking for new suppliers. Her strategy:

**Before the fair:**
- Studied the exhibitor list
- Identified 15 companies to visit
- Prepared questions to ask

**At the exhibition:**
- Approached booths professionally: "Hello, I'm Sarah Chen from Fashion Direct. We import clothing to the UK. Could you tell me about your products?"
- Asked specific questions: "What's your minimum order quantity?" "What are your lead times?" "Do you have certifications?"
- Collected samples and brochures: "Could I take a sample of this fabric?"
- Took notes on business cards: Wrote details about each supplier immediately

**After the fair:**
- Reviewed all materials collected
- Shortlisted 5 suppliers
- Sent follow-up emails to promising contacts

**Result:** Found 2 new suppliers, negotiated better prices than current suppliers.

### **Comprehension Questions:**

1. What did Marco do before the trade fair?
2. How did Marco qualify visitors at his booth?
3. What questions did Sarah ask at booths?
4. What did both Marco and Sarah do after the fair?

*Discuss 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### **Key Expressions:**

**FOR EXHIBITORS (At Your Booth):**

**1. Opening Conversations:**
- "Good morning! Welcome to [company]!"
- "Hi! Are you looking for anything particular?"
- "Hello! Have you seen [product] before?"
- "Feel free to look around. Let me know if you have any questions."

**2. Qualifying Visitors:**
- "What kind of products are you interested in?"
- "What does your company do?"
- "Are you currently using [product type]?"
- "What brings you to the fair today?"

**3. Presenting Products:**
- "Let me show you our latest..."
- "This is our bestselling product."
- "Feel free to touch/try this."
- "Would you like to see a demonstration?"

**4. Gathering Information:**
- "May I have your business card?"
- "What's the best way to reach you?"
- "Are you the decision-maker for [product]?"
- "When are you looking to make a purchase?"

**FOR VISITORS (Approaching Booths):**

**5. Introducing Yourself:**
- "Hello, I'm [name] from [company]."
- "Good afternoon. We're looking for [product]."
- "Hi, I'm interested in learning about your products."

**6. Asking About Products:**
- "Could you tell me about [product]?"
- "What makes your product different?"
- "Do you have a catalog I could look at?"
- "Could I see some samples?"

**7. Asking Business Questions:**
- "What's your minimum order quantity?"
- "What are your lead times?"
- "Do you ship internationally?"
- "What payment terms do you offer?"
- "Do you have any certifications?"

**8. Collecting Materials:**
- "May I take a brochure?"
- "Could I have a sample?"
- "Do you have a price list?"

**FOLLOW-UP:**

**9. Setting Next Steps:**
- "I'll send you our catalog tomorrow."
- "Let me email you the details."
- "Can we schedule a call next week?"
- "I'll be in touch within two days."

**10. Ending Conversations:**
- "Thank you for your time."
- "It was great meeting you."
- "I look forward to hearing from you."
- "Let's stay in touch."

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Exhibitor:** Good morning! Welcome to Premium Textiles!  
**Visitor:** Hello, I'm Anna from Fashion Boutique. We're looking for high-quality fabrics for our new collection.  
**Exhibitor:** Great! What type of fabrics are you interested in?  
**Visitor:** Mainly cotton and linen. Do you have organic options?  
**Exhibitor:** Yes, absolutely. Let me show you our organic cotton range. Feel this – it's 100% certified organic. What quantities do you typically order?  
**Visitor:** Usually 500-1000 meters per design. What's your minimum order?  
**Exhibitor:** Our minimum is 300 meters. May I have your business card? I'll send you pricing and our full catalog.  
**Visitor:** Sure, here you are. Could I take some samples?  
**Exhibitor:** Of course! I'll prepare a sample pack for you.

*Practice in pairs for 5 minutes.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### **Exercise 1: Match Questions to Answers**

**Questions:**
1. What's your minimum order quantity?
2. Do you ship internationally?
3. What are your payment terms?
4. Do you have certifications?
5. What are your lead times?

**Answers:**
a) Yes, we ship worldwide. Shipping takes 2-3 weeks.  
b) Our MOQ is 500 units.  
c) We're ISO certified and have organic certifications.  
d) Typically 4-6 weeks after order confirmation.  
e) 30% deposit, 70% before shipping.

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-a, 3-e, 4-c, 5-d

*Practice asking and answering these questions in pairs – 4 minutes.*

### **Exercise 2: Qualify the Visitor**

"You're exhibiting at a trade fair. A visitor approaches your booth. What questions will you ask to understand if they're a good potential customer?"

*Students brainstorm questions in pairs – 3 minutes.*

Sample questions:
- What does your company do?
- What are you looking for today?
- Are you currently using [similar product]?
- What's your budget/quantity?
- When are you looking to purchase?

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Trade Fair Simulation (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now we'll simulate a trade fair! Half of you are exhibitors with booths. Half are visitors looking for suppliers. We'll switch roles after 6 minutes!"

### **Setup:**

**Group A: Exhibitors (4 students)**
Each has a booth:
1. Textile manufacturer (fabrics)
2. Packaging supplier
3. Logistics company
4. Marketing services agency

**Group B: Visitors (4 students)**
Each is looking for:
1. New fabric supplier
2. Packaging for products
3. Shipping company
4. Marketing help

**Instructions:**

"Exhibitors: Stand at your 'booth' (your desk). Greet visitors, present products, ask questions, collect business cards (paper).

Visitors: Visit all 4 booths. Introduce yourself, ask questions, collect information.

Everyone: Exchange 'business cards' (write your info on paper). You have 6 minutes!"

*After 6 minutes: SWITCH ROLES*

*After 12 minutes total:*

"Who made good connections? What did you learn? What was challenging?"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent trade fair practice!

**TRADE FAIR SUCCESS FORMULA:**

**Before:**
- Research exhibitors/visitors
- Prepare materials
- Set goals

**During:**
- Be approachable and friendly
- Ask qualifying questions
- Take good notes
- Collect contacts

**After:**
- Follow up within 48 hours
- Send promised information
- Build the relationship

Remember: Trade fairs are about starting relationships, not closing deals immediately!

What will you do at your next trade fair? 30 seconds."

---

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

**Option 1:** Write a follow-up email (80-100 words) to someone you met at a trade fair.

**Option 2:** Create a list of 10 questions you would ask at a trade fair booth in your industry.

**Option 3:** Research an upcoming trade fair in your industry. Write about it (100-120 words): When? Where? Why would you attend?
