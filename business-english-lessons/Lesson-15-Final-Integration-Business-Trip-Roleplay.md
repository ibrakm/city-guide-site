# Lesson 15: Final Integration Lesson – Full Business Trip Role-play

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Integrating all skills from Lessons 1-14 in a comprehensive business trip simulation

---

## **Lesson Overview**

This final lesson integrates everything students have learned in Lessons 1-14. Students will participate in a complete business trip simulation that includes:
- Introductions
- Travel (airport, hotel)
- Meeting participation
- Product presentations
- Negotiations
- Problem-solving
- Customer service

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Review (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome to our final lesson! Congratulations on completing Lessons 1-14! Today is special – we're going to use EVERYTHING you've learned in one big simulation: a complete business trip!

First, let's review quickly. I'll ask questions about what we've learned. Answer quickly!"

**Review Questions (Quick-fire round):**

**Teacher asks (students answer as a group):**

1. "How do you introduce yourself professionally?" → *"I'm [name] from [company]."*
2. "How do you make a polite request?" → *"Could you... / Would you mind..."*
3. "You're late for a meeting. What do you say?" → *"I'm very sorry, but..."*
4. "How do you disagree politely in a meeting?" → *"I see your point, but..."*
5. "A customer complains. What's the first thing you do?" → *"Listen and apologize"*
6. "How do you describe a product benefit?" → *"This means you can... / This allows you to..."*

**Teacher Script:**
"Excellent! You remember everything! Now, let's put it all into practice. Today you're going on a business trip!"

**Instructions:**
"Quickly discuss with your partner: What have you learned in the past 14 lessons? What was most useful? 3 minutes!"

*After 3 minutes:*

"Good! Today you'll need ALL these skills. Let's begin our business trip!"

---

## **Stage 2: Simulation Setup & Role Assignment (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Here's the situation: You work for two different companies. Group A is from **TechGlobal** (a technology company selling software and equipment). Group B is from **Business Solutions Inc.** (a company that needs new technology).

TechGlobal is visiting Business Solutions Inc. to:
1. Introduce their company and products
2. Have meetings and negotiations
3. Build a business relationship

The trip includes: arrival, meetings, presentations, negotiations, a business dinner, and some problems you'll need to solve!"

**Divide class into two groups of 4:**

**GROUP A: TechGlobal (Visitors/Suppliers)**
- Person 1: Sarah Chen – Sales Director
- Person 2: David Kim – Product Manager
- Person 3: Maria Santos – Technical Specialist
- Person 4: John Park – Business Development Manager

**GROUP B: Business Solutions Inc. (Hosts/Potential Clients)**
- Person 5: Anna Wilson – CEO
- Person 6: Tom Lee – IT Manager
- Person 7: Lisa Martinez – Purchasing Manager
- Person 8: Robert Brown – Operations Director

### **Teacher distributes role cards to each student**

**Teacher Script:**
"Read your role card carefully. It tells you who you are, what you want, and what you need to do. You have 2 minutes to read!"

*Students read their roles.*

---

## **Stage 3: Simulation Phase 1 – Arrival & Introductions (10 min)**

### **Scenario:**

**Teacher Script:**
"TechGlobal team has just arrived in the city. It's Monday morning. Business Solutions Inc. is meeting them at their hotel lobby. You're meeting for the first time. Group B, you're already at the hotel. Group A, you just came down from your rooms. Introduce yourselves, make small talk, and arrange to go to the office together. You have 5 minutes. Go!"

**What students should do:**
- Professional introductions
- Exchange business cards (use paper/imaginary)
- Small talk (travel, hotel, weather)
- Polite conversation

*Monitor and listen for: professional introductions, small talk, polite language.*

*After 5 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now you're all going to the Business Solutions office. On the way, continue chatting. Group B, give directions to your office. Everyone, keep making small talk! 3 minutes!"

**What students should do:**
- Give/follow directions
- Continue small talk about the city, business, weekend plans
- Build rapport

*After 3 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Perfect! You've arrived at the office. Let's move to the meeting!"

---

## **Stage 4: Simulation Phase 2 – The Business Meeting (12 min)**

### **Scenario:**

**Teacher Script:**
"You're now in the conference room. Business Solutions wants to learn about TechGlobal's products. TechGlobal wants to understand Business Solutions' needs. Have a meeting! 

Group B (Business Solutions): Ask about their company, products, and services.  
Group A (TechGlobal): Present your company and products. Describe features and benefits.

Everyone: Ask questions, give opinions, participate actively! You have 8 minutes!"

**Role Card Information:**

**TechGlobal offers:**
- Cloud-based business management software ($99-$299 per month)
- Cybersecurity services
- IT consulting and training
- 24/7 technical support

**Business Solutions Inc. needs:**
- Better software for managing customer data (current system is old and slow)
- Improved cybersecurity (had a security problem last year)
- Training for 50 employees
- Budget: approximately $10,000-$15,000 per year

**What students should do:**
- TechGlobal: Present products, describe benefits, answer questions
- Business Solutions: Ask questions, express opinions, discuss needs
- Everyone: Use meeting language (agreeing, disagreeing, suggesting)

*Monitor for: product descriptions, meeting participation, opinion expressions.*

*After 8 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Excellent meeting! Now, Business Solutions team, discuss internally: Are you interested? What concerns do you have? 2 minutes!"

*Group B discusses internally.*

**Teacher Script:**
"And TechGlobal team, discuss: What's your strategy? What discount can you offer? 2 minutes!"

*Group A discusses internally.*

---

## **Stage 5: Simulation Phase 3 – Negotiation (10 min)**

### **Scenario:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, it's time to negotiate! Business Solutions is interested, but they think the price is too high. TechGlobal wants this client! Negotiate the best deal for both sides!

Business Solutions: Your maximum budget is $12,000 per year. Try to get a good price and extra services!

TechGlobal: You can offer discounts (up to 20% off), free training, extended support. But you need at least $10,000 per year to make a profit!

Negotiate! Find a solution that works for both sides. You have 7 minutes!"

**What students should do:**
- Request quotes
- Negotiate prices
- Make offers and counteroffers
- Discuss payment terms
- Reach an agreement

*Monitor for: negotiation language, polite disagreement, compromising.*

*After 7 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Stop! Did you reach an agreement? What's the final deal?"

*Each group reports their agreement.*

"Excellent negotiation! Both sides worked together to find a solution!"

---

## **Stage 6: Simulation Phase 4 – Problem Solving (8 min)**

### **Scenario:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Oh no! Some problems have occurred! I'm giving each person a 'problem card.' You must solve your problem using the language we've learned!"

### **Problem Cards (distribute randomly):**

**Problem 1 (TechGlobal member):**
You just realized you left important product brochures at the hotel! You need them for this afternoon's presentation! Call the hotel and ask them to send someone to bring them to the office urgently!

**Problem 2 (Business Solutions member):**
You need to reschedule tomorrow's meeting from 9 AM to 2 PM because of an emergency. Call/tell the TechGlobal team and apologize!

**Problem 3 (TechGlobal member):**
You received an angry email from another client saying your software has a problem. You need to handle this complaint professionally. Write/explain your response!

**Problem 4 (Business Solutions member):**
Your company car that was supposed to take the TechGlobal team to dinner broke down! You need to arrange taxis urgently and apologize for the inconvenience!

**Problem 5 (TechGlobal member):**
Your colleague (another TechGlobal team member) is sick and can't attend tomorrow's training session. You need to call Business Solutions and suggest a solution!

**Problem 6 (Business Solutions member):**
The conference room for tomorrow's presentation is double-booked! You need to find another room or reschedule. Explain the problem to TechGlobal!

**Problem 7 (TechGlobal member):**
Your laptop isn't working! You need it for the presentation this afternoon! Ask Business Solutions if you can borrow one or use their equipment!

**Problem 8 (Business Solutions member):**
The catering for lunch hasn't arrived! You need to apologize to the TechGlobal team and find a quick solution (order food delivery? go to a restaurant?).

**Teacher Script:**
"Read your problem card. You have 6 minutes to solve your problem! Find the right person and handle it professionally!"

*Students work on solving their problems with partners.*

*After 6 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Good problem-solving! How did you handle the issues? Let's hear two examples!"

*Two students briefly explain how they solved their problems.*

---

## **Stage 7: Simulation Phase 5 – Business Dinner (10 min)**

### **Scenario:**

**Teacher Script:**
"It's 7 PM. You're all at a restaurant for a business dinner! This is a more relaxed setting. Group B, you're the hosts, so you help everyone order and make recommendations. Everyone: make small talk, build relationships, discuss non-business topics too!

Arrange your chairs like you're sitting at a restaurant table. You have 8 minutes!"

**What students should do:**
- Make restaurant recommendations
- Order food (imaginary menu)
- Make small talk (hobbies, sports, culture, travel)
- Build rapport
- Avoid controversial topics!
- Be friendly and professional

*Monitor for: small talk topics, restaurant language, social skills.*

*After 8 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Wonderful dinner conversation! You've built great business relationships!"

---

## **Stage 8: Wrap-up, Feedback & Reflection (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Congratulations! You've completed a full business trip simulation! Let's reflect on what you did today."

**Teacher asks:**

1. "What parts of the simulation were easy? What was difficult?"
2. "What language from Lessons 1-14 did you use today?"
3. "What did you learn about yourselves as English communicators?"
4. "What will you practice more after this course?"

*Students discuss in pairs for 2 minutes, then share with class.*

### **Teacher Feedback:**

**Teacher Script:**
"I'm very proud of all of you! Today you:
- Introduced yourselves professionally
- Made small talk and built relationships
- Participated in meetings and gave opinions
- Described products and benefits
- Negotiated and reached agreements
- Solved problems calmly
- Handled social business situations

Let me share some excellent language I heard today..."

*Teacher shares 4-5 specific examples of good language use from students.*

"I also noticed a few areas for improvement..."

*Teacher gives 2-3 gentle corrections of common errors.*

### **Final Reflection:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Think about this: 15 lessons ago, could you do all these things in English? Look how much you've improved!

Remember these THREE KEY POINTS from our 15 lessons:

1. **Preparation** – Know your key phrases for each situation
2. **Confidence** – You CAN communicate in business English! Don't be afraid to make mistakes!
3. **Politeness** – In business, being polite is MORE important than being perfect!

Keep practicing! Use English at work! Watch business videos! Read business articles! Your English will continue to improve!"

### **Celebration:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's celebrate! Turn to the person next to you and say: 'Congratulations! You did a great job in this course!' Then shake hands or give a high-five!"

*Students congratulate each other.*

"Thank you all for your hard work, participation, and positive attitude! It's been a pleasure teaching you! Good luck with your future business communication in English!"

---

## **Stage 9: Course Evaluation & Certificates (5 min - Optional)**

### Teacher Script:
"Before we finish, I'd like your feedback. What did you like about this course? What would you change? What topics would you like to study next?"

*Quick feedback discussion or written evaluation forms.*

**Optional:** If you have certificates prepared, distribute them now.

"Thank you again! Stay in touch, keep practicing, and remember – you're all capable business English communicators!"

---

## **Materials Needed:**
- 8 detailed role cards (describing each person's job, goals, company info)
- 8 problem cards
- Tables/chairs arranged for meetings and dinner
- Optional: props (phones, paper for "business cards", menus)
- Optional: certificates of completion

## **Key Vocabulary Reviewed:**
All vocabulary from Lessons 1-14, including: introductions, requests, apologies, phone calls, meetings, travel, directions, problems, emails, opinions, products, negotiations, customer service

## **Timing Breakdown:**
- Warm-up & review: 10 min
- Role assignment: 5 min
- Phase 1 (Arrivals & introductions): 10 min
- Phase 2 (Meeting): 12 min
- Phase 3 (Negotiation): 10 min
- Phase 4 (Problems): 8 min
- Phase 5 (Dinner): 10 min
- Wrap-up & reflection: 10 min
- **Total: 75 minutes**

---

## **Teacher Notes:**

This final lesson is designed to be:
- **Integrative** – Uses all skills from previous 14 lessons
- **Realistic** – Simulates real business scenarios
- **Student-centered** – Students speak 90%+ of the time
- **Confidence-building** – Shows students how much they've learned
- **Memorable** – Creates a positive final experience

**Flexibility:** If your class finishes any phase quickly, extend the business dinner or add more small talk time. If running short on time, you can shorten the problem-solving phase.

**Assessment:** Use this lesson to observe each student's overall progress. Note areas where students excel and areas needing further practice.

**Follow-up:** Consider giving students a final self-assessment questionnaire about their confidence in different business English skills.
