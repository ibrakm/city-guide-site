# Lesson 14: Customer Service & Handling Complaints

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Dealing with customer complaints, providing solutions, staying professional under pressure

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning about customer service and handling complaints. This is challenging but very important! Let's discuss."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Do you work with customers in your job?
2. Have you ever had to deal with a customer complaint? What happened?
3. How do you feel when a customer complains?
4. What makes good customer service?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your experiences – both good and bad!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share! Who has experience with customer complaints? How did you handle it?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

**Teacher adds:**
"Dealing with complaints is difficult, especially in a foreign language! But today we'll learn how to stay calm, professional, and find solutions."

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now, I'm writing 'CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS' on the board. In pairs, brainstorm: What do customers complain about? What phrases do you use to help them? What problems happen? 2 minutes!"

*Write "CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me complaint situations and helpful phrases!"

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: delay, defective, broken, wrong, disappointed, apologize, refund, replace, solution, investigate, inconvenience, unacceptable, satisfied, resolve, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What are common complaints?"
- "What do you say when you apologize?"
- "What solutions can you offer?"

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now I'm going to read three customer service conversations. Listen to how the service representative handles each complaint. I'll read each one twice."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Conversation 1: Product Problem**

**Customer:** Hello, I bought this coffee machine from your store last week, and it's already broken. It doesn't turn on at all!

**Service Rep:** I'm very sorry to hear that. That must be very frustrating. Could I see your receipt, please?

**Customer:** Here it is. I only used it three times! This is unacceptable.

**Service Rep:** I completely understand your frustration. You're absolutely right – this shouldn't happen. Let me help you. We can offer you two options: a full refund, or we can replace it with a new machine immediately.

**Customer:** I'd like a replacement, please. But I need to be sure this one will work!

**Service Rep:** Of course. I'll personally test the new machine before you leave to make sure it's working perfectly. And it comes with a 2-year warranty, so you're fully protected.

**Customer:** Okay, thank you. I appreciate your help.

**Service Rep:** You're very welcome, and again, I apologize for the inconvenience.

---

**Conversation 2: Delivery Delay**

*Phone conversation*

**Customer:** Hi, I'm calling about order number 5432. I ordered it three weeks ago, and it still hasn't arrived! You said it would take 7-10 days!

**Service Rep:** I sincerely apologize for this delay. Let me check your order status immediately... I can see your order here. It appears there was a problem at our warehouse, which caused the delay.

**Customer:** This is very disappointing! I needed this for an important event last week!

**Service Rep:** I completely understand, and I'm truly sorry this has happened. The item is being shipped today by express delivery, so you'll receive it tomorrow. I'd also like to offer you a 20% discount on this order as an apology.

**Customer:** Well... I suppose that's fair. But I really needed it last week.

**Service Rep:** I understand, and you're absolutely right to be upset. Is there anything else I can do to help?

**Customer:** No, as long as it arrives tomorrow.

**Service Rep:** I'll send you a tracking number by email within the hour, so you can monitor the delivery yourself. Again, my sincere apologies.

**Customer:** Okay, thank you.

---

**Conversation 3: Wrong Order**

**Customer:** Excuse me, I ordered the blue jacket in size medium, but you've sent me a red one in size large! This is completely wrong!

**Service Rep:** I'm very sorry about this mistake. Let me look at your order... Yes, I can see the error. This is our fault. I apologize.

**Customer:** So what are you going to do about it?

**Service Rep:** We'll send you the correct jacket immediately – blue, size medium – at no extra cost. You'll receive it within 2-3 business days.

**Customer:** And what about this wrong jacket? Do I have to return it?

**Service Rep:** We'll arrange a free pickup, or if you prefer, you can return it to any of our stores. We'll also refund the original shipping cost.

**Customer:** Hmm... actually, can I just keep this one and get a partial refund instead? My brother might like it.

**Service Rep:** Let me check with my manager... Yes, we can offer you a 40% refund, and you can keep the jacket.

**Customer:** That works for me. Thank you.

**Service Rep:** You're welcome. Again, I apologize for the error. Is there anything else I can help you with today?

**Customer:** No, that's all. Thanks.

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What was wrong in Conversation 1? What solution did the rep offer?
2. In Conversation 2, why was the order delayed?
3. What compensation did the rep offer in Conversation 2?
4. What mistake happened in Conversation 3?
5. What creative solution did the customer suggest?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. Did the service reps handle the complaints well? Why?
2. What phrases did they use to apologize?
3. Which complaint was most difficult? Why?
4. How would you handle these situations?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's learn key phrases for handling customer complaints professionally."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**STEP 1: LISTENING & ACKNOWLEDGING:**

**1. Showing You Understand:**
- I understand.
- I completely understand your frustration.
- I can see why you're upset.
- You're absolutely right.
- That must be very frustrating/disappointing.

**STEP 2: APOLOGIZING:**

**2. Expressing Apology:**
- I'm very sorry about this.
- I sincerely apologize for [problem].
- Please accept my apologies.
- I'm truly sorry this has happened.
- This is unacceptable, and I apologize.

**STEP 3: TAKING RESPONSIBILITY:**

**3. Acknowledging the Problem:**
- This is our fault/mistake.
- This shouldn't have happened.
- You're right to be upset.
- We made an error.

**IMPORTANT:** Don't blame others or make excuses!
- ❌ "The delivery company lost it – not our fault."
- ✅ "There was a problem with the delivery. I sincerely apologize."

**STEP 4: FINDING SOLUTIONS:**

**4. Offering Solutions:**
- Let me help you with this.
- I can offer you [solution].
- We have two options: [A] or [B].
- What I can do is [solution].
- Would you prefer [option A] or [option B]?

**5. Taking Action:**
- Let me check this for you immediately.
- I'll investigate this right away.
- I'll contact [department] for you.
- Let me see what I can do.

**STEP 5: OFFERING COMPENSATION:**

**6. Making It Right:**
- I'd like to offer you [compensation].
- As an apology, we can [solution].
- We'll give you a full refund/replacement.
- We'll offer you a [discount/credit/upgrade].

**STEP 6: FOLLOWING UP:**

**7. Ensuring Resolution:**
- Is there anything else I can do for you?
- Are you satisfied with this solution?
- I'll send you a confirmation email.
- Please contact me if you have any other concerns.

**THE CUSTOMER SERVICE FORMULA:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember this formula for handling complaints:

1. **LISTEN** – Let the customer explain
2. **ACKNOWLEDGE** – Show you understand
3. **APOLOGIZE** – Say sorry sincerely
4. **SOLVE** – Offer a solution
5. **FOLLOW UP** – Make sure they're satisfied

Never argue with customers! Even if they're wrong, stay calm and professional."

### **Useful Phrases to AVOID:**

**Teacher Script:**
"DON'T say these things:
- ❌ 'That's not my fault.'
- ❌ 'You're wrong.'
- ❌ 'Calm down.' (This makes people more angry!)
- ❌ 'There's nothing I can do.'
- ❌ 'That's our policy.' (Offer solutions, not just rules!)

INSTEAD say:
- ✅ 'I apologize for this problem.'
- ✅ 'I understand your concern.'
- ✅ 'Let me see what I can do to help.'
- ✅ 'I'll find a solution for you.'"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll read the Customer, you all read the Service Rep."

**Customer:** This product is defective! I want my money back!  
**Service Rep:** I'm very sorry about this. That must be very frustrating. May I see your receipt?  
**Customer:** Here! I bought it yesterday!  
**Service Rep:** I completely understand. This shouldn't have happened. I can offer you a full refund or a replacement. Which would you prefer?  
**Customer:** A refund, please.  
**Service Rep:** Of course. I'll process that immediately. Again, I apologize for the inconvenience.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs, then create your own complaint situations!"

*Give 4 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Put the Steps in Order**

**Instructions:**
Number these steps 1-6 for handling a complaint:

___ a) Offer a solution or compensation  
___ b) Listen to the customer's problem  
___ c) Follow up and confirm they're satisfied  
___ d) Apologize sincerely  
___ e) Take action to resolve the problem  
___ f) Acknowledge their feelings and show understanding

**Correct Order:** b(1), f(2), d(3), a(4), e(5), c(6)

**Teacher Script:**
"Put these in the correct order with your partner. This is the customer service process!"

*Give 2 minutes. Check together.*

### **Exercise 2: Professional or Unprofessional?**

**Instructions:**
Mark each response P (professional) or U (unprofessional):

1. ___ "That's not my problem."
2. ___ "I completely understand your frustration."
3. ___ "Calm down!"
4. ___ "I sincerely apologize for this mistake."
5. ___ "You should have read the instructions."
6. ___ "Let me see what I can do to help you."
7. ___ "That's our policy. I can't change it."
8. ___ "I'll investigate this immediately."

**Answers:** 1-U, 2-P, 3-U, 4-P, 5-U, 6-P, 7-U, 8-P

**Teacher Script:**
"Check with your partner. Then rewrite the unprofessional ones to make them professional!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Sample answers:**
1. "I understand your concern. Let me help you."
3. "I understand this is frustrating."
5. "I apologize for the confusion. Let me explain how it works."
7. "I understand this is our policy. However, let me check if there's anything I can do in this situation."

### **Exercise 3: Match Problems with Solutions**

**Problems:**
1. Product arrived broken
2. Order is 2 weeks late
3. Wrong item was delivered
4. Customer received poor service

**Solutions:**
a) Offer free replacement + discount on next order  
b) Offer refund or exchange for correct item + free return  
c) Offer express shipping + 15% discount + tracking number  
d) Offer full refund or replacement + personal quality check

**Answers:** 1-d, 2-c, 3-b, 4-a

*Give 3 minutes to match and practice offering these solutions.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Perfect! Now realistic customer service practice! Work in pairs. One person is the customer (who has a problem), one is the service representative (who solves it). You'll do 2-3 different situations. Switch roles!"

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Situation 1A: Customer (Angry!)**
You ordered a laptop computer online 2 weeks ago for your business. It was supposed to arrive in 5-7 days. It still hasn't arrived! You need it urgently for work. You're very angry and frustrated. The website tracking doesn't work.

**Situation 1B: Service Rep**
Check the customer's order. The laptop was shipped but there was a warehouse error – it went to the wrong address! You can:
- Send a replacement by express delivery (arrives tomorrow)
- Offer 15% discount
- Provide direct phone number for updates
Apologize and solve the problem!

---

**Situation 2A: Customer (Disappointed)**
You bought an expensive office chair from a furniture store. After only 2 months, it's broken (the back support is broken). You paid $400! The store said it was "high quality" and "durable." You want a refund or replacement.

**Situation 2B: Service Rep**
This is unusual – these chairs normally last years! You can offer:
- Full replacement with a new chair (different model, better quality)
- Full refund
- Repair + 50% discount on another product
Be understanding and professional!

---

**Situation 3A: Customer (Frustrated)**
You're at a hotel. Problems with your room:
- Air conditioning doesn't work (very hot!)
- No hot water in shower
- Room is noisy (can hear traffic)
- WiFi doesn't work
You paid for a "superior business room"! This is unacceptable! You have important work to do tonight.

**Situation 3B: Hotel Manager**
These problems are serious! You can offer:
- Immediate room change to a suite (much better, quiet, everything works)
- Free breakfast for duration of stay
- 30% refund on room price
- Complimentary room service tonight
Fix this situation – you don't want a bad review!

---

**Situation 4A: Customer (Confused and Annoyed)**
You ordered:
- 50 blue pens
- 30 notebooks
- 20 folders

You received:
- 30 blue pens (only 30, not 50!)
- 30 RED notebooks (you wanted white!)
- 25 folders (you only ordered 20!)

Everything is wrong! You need these for a conference tomorrow! Call the company and complain!

**Situation 4B: Service Rep**
This is a serious error! Multiple mistakes! You can:
- Send correct items by emergency courier (arrives tonight or early tomorrow morning)
- Full refund on this order
- Give them the extra 5 folders for free
- Offer 25% discount on next order
Apologize sincerely – this is embarrassing!

---

**Situation 5A: Customer (Very Angry)**
You took your car to a repair shop for an oil change (should cost $50, take 1 hour). You left it there for 3 hours. Now they say:
- It cost $350 (they found "other problems")
- It needs another 2 hours of work
- You didn't approve this extra work!
You're very angry – you think they're trying to cheat you!

**Situation 5B: Service Manager**
Your mechanic found serious safety problems (brakes nearly failing). You had to fix them – it's dangerous not to! But you should have called the customer first. You can:
- Show the customer the broken parts (prove it was necessary)
- Offer 15% discount on the repairs
- Explain you were trying to keep them safe
- Next oil change free
Stay calm even if customer is angry!

---

**Situation 6A: Customer (Disappointed)**
You hired a cleaning company to clean your office. They came yesterday. Problems:
- They didn't clean the bathrooms properly
- Didn't empty all the trash bins
- Didn't vacuum under desks
- Finished in 1 hour (should take 2 hours!)
You paid $200! You want a refund or they come back and do it properly!

**Situation 6B: Cleaning Company Manager**
This is not acceptable! Your staff did poor work! You can offer:
- Send a team back TODAY to clean properly (no extra charge)
- 50% refund on this cleaning
- Next cleaning free
- Assign a supervisor to check quality next time
Apologize – this damages your reputation!

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair: do 2-3 different situations. Customer: be upset! Show real frustration! Service rep: stay calm, use the phrases we learned, find solutions!

Remember:
- Customer: explain the problem clearly
- Service rep: listen, apologize, solve!

You have 12 minutes. Go!"

*Monitor. Listen for complaint handling language. Take notes.*

*After 12 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Great work! That was challenging! Who had a very angry customer? How did you handle it?"

*Brief discussion.*

"Let's see one example. Pair 3, can you show us one of your situations?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Excellent! What good phrases did you hear? What made the solution effective?"

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic customer service! You all handled difficult situations very professionally!

Let's remember the FIVE GOLDEN RULES for handling complaints:

1. **Stay calm** – Never argue, even if the customer is rude
2. **Listen carefully** – Let them explain fully before you respond
3. **Apologize sincerely** – 'I sincerely apologize' is powerful!
4. **Take responsibility** – Don't blame others or make excuses
5. **Offer solutions** – Always give options, not just problems

One important point: The words you use are only 30% of communication. Your TONE is 70%! Say 'I apologize' with a sincere, calm voice, not an annoyed or sarcastic voice!

Think: What will you do differently next time you handle a customer complaint? 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: A complaint is an opportunity! If you handle it well, you can turn an angry customer into a loyal customer!"
