# Lesson 3: Polite Requests & Apologies in Business

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Making polite requests, asking for help, apologizing professionally

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Hello everyone! Today we're going to learn how to make polite requests and apologize in business situations. Let's start with some questions. Think about your answers."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. When do you need to ask for help at work?
2. What's the difference between asking a friend for help and asking a colleague?
3. When was the last time you had to apologize at work? What happened?
4. Is it important to be polite in business? Why?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. You have 4 minutes. Partner A, start with questions 1 and 2. Go!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share. Who wants to tell us: Why is politeness important in business?"

*Take 2-3 responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good answers! Now, I'm going to write 'POLITE REQUESTS AT WORK' on the board. Think about different situations where you need to ask people for things at work. What do you ask for? Brainstorm words and phrases with your partner. 2 minutes!"

*Write "POLITE REQUESTS AT WORK" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! What did you think of? Give me your ideas."

*Collect vocabulary on board. Expected answers: could you, would you, please, help, favor, send documents, check something, reschedule, information, deadline, excuse me, sorry, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you ask people to send you?"
- "What do you need help with?"
- "What words make requests more polite?"

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read some business emails. These show polite requests and apologies. Read silently, then we'll discuss."

### **Reading Text:**

**Email 1: Polite Request**

From: james.wong@techsolutions.com  
To: anna.brown@supplies.co.uk  
Subject: Request for Product Catalog

Dear Ms. Brown,

I hope this email finds you well. I'm writing to ask if you could send me your latest product catalog. We're looking for new suppliers for office equipment, and we're very interested in your products.

Could you also provide information about your delivery times and payment terms? We would need regular deliveries to our main office in Singapore.

I would appreciate it if you could send this information by the end of this week.

Thank you very much for your help.

Best regards,  
James Wong

---

**Email 2: Apology**

From: michael.peters@globalship.com  
To: susan.li@trading.com  
Subject: Apology for Delivery Delay

Dear Ms. Li,

I'm writing to apologize for the delay in delivering your order #3456. Unfortunately, we experienced some technical problems at our warehouse last week, which caused the delay.

I'm very sorry for any inconvenience this may have caused. Your order will be delivered tomorrow morning, 15th March, before 10 AM.

As an apology, we would like to offer you a 10% discount on your next order.

Please accept our sincere apologies. If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact me.

Best regards,  
Michael Peters

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, answer these questions with your partner:"

1. What does James want from Ms. Brown?
2. What information does James need?
3. Why is Michael writing to Ms. Li?
4. What does Michael offer as an apology?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now discuss these questions:"

1. Are these emails polite? How do you know?
2. Have you ever written a similar email? What did you write?
3. What would happen if these emails were not polite?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's learn the key language for making polite requests and apologizing. This is very important in business!"

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**1. Making Polite Requests:**
- "Could you [verb], please?"
- "Would you mind [verb+-ing]?"
- "I would appreciate it if you could [verb]"
- "Would it be possible to [verb]?"

**Examples:**
- "Could you send me the report, please?"
- "Would you mind checking these figures?"
- "I would appreciate it if you could reply by Friday."
- "Would it be possible to reschedule our meeting?"

**2. Apologizing:**
- "I'm sorry for [noun/-ing]"
- "I apologize for [noun/-ing]"
- "Please accept my apologies for [noun/-ing]"
- "I'm very sorry for any inconvenience"

**Examples:**
- "I'm sorry for the delay."
- "I apologize for being late."
- "Please accept my apologies for the mistake."

**3. Responding to Requests:**
- "Of course!"
- "Certainly!"
- "No problem."
- "I'd be happy to help."

### **Grammar Note:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Notice: After 'Would you mind,' we use the -ING form:
- 'Would you mind SENDING the file?' (not 'Would you mind TO SEND')

After 'Could you' and 'I would appreciate it if you could,' we use the base verb:
- 'Could you SEND the file?' (not 'Could you SENDING')"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll read Person A, you all read Person B first."

**Person A:** Excuse me, could you help me with something?  
**Person B:** Of course! What do you need?  
**Person A:** Would you mind checking this contract? I want to make sure it's correct.  
**Person B:** Certainly. When do you need it?  
**Person A:** I would appreciate it if you could finish it by tomorrow.  
**Person B:** No problem. I'll have it ready by then.  
**Person A:** Thank you so much!

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs. Partner A is A, Partner B is B. Then swap roles."

*Give 2 minutes.*

"Now, change the dialogue. Think of a different favor to ask. Practice your new dialogue!"

*Give 3 minutes. Monitor.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's practice with some exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Make the Requests More Polite**

**Teacher Script:**
"These requests are too direct. Make them more polite using our phrases."

**Instructions:**
Rewrite these requests more politely:

1. Send me the report.  
   → Could you _________________, please?

2. Check my presentation.  
   → Would you mind _________________?

3. Reply before Friday.  
   → I would appreciate it if you could _________________.

4. Reschedule the meeting.  
   → Would it be possible to _________________?

5. Give me more information.  
   → Could you _________________, please?

**Sample Answers:**
1. Could you send me the report, please?
2. Would you mind checking my presentation?
3. I would appreciate it if you could reply before Friday.
4. Would it be possible to reschedule the meeting?
5. Could you give me more information, please?

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's check together. Number 1, Paolo?"

*Check all answers.*

### **Exercise 2: Apology Responses**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, match each situation with an appropriate apology."

**Situations:**
1. You're 15 minutes late for a meeting.
2. You forgot to send an important email.
3. Your company delivered the wrong products.
4. You made a mistake in a report.

**Apologies:**
a) I'm very sorry for the error in the report.  
b) I apologize for being late.  
c) Please accept our apologies for sending the wrong items.  
d) I'm sorry for not sending the email.

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-d, 3-c, 4-a

**Teacher Script:**
"Practice saying these apologies with your partner. Partner A gives the situation, Partner B apologizes."

*Give 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now it's time for real practice. You'll work in pairs. Each pair will get two role-play cards. Do both situations. I'll give you 8 minutes."

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Prepare 4 sets (8 cards total) for pair work:**

**Situation 1A:**
You need your colleague to:
- Send you last month's sales figures
- Check your presentation slides
- Attend a meeting tomorrow at 3 PM
(Make polite requests)

**Situation 1B:**
You are the colleague. Listen and respond politely. You can help with everything!

---

**Situation 2A:**
You are a supplier. You need to apologize because:
- The delivery is 3 days late
- You sent wrong quantity (100 instead of 150)
- Some products are damaged
(Apologize and offer a solution)

**Situation 2B:**
You are the customer. You're unhappy about the problems. Listen to the apology. Accept or ask for a better solution.

---

**Situation 3A:**
You need your manager to:
- Give you extra time to finish a report (need 2 more days)
- Approve your holiday request
- Send feedback on your work
(Make polite requests)

**Situation 3B:**
You are the manager. Listen to the requests. You can approve the holiday and send feedback, but the report must be ready tomorrow.

---

**Situation 4A:**
You need to apologize to a client because:
- You missed their phone call
- You gave them wrong information about prices
- You forgot to confirm their booking
(Apologize professionally)

**Situation 4B:**
You are the client. You're not very happy. Listen to the apology and ask questions.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair, take your role cards. Do Situation 1 first. Then swap roles and do Situation 2. Remember to use our polite phrases! You have 10 minutes total."

*Students work in pairs. Monitor and take notes.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Good work! Let's hear from one pair. Can you show us one situation?"

*Choose one strong pair to demonstrate.*

"Excellent! What polite phrases did you hear?"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"You all did wonderful work today! I heard great polite language. For example, I heard Kenji say 'Would you mind checking this?' – perfect! And I heard Lisa say 'I apologize for the inconvenience' – very professional!

Let me correct two common mistakes:

1. We say 'Would you mind SENDING' (with -ING), not 'Would you mind TO SEND.'

2. We say 'I'm sorry FOR the delay,' not 'I'm sorry ABOUT the delay.' Use FOR after 'sorry' in apologies.

Now think: How can being polite help you in business? Discuss with your partner for 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Exactly! Politeness builds good relationships and makes people want to help you. Practice these phrases every day!"
