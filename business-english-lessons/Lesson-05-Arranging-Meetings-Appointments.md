# Lesson 5: Arranging Meetings & Appointments

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Scheduling meetings, suggesting times, confirming appointments, rescheduling

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning how to arrange meetings and appointments in English. Let's start with some questions."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. How many meetings do you have in a typical week?
2. How do you usually arrange meetings – by email, phone, or in person?
3. Have you ever had to change a meeting time? What happened?
4. What information do you need when you arrange a meeting?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions in pairs. You have 4 minutes. Partner A, talk about questions 1 and 2 first. Partner B, answer 3 and 4. Then discuss together!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share! Who has a lot of meetings every week? What kind of meetings?"

*Take 2-3 responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now let's think about meetings. I'm writing 'SCHEDULING MEETINGS' on the board. With your partner, brainstorm: What do you say when you arrange a meeting? What questions do you ask? What information is important? 2 minutes!"

*Write "SCHEDULING MEETINGS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! What did you think of? Give me your ideas."

*Collect on board. Expected answers: time, date, place, available, busy, free, confirm, reschedule, postpone, cancel, agenda, calendar, venue, attendees, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you ask about time?"
- "What if you need to change the meeting?"
- "What words talk about your schedule?"

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent vocabulary! Now let's read some emails about arranging meetings. Read both emails, then answer the questions."

### **Reading Text:**

**Email 1: Suggesting a Meeting**

From: david.miller@techsales.com  
To: anna.wong@globalimports.com  
Subject: Meeting to Discuss Partnership

Dear Ms. Wong,

Thank you for your interest in our products. I would like to arrange a meeting to discuss a possible partnership between our companies.

Are you available next week? I'm free on Tuesday afternoon or Wednesday morning. We could meet at your office or at a café nearby – whichever is more convenient for you.

The meeting should take about one hour. I'd like to show you our new product line and discuss pricing.

Please let me know which day and time work best for you.

Best regards,  
David Miller  
Sales Director

---

**Email 2: Confirming and Rescheduling**

From: anna.wong@globalimports.com  
To: david.miller@techsales.com  
Subject: RE: Meeting to Discuss Partnership

Dear David,

Thank you for your email. I'm very interested in meeting with you.

Unfortunately, I'm not available next Tuesday or Wednesday. I have meetings all day Tuesday, and I'm traveling on Wednesday.

Would Thursday, 24th March, at 10 AM work for you? We could meet at my office – the address is 123 Business Park, Room 5B.

If Thursday doesn't suit you, I'm also free on Friday afternoon after 2 PM.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,  
Anna Wong  
Purchasing Manager

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Answer these questions with your partner:"

1. Why does David want to meet Anna?
2. When is David available?
3. Why can't Anna meet on Tuesday?
4. What times does Anna suggest?
5. Where does Anna suggest meeting?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now discuss:"

1. Are these emails polite and professional? How do you know?
2. What information do they include about the meeting?
3. How do you usually arrange meetings in your company?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's learn the key language for arranging meetings. These phrases will make you sound professional and clear."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**1. Suggesting a Meeting:**
- "I'd like to arrange/schedule a meeting."
- "Could we meet to discuss [topic]?"
- "I was wondering if we could meet..."
- "Are you available on [day/date]?"

**Examples:**
- "I'd like to arrange a meeting about the new project."
- "Could we meet to discuss the contract?"
- "Are you available next Thursday?"

**2. Suggesting Times:**
- "I'm free on [day] at [time]."
- "How about [day] at [time]?"
- "Would [day/time] work for you?"
- "Does [day/time] suit you?"

**Examples:**
- "I'm free on Monday at 3 PM."
- "How about Tuesday morning?"
- "Would 2 PM work for you?"

**3. Saying You're Not Available:**
- "I'm afraid I'm not available on [day]."
- "Unfortunately, I'm busy at that time."
- "I have another meeting on [day]."
- "Sorry, that doesn't work for me."

**4. Suggesting Alternative Times:**
- "What about [different time]?"
- "How about [different day] instead?"
- "I'm also free on [alternative time]."
- "Would [alternative] be better?"

**5. Confirming Details:**
- "Let me confirm the details."
- "So that's [day] at [time], correct?"
- "I'll see you on [day] at [time]."
- "Looking forward to meeting you."

**6. Rescheduling/Canceling:**
- "I'm sorry, but I need to reschedule our meeting."
- "Could we postpone our meeting?"
- "Would it be possible to change the time?"
- "Unfortunately, I have to cancel our meeting."

### **Time Expressions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember these useful time phrases:"
- "next Monday" / "this Friday"
- "the week of May 15th"
- "early/mid/late next month"
- "at the beginning/end of the week"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! Read this dialogue with me."

**Person A:** Hi Sarah, I'd like to arrange a meeting to discuss the new project. Are you available this week?  
**Person B:** Let me check my calendar... I'm afraid I'm busy most of this week. How about next Monday?  
**Person A:** Monday could work. What time are you free?  
**Person B:** I'm free in the afternoon. Would 2:30 PM suit you?  
**Person A:** Yes, that's perfect. Where shall we meet?  
**Person B:** Let's meet in Conference Room B. The meeting should take about an hour.  
**Person A:** Great! So that's Monday at 2:30 in Conference Room B. I'll see you then!

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice in pairs. Then create your own dialogue – arrange a meeting for a different topic."

*Give 4-5 minutes. Monitor.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great work! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Complete the Conversation**

**Instructions:**
Choose the correct phrase: *I'd like to / How about / I'm afraid / work for / Let me confirm*

**Person A:** Hi Tom, (1) _______ arrange a meeting next week.  
**Person B:** Sure! (2) _______ Tuesday at 10 AM?  
**Person A:** (3) _______ I'm not available on Tuesday. Would Wednesday (4) _______ you?  
**Person B:** Wednesday is fine. (5) _______ the details – Wednesday at 10 AM in my office?  
**Person A:** Perfect!

**Answers:** 1. I'd like to, 2. How about, 3. I'm afraid, 4. work for, 5. Let me confirm

**Teacher Script:**
"Complete the conversation with your partner. Then practice reading it!"

*Give 3 minutes. Check answers.*

### **Exercise 2: Respond to These Situations**

**Teacher Script:**
"What would you say in these situations? Discuss with your partner and write your answers."

1. Someone suggests Monday at 9 AM, but you have another meeting.  
   Your response: _______

2. You want to arrange a meeting with your supplier next week.  
   What you say: _______

3. Someone asks if Thursday at 3 PM works for you. It's perfect.  
   Your response: _______

4. You need to change a meeting from Tuesday to Wednesday.  
   What you say: _______

**Sample Answers:**
1. "I'm afraid I'm not available at 9 AM. I have another meeting. How about 11 AM?"
2. "I'd like to arrange a meeting to discuss our next order. Are you available next week?"
3. "Yes, Thursday at 3 PM works perfectly for me."
4. "I'm sorry, but I need to reschedule our meeting. Would Wednesday be possible instead?"

**Teacher Script:**
"Share your answers with another pair. Are they similar or different?"

*Give 4 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now real practice! You'll work in pairs. You'll have two different situations. Do both – one where you arrange a meeting, and one where you reschedule. I'll give you role cards."

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Prepare 4 sets (8 cards total):**

**Situation 1A: Client**
You want to meet with a new supplier (your partner) to discuss products for your store. You need to see samples and discuss prices. You're free: Wednesday afternoon, Thursday morning, or Friday all day. Suggest meeting at your office or a café.

**Situation 1B: Supplier**
A new client (your partner) wants to meet you. You want this meeting! You're free: Tuesday all day, Thursday after 2 PM, Friday morning only. You prefer to meet at your office (you can show them the showroom). The meeting needs 1-2 hours.

---

**Situation 2A: Marketing Manager**
You scheduled a meeting with the IT department (your partner) for Monday at 10 AM to discuss the new website. But now you have an urgent meeting with a client on Monday. You need to reschedule. You're free: Monday 3 PM, Tuesday morning, Wednesday afternoon.

**Situation 2B: IT Manager**
You have a meeting scheduled with Marketing (your partner) on Monday at 10 AM about the website. You prepared everything! Now they want to reschedule. You're quite busy: Monday you're free only at 10 AM, Tuesday afternoon is okay, Wednesday you're on holiday. Find a solution!

---

**Situation 3A: Conference Organizer**
You need to arrange a planning meeting with your team (your partner) for next week. You need to discuss: budget, venue, speakers. The meeting needs 2 hours. You're free: Monday after 2 PM, Wednesday morning, Thursday all day.

**Situation 3B: Team Member**
Your organizer (your partner) wants a planning meeting. You want to attend! You're free: Monday morning, Tuesday all day, Thursday before 11 AM. You prefer morning meetings (you concentrate better). The office meeting room is available Tuesday and Thursday.

---

**Situation 4A: Sales Director**
You want to meet with an important potential client (your partner) to present your new products. This is important! You're willing to meet whenever they're free. You're available all next week except Tuesday afternoon. Suggest lunch meeting or office meeting.

**Situation 4B: Potential Client**
A sales director (your partner) wants to meet you. You're interested, but very busy. You're free: Monday 4-5 PM only, Wednesday morning, Friday lunch time. You prefer short meetings (max 1 hour). Your office or restaurant is fine.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair, take your role cards. First, read your role carefully. Think about: when you're free, what you need to say. Then start the conversation – try to find a time that works for both of you! You have 10 minutes for both situations."

*Monitor and take notes.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Great negotiations! Did everyone find a time? Who had difficulty? What was the problem?"

*Discuss challenges.*

"Let's hear one example. Pair 4, can you show us your conversation?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful work today! I heard excellent language for arranging meetings. I heard Chen say 'How about Thursday at 2 PM?' – perfect! And I heard Maria say 'I'm afraid I'm not available on Monday' – very polite!

Let me point out two important things:

1. Always suggest an alternative when you can't meet: Don't just say 'I'm busy.' Say 'I'm busy on Monday, but how about Tuesday?'

2. Always confirm the details at the end: 'So that's Wednesday at 3 PM in your office, correct?' This avoids confusion!

Now think: What's the most important thing to remember when arranging meetings in English? Discuss for 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Exactly! Be clear, be polite, and always confirm! Practice this language when you arrange your real meetings this week!"

### Teacher Script:

**Option 2 (Speaking):** Practice having a phone conversation with a friend or colleague. Arrange a real meeting for next week. Try to use at least 5 phrases from today's lesson.

Bring your homework next time. Any questions? See you next lesson!"
