# Lesson 13: Describing Products & Services

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Presenting products/services, describing features and benefits, making comparisons

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Today we're learning how to describe products and services in English. This is essential when you talk to clients, give presentations, or explain what your company does. Let's start!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. What product or service does your company offer?
2. Have you ever had to describe your product/service in English? To whom?
3. What's difficult about describing products in a foreign language?
4. What makes a product description effective?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Think about your own work experience!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's share! What products or services do your companies offer?"

*Take 3-4 responses. Students briefly describe their products.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now, I'm writing 'DESCRIBING PRODUCTS' on the board. Think about when you describe a product or service. In pairs, brainstorm: What adjectives do you use? What information do you include? What phrases are useful? 2 minutes!"

*Write "DESCRIBING PRODUCTS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Okay! Give me your words and phrases!"

*Collect vocabulary. Expected: features, benefits, quality, durable, efficient, innovative, advantages, specifications, compared to, better than, suitable for, designed for, made from, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What adjectives describe good products?"
- "What information do customers want to know?"
- "How do you compare two products?"

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read three different product descriptions. Notice how they describe features, benefits, and advantages. Read all three."

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great analysis! Now let's learn key language for describing products and services effectively."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**INTRODUCING PRODUCTS:**

**1. Opening Statements:**
- This is our [latest/new/flagship] product.
- Let me introduce our [product/service].
- I'd like to present [product name].
- This product is designed for [target customer].

**Examples:**
- "This is our latest smartphone model."
- "Let me introduce our cloud storage service."

**DESCRIBING FEATURES:**

**2. Stating Features (What it has/What it does):**
- It has [feature].
- It offers [feature].
- It comes with [feature].
- It includes [feature].
- It provides [feature].

**Examples:**
- "It has a 6.5-inch screen."
- "It comes with free technical support."

**3. Specifications:**
- The [product] is [dimension/capacity/power].
- It measures [size].
- It weighs [weight].
- The capacity is [amount].

**Examples:**
- "The capacity is 500 liters."
- "It weighs only 2 kilograms."

**EXPLAINING BENEFITS (Why it's good!):**

**4. Features → Benefits:**
- This means (that)...
- This allows you to...
- This ensures (that)...
- Thanks to [feature], you can...

**Examples:**
- "It has a long battery life. This means you can use it all day without charging."
- "Thanks to cloud storage, you can access your files from anywhere."

**5. Highlighting Advantages:**
- The main advantage is...
- The key benefit is...
- What makes it special is...
- It's perfect/ideal for [purpose/user].

**Examples:**
- "The main advantage is its durability."
- "It's perfect for small businesses."

**MAKING COMPARISONS:**

**6. Comparing with Other Products:**
- Compared to [other product], this is [adjective + -er].
- Unlike [other product], this one [advantage].
- It's more [adjective] than [competitor].
- It's [adjective] + -er than competitors.

**Examples:**
- "Compared to our old model, this is 30% faster."
- "Unlike traditional software, this is cloud-based."
- "It's more affordable than similar products."

**7. Superlatives:**
- It's the [most adjective] product on the market.
- It's the [adjective + -est] in its category.

**Examples:**
- "It's the most reliable laptop available."
- "It's the fastest printer in its class."

**IMPORTANT VOCABULARY:**

**Features vs. Benefits:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember the difference:
- **FEATURE** = what the product HAS
  Example: 'It has a 48-megapixel camera.'
- **BENEFIT** = what the customer GETS (why it's useful)
  Example: 'You can take professional-quality photos.'

ALWAYS explain benefits, not just features! Customers care about benefits!"

**Useful Adjectives:**

Write on board:
- Quality: high-quality, reliable, durable, long-lasting
- Performance: efficient, powerful, fast, effective
- Design: innovative, modern, sleek, compact, lightweight
- Cost: affordable, cost-effective, economical
- Ease of use: user-friendly, easy to use, simple, intuitive

### **Practice:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll give you a product. You describe it using features AND benefits."

**Example:**
**Product:** A laptop with 10-hour battery life

**Teacher demonstrates:**
"This laptop has a 10-hour battery life. [FEATURE] This means you can work all day without needing to charge it. [BENEFIT] It's perfect for business travelers. [WHO IT'S FOR]"

**Now students practice:**
Give students these products to describe in pairs:
1. A smartphone with a waterproof design
2. Software with 24/7 customer support
3. A car with excellent fuel efficiency
4. Office furniture made from sustainable materials

*Give 5 minutes. Monitor and help.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent descriptions! Now let's practice with exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Feature or Benefit?**

**Instructions:**
Mark each sentence F (feature) or B (benefit):

1. ___ "It has a 2-year warranty."
2. ___ "This ensures peace of mind."
3. ___ "You can save up to 30% on energy costs."
4. ___ "It includes free shipping."
5. ___ "This allows you to work more efficiently."
6. ___ "The screen is 15 inches."
7. ___ "You'll never lose your data."
8. ___ "It comes with free training."

**Answers:** 1-F, 2-B, 3-B, 4-F, 5-B, 6-F, 7-B, 8-F

**Teacher Script:**
"Check with your partner. Then, for each FEATURE, create a BENEFIT sentence!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Example:**
- Feature: "It has a 2-year warranty."
- Benefit: "This means you're protected if anything goes wrong."

### **Exercise 2: Make Comparisons**

**Instructions:**
Complete the comparisons using the words in brackets:

1. Compared to our old model, this one is _______ (fast / 40%).
2. Unlike traditional phones, this device _______ (waterproof).
3. It's _______ than competitors' products (affordable).
4. This software is _______ user-friendly system on the market (most).

**Answers:**
1. 40% faster / much faster
2. is waterproof
3. more affordable
4. the most

**Teacher Script:**
"Complete the sentences with your partner!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

### **Exercise 3: Transform Features into Benefits**

**Instructions:**
Rewrite these feature statements to include benefits:

1. "It has 256GB of storage."  
   → "It has 256GB of storage, which means _______."

2. "The software is cloud-based."  
   → "The software is cloud-based. This allows _______."

3. "This machine uses 50% less energy."  
   → "This machine uses 50% less energy, so _______."

**Sample Answers:**
1. "...which means you can store thousands of files, photos, and videos."
2. "This allows you to access your data from anywhere, on any device."
3. "...so you'll save money on electricity bills."

*Give 4 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Product Presentation (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Perfect! Now you'll create and present your own product! Work in pairs. You'll invent a product, describe its features and benefits, and present it to the class!"

### **Instructions:**

**Step 1: Choose a Product Category (2 minutes)**

**Teacher Script:**
"In pairs, choose ONE of these product categories, or invent your own:"

1. A new smartphone or tablet
2. Business software or app
3. Office equipment (printer, coffee machine, desk, etc.)
4. A new service (delivery service, consulting, training, etc.)
5. Industrial equipment or machinery
6. A vehicle (car, truck, electric scooter, etc.)

**Step 2: Create Your Product (5 minutes)**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, invent your product! Give it a name. Decide:
- What are its key features? (at least 4-5 features)
- What are the benefits? (Why is it useful?)
- Who is it for? (target customers)
- How is it better than competitors?

Make notes – you'll present this to the class!"

*Students work in pairs. Monitor and help with vocabulary.*

**Step 3: Present Your Product (8 minutes total, ~2 min per pair)**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, each pair will present their product to the class! Imagine we are potential customers. You have 1-2 minutes to present. Include:
- Product name and what it is
- Key features
- Benefits (why we should buy it!)
- How it's better than competitors
- Who it's perfect for

First pair, you're up!"

*Each pair presents. Other students listen and can ask 1 question after each presentation.*

**Teacher provides feedback after each presentation:**
- Point out good use of language
- Correct 1-2 errors
- Ask a follow-up question to practice more

*After all presentations:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Excellent presentations! Now, if you could buy ONE product you heard about today, which would it be? Why?"

*Quick class discussion.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful product presentations! You all did a great job describing features and benefits!

Let's remember the FIVE KEY RULES for describing products:

1. **Start with who it's for** – 'This product is designed for busy professionals...'

2. **Describe features clearly** – Use specific numbers and details: '10-hour battery life,' '256GB storage'

3. **ALWAYS explain benefits** – Don't just say what it HAS, say what customers GET: 'This means you can...' / 'This allows you to...'

4. **Make comparisons** – Show how it's better: 'Unlike competitors...' / 'Compared to older models...'

5. **Use powerful adjectives** – reliable, efficient, innovative, cost-effective, user-friendly

Think: How can you describe YOUR company's products better? What will you change? 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Remember: customers don't buy features – they buy benefits! Always answer the question: 'Why should I buy this?'"
