# Business English Course for B1 Adult Learners
## Complete Course Overview & Index

**Course Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Number of Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Class Format:** Face-to-face (not online)  
**Lesson Duration:** 60–75 minutes each  
**Total Lessons:** 15

---

## 📋 Course Description

This comprehensive Business English course is designed for adult learners at B1 level who need to improve their English communication skills in real business and trade contexts. The course focuses on practical, real-world scenarios including meetings, negotiations, customer relations, and business travel.

### Main Course Goals:

✅ Improve English communication skills in authentic business contexts  
✅ Build confidence in speaking and listening  
✅ Prepare students for real-life professional scenarios  
✅ Ensure active participation with 70%+ student speaking time  
✅ Develop practical language skills for immediate workplace application

---

## 🎯 Teaching Methodology

### Lesson Structure (Applied to Every Lesson):

1. **Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)** – Activates prior knowledge and introduces topic
2. **Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5-7 min)** – Student-centered vocabulary activation
3. **Reading or Listening Input (10-12 min)** – Authentic business contexts with comprehension
4. **Language Focus (15 min)** – Key expressions, phrases, and practical grammar
5. **Guided Practice (10 min)** – Controlled exercises to internalize language
6. **Communicative Task/Role-play (15 min)** – Realistic business scenarios
7. **Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)** – Review, error correction, and application
8. **Homework (Optional)** – Extension activities for further practice

### Key Features:

- **Complete teacher scripts** for every stage
- **Pair work and group activities** in every lesson
- **Realistic role-plays** based on authentic business situations
- **Reading and listening texts** in each lesson
- **Practical focus** – no academic grammar exercises
- **Materials provided** – role cards, handouts, exercises
- **Timing breakdown** – ensures lessons fit 60-75 minute timeframe

## 📚 Complete Lesson Index

### **PART 1: Business Basics (Lessons 1-5)**

#### **Lesson 1: Introducing Yourself Professionally**
- **Focus:** Professional introductions, small talk, making first impressions
- **Key Language:** "I'm [name] from [company]", "We specialize in...", "Pleased to meet you"
- **Main Activities:** Networking role-play at trade fair
- **Real-world Application:** Trade fairs, conferences, first meetings with clients

#### **Lesson 2: Talking About Work & Responsibilities**
- **Focus:** Describing job duties, daily tasks, professional responsibilities
- **Key Language:** "I'm responsible for...", "My job involves...", "I deal with..."
- **Main Activities:** Job description role-plays, networking breakfast
- **Real-world Application:** Explaining your role to partners, colleagues, clients

#### **Lesson 3: Polite Requests & Apologies in Business**
- **Focus:** Making polite requests, asking for help, apologizing professionally
- **Key Language:** "Could you...", "Would you mind...", "I apologize for..."
- **Main Activities:** Email reading, request/apology role-plays
- **Real-world Application:** Email communication, workplace interactions

#### **Lesson 4: Making & Receiving Phone Calls**
- **Focus:** Professional telephone language, leaving messages, handling calls
- **Key Language:** "Could I speak to...", "Can I take a message?", "Let me confirm..."
- **Main Activities:** Phone conversation practice (back-to-back seating)
- **Real-world Application:** Business phone calls, taking/leaving messages

#### **Lesson 5: Arranging Meetings & Appointments**
- **Focus:** Scheduling meetings, suggesting times, confirming appointments, rescheduling
- **Key Language:** "Are you available...?", "I'm afraid I'm busy...", "Let me confirm..."
- **Main Activities:** Email negotiation, meeting scheduling role-plays
- **Real-world Application:** Setting up meetings with clients, colleagues, suppliers

---

### **PART 2: Business Travel & Trade (Lessons 6-10)**

#### **Lesson 6: At the Airport & Hotel**
- **Focus:** Travel vocabulary, check-in procedures, handling travel problems
- **Key Language:** "I have a flight to...", "I have a reservation under...", "What time is checkout?"
- **Main Activities:** Airport and hotel check-in scenarios, problem situations
- **Real-world Application:** Business travel, staying in hotels abroad

#### **Lesson 7: Dining with Clients & Small Talk**
- **Focus:** Restaurant language, making small talk, socializing with business contacts
- **Key Language:** "I can recommend...", "How was your flight?", small talk topics
- **Main Activities:** Business dinner simulation, conversation practice
- **Real-world Application:** Client dinners, networking events, relationship building

#### **Lesson 8: Shopping & Negotiating Prices**
- **Focus:** Shopping for business, negotiating prices, discussing discounts
- **Key Language:** "Could you give me a quote?", "Would it be possible to offer a discount?", "If you can..."
- **Main Activities:** Price negotiation role-plays with different scenarios
- **Real-world Application:** Purchasing for company, negotiating with suppliers

#### **Lesson 9: Giving Directions & Explaining Locations**
- **Focus:** Giving and understanding directions, explaining locations, prepositions of place
- **Key Language:** "Turn left/right", "It's opposite...", "Walk straight until..."
- **Main Activities:** Direction-giving practice, map activities
- **Real-world Application:** Helping visitors, finding locations, explaining routes

#### **Lesson 10: Handling Problems Abroad**
- **Focus:** Solving travel problems, reporting issues, asking for help, staying calm
- **Key Language:** "I have a problem", "What should I do?", "This is urgent"
- **Main Activities:** Problem-solving scenarios (lost luggage, delays, mistakes)
- **Real-world Application:** Managing travel emergencies, handling unexpected situations

---

### **PART 3: Workplace Communication (Lessons 11-15)**

#### **Lesson 11: Writing Professional Emails**
- **Focus:** Email structure, formal vs. informal tone, common business email types
- **Key Language:** Email greetings, openings, closings, "I am writing to...", "I look forward to..."
- **Main Activities:** Email analysis, writing practice with peer review
- **Real-world Application:** Daily business correspondence

#### **Lesson 12: Participating in Meetings (Opinions, Agreeing/Disagreeing)**
- **Focus:** Expressing opinions, agreeing/disagreeing politely, participating actively
- **Key Language:** "I think...", "I see your point, but...", "I completely agree"
- **Main Activities:** Meeting simulations with decision-making tasks
- **Real-world Application:** Team meetings, client meetings, project discussions

#### **Lesson 13: Describing Products & Services**
- **Focus:** Presenting products/services, describing features and benefits, making comparisons
- **Key Language:** "It has...", "This means you can...", "Compared to...", "It's perfect for..."
- **Main Activities:** Product invention and presentation to class
- **Real-world Application:** Sales presentations, product demonstrations, client pitches

#### **Lesson 14: Customer Service & Handling Complaints**
- **Focus:** Dealing with complaints, providing solutions, staying professional under pressure
- **Key Language:** "I understand your frustration", "I sincerely apologize", "Let me help you"
- **Main Activities:** Customer complaint role-plays with various scenarios
- **Real-world Application:** Customer service situations, managing difficult clients

#### **Lesson 15: Final Integration Lesson – Full Business Trip Role-play**
- **Focus:** Integrating all skills from Lessons 1-14 in comprehensive simulation
- **Key Activities:** 
  - Arrivals and introductions
  - Business meetings and presentations
  - Negotiations
  - Problem-solving
  - Business dinner
- **Real-world Application:** Complete business trip experience from start to finish

## 🌟 Teaching Tips for Maximum Success

### 1. **Create a Supportive Environment**
- Encourage risk-taking and experimentation
- Normalize mistakes as part of learning
- Celebrate progress and effort
- Build a community of learners

### 2. **Maximize Speaking Time**
- Use pair work extensively
- Monitor without interrupting
- Note errors for later correction
- Give clear time limits for activities

### 3. **Make It Relevant**
- Connect to students' real jobs when possible
- Invite students to share their experiences
- Use company-specific examples if teaching in-house
- Assign homework based on real work tasks

### 4. **Manage Mixed Abilities**
- Pair stronger with weaker students sometimes
- Provide extra support to struggling learners
- Challenge advanced students with extension questions
- Differentiate homework options

### 5. **Cultural Sensitivity**
- Be aware of cultural differences in business practices
- Discuss how business customs vary across cultures
- Respect different communication styles
- Adapt scenarios to students' cultural contexts

## 💡 Homework Philosophy

Homework is **optional** in this course because:
- Adult learners have busy schedules
- Primary learning happens in class
- Homework should reinforce, not burden

**However**, homework greatly enhances learning. Each lesson offers 2-3 homework options:
- **Writing tasks** (for those who want writing practice)
- **Speaking tasks** (for those who want fluency practice)
- **Real-world tasks** (for those who want immediate application)

**Recommendation:** Encourage at least 15-20 minutes of homework per lesson.

## ✅ Quick Start Guide for Teachers

### Before Starting the Course:
1. Read through all 15 lesson plans
2. Prepare role cards and handouts for Lesson 1
3. Set up classroom for pair/group work
4. Introduce yourself and course objectives to students
5. Conduct a brief needs analysis (optional)

### For Each Lesson:
1. Review the lesson plan 24 hours in advance
2. Prepare all materials and role cards
3. Practice reading the listening texts aloud
4. Arrange classroom furniture appropriately
5. Have a timer ready for activities

### During Each Lesson:
1. Follow the teacher scripts (but adapt to your style)
2. Monitor student speaking time (aim for 70%+)
3. Take notes on errors for wrap-up stage
4. Be flexible with timing (some activities may run long/short)
5. End on time and assign homework

### After Each Lesson:
1. Reflect on what went well and what to improve
2. Note any students who need extra support
3. Prepare materials for next lesson
4. Follow up with absent students if necessary

## 🌍 Adaptations for Different Contexts

### In-Company Training:
- Use company-specific examples and vocabulary
- Incorporate real company documents
- Invite different departments to share their work
- Connect to company's international business needs

### Language Schools:
- Mix students from different industries
- Focus on transferable skills
- Use generic business scenarios
- Encourage networking among students

### Online Adaptation (if needed):
- Use breakout rooms for pair/group work
- Use screen share for reading texts
- Utilize online whiteboards for brainstorming
- Email role cards before class
- Use chat for written exercises

### Intensive Courses:
- Teach 2 lessons per day
- Include longer breaks between lessons
- Add review sessions after Lessons 5 and 10
- Extend final simulation to 90-120 minutes
