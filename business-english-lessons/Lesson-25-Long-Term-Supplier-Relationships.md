# Lesson 25: Long-term Supplier Relationships

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Building trust, maintaining relationships, polite follow-up, resolving issues diplomatically

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome! Today we're discussing long-term supplier relationships – essential for sustainable business. A good supplier partnership is built on trust, communication, and mutual respect. Let's explore how to build and maintain these relationships!"

**Warm-up Questions:**
1. Do you work with long-term suppliers? How long have you worked together?
2. What makes a good supplier relationship?
3. Have you ever had problems with a supplier? How did you resolve them?
4. What's more important: lowest price or reliable relationship?

*Discuss 4 minutes, share responses.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

*Brainstorm: reliability, consistency, trust, partnership, mutual benefit, transparency, communication, quality, delivery times, payment terms, problem resolution, etc.*

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### **Reading Text:**

**Building a Strong Supplier Partnership: A Case Study**

Elena manages purchasing for a fashion brand. Five years ago, she found a fabric supplier in Vietnam – Green Textiles. Today, they're her most trusted partner. Here's how she built the relationship:

**Year 1: Building Trust**
- Started with small orders to test quality
- Communicated clearly about expectations
- Paid on time every time
- Gave honest feedback about products
- Visited their factory

**Communication she used:**
"We're very pleased with the quality. We'd like to increase our order for next season."
"We noticed a small issue with batch #045. Can we discuss how to prevent this in future?"

**Years 2-3: Growing Partnership**
- Gradually increased order sizes
- Introduced them to other departments
- Gave advance notice of upcoming needs
- Helped them understand her market
- Negotiated better prices based on loyalty

**Key phrase:**
"We value our partnership. As we're ordering more regularly now, could we discuss volume discounts?"

**Years 4-5: Strong Partnership**
- Green Textiles prioritizes her orders
- They notify her early about supply issues
- They offer her first access to new fabrics
- They're flexible on payment terms
- They collaborate on product development

**When problems occurred:**
"I understand there's a delay due to factory maintenance. Thanks for informing us early. Can you confirm the new delivery date? We'll adjust our production schedule."

**The relationship works because:**
- **Elena:** Communicates clearly, pays promptly, treats them with respect, gives advance notice, shows loyalty
- **Green Textiles:** Delivers consistently, communicates proactively, maintains quality, offers good service, values the partnership

**Results:**
- Reliable supply for 5 years
- Better prices than competitors get
- Priority treatment
- Innovation collaboration
- Mutual business growth

### **Comprehension:**

1. How did Elena build trust in Year 1?
2. What communication style does she use?
3. How did the relationship evolve over 5 years?
4. What do both sides gain from this partnership?

*Discuss 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### **Key Expressions:**

**BUILDING THE RELATIONSHIP:**

**1. Expressing Satisfaction:**
- "We're very pleased with [product/service]."
- "The quality has been consistently excellent."
- "We appreciate your reliability."
- "We value our partnership."

**2. Giving Constructive Feedback:**
- "Overall the quality is good, but we noticed..."
- "Could we discuss improving [aspect]?"
- "We'd like to see better [X] in future orders."
- "I wanted to bring to your attention..."

**3. Building Loyalty:**
- "We're looking to build a long-term relationship."
- "We see you as a key partner."
- "We prefer working with trusted suppliers."
- "We'd like to expand our business together."

**MAINTAINING COMMUNICATION:**

**4. Regular Check-ins:**
- "I wanted to touch base about..."
- "Just checking in on the current order..."
- "How are things progressing with...?"
- "I wanted to update you on our plans..."

**5. Advance Planning:**
- "I wanted to give you advance notice that..."
- "Looking ahead to next quarter..."
- "We anticipate needing..."
- "To help you plan ahead..."

**6. Showing Appreciation:**
- "Thank you for prioritizing our order."
- "We really appreciate your flexibility."
- "Thanks for going the extra mile."
- "Your quick response was very helpful."

**HANDLING PROBLEMS:**

**7. Addressing Issues Diplomatically:**
- "We've noticed a concern that we'd like to discuss..."
- "Can we talk about how to prevent this in future?"
- "I wanted to bring this to your attention..."
- "Let's work together to resolve this."

**8. Understanding Their Challenges:**
- "I understand you're facing challenges with..."
- "We appreciate you keeping us informed."
- "How can we support you during this time?"
- "Let's find a solution that works for both of us."

**9. Resolving Issues:**
- "How can we address this together?"
- "What would be a fair resolution?"
- "Could we agree on...?"
- "Let's find a middle ground."

**NEGOTIATING AS PARTNERS:**

**10. Long-term Negotiation:**
- "Given our growing partnership..."
- "As a loyal customer..."
- "We've been working together for [time]..."
- "Could we discuss better terms for continued partnership?"

### **Practice Dialogues:**

**Dialogue 1: Regular Check-in**
**Buyer:** Hi Ming, I wanted to touch base about our current order. How's production going?  
**Supplier:** It's going well. We're on schedule for delivery on the 15th.  
**Buyer:** That's great to hear. Also, I wanted to give you advance notice – we're planning a larger order for Q4. About 30% more than usual.  
**Supplier:** Thank you for the advance notice! That's very helpful for our planning. Let's schedule a call next month to discuss details?  
**Buyer:** Perfect. I really appreciate our partnership, Ming.

**Dialogue 2: Handling a Problem**
**Buyer:** Sofia, we received the shipment, and we noticed some quality issues with batch #234. About 5% of the items don't meet our standards.  
**Supplier:** I apologize for that. Can you send me photos so we can investigate?  
**Buyer:** Absolutely, I'll email them today. I know this isn't typical of your quality. How should we handle this?  
**Supplier:** We can send replacement items by next week, and I'll personally inspect them before shipping. We'll also investigate how this happened to prevent it in future.  
**Buyer:** That sounds like a fair resolution. Thank you for addressing this quickly. We value our relationship and I'm confident this was just a one-off issue.

*Practice in pairs – 5 minutes.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### **Exercise: Communication Scenarios**

What would you say in these situations?

1. You've been working with a supplier for 3 years. Quality has always been excellent. You want to negotiate a 10% discount for loyalty.

2. Your supplier informed you about a 2-week delivery delay due to equipment problems. You're understanding but need to know the impact on your business.

3. You're very satisfied with your supplier and want to give positive feedback.

4. Your supplier went above and beyond to rush an urgent order. You want to show appreciation.

*Discuss and role-play in pairs – 10 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Supplier Relationship Scenarios (15 min)**

### **Relationship Scenario Cards:**

**Scenario 1: Building Trust (Year 1)**
**A (Buyer):** You found a new supplier. Place your first order (small test). Be professional, state expectations clearly.  
**B (Supplier):** New customer. Show reliability, ask about their needs, deliver good service.

**Scenario 2: Growing Partnership (Year 2)**
**A (Buyer):** You're happy with supplier. Increase order size, ask about volume discounts.  
**B (Supplier):** Valued customer. Offer better terms to encourage loyalty.

**Scenario 3: Problem Resolution**
**A (Buyer):** 10% of last shipment was defective. Address diplomatically – you value the relationship.  
**B (Supplier):** This is unusual. Apologize, investigate, offer solution, maintain relationship.

**Scenario 4: Long-term Partnership**
**A (Buyer):** 5-year partnership. Give advance notice of big Q4 order, discuss new product collaboration.  
**B (Supplier):** Loyal customer. Show appreciation, prioritize their needs, propose innovation.

*Students do 2-3 scenarios – 12 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent relationship building!

**KEYS TO LONG-TERM SUPPLIER RELATIONSHIPS:**

1. **Communication** – Regular, honest, proactive
2. **Reliability** – Pay on time, honor commitments
3. **Respect** – Treat them as partners, not just vendors
4. **Transparency** – Share forecasts, be open about needs
5. **Problem-solving** – Address issues together, not blame
6. **Loyalty** – Don't switch for small price differences
7. **Appreciation** – Acknowledge good service

Remember: A good supplier is a business asset. Invest in the relationship!"
