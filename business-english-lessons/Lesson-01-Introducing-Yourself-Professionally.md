# Lesson 1: Introducing Yourself Professionally

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Professional introductions, small talk, making first impressions

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon, everyone! Welcome to today's lesson. Today we're going to learn how to introduce ourselves in professional situations. Let me ask you some questions to start. Think about your answers, then I'll ask you to discuss with a partner."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. When was the last time you met someone new in a business situation?
2. What information do you usually give when you introduce yourself at work?
3. Do you find it easy or difficult to introduce yourself in English? Why?
4. What's the difference between introducing yourself to a friend and introducing yourself in business?

**Instructions:**
"Now, discuss these questions with your partner for 3 minutes. Partner A, start with question 1. Partner B, listen and ask follow-up questions. Then swap."

*After 3 minutes:*
"Okay, let's hear some of your answers. Maria, what did you and your partner talk about?"

*Take 2-3 responses from different pairs.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good! Now, let's think about professional introductions. I'm going to write 'BUSINESS INTRODUCTIONS' on the board. In pairs, you have 2 minutes to write down as many words or phrases related to introducing yourself in business. Think about: what information you give, what questions you ask, where these introductions happen."

*Write "BUSINESS INTRODUCTIONS" on the board. Give 2 minutes.*

"Okay, stop! Let's see what you have. Pair 1, give me one word or phrase."

*Collect vocabulary on board. Expected answers: name, company, job title, handshake, nice to meet you, pleased to meet you, business card, conference, networking, colleague, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What about your job? What do you say?"
- "Where do these introductions happen?"
- "What do you give someone when you meet them?"

---

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's read a short text about two businesspeople meeting for the first time. This is at a trade fair in London. Please read silently."

### **Reading Text:**

**Meeting at a Trade Fair**

Sarah Chen is a sales manager from Taiwan. She works for TechPro Solutions, a company that sells computer equipment. Today she's at the International Technology Fair in London. She wants to meet new customers and partners.

At the networking coffee break, Sarah sees a man standing alone. She walks over and smiles. "Good morning. I'm Sarah Chen from TechPro Solutions. We specialize in computer equipment for offices."

The man shakes her hand. "Pleased to meet you, Sarah. I'm David Miller from Office Supplies UK. We're always looking for good technology partners."

Sarah gives him her business card. "Great! Here's my card. What exactly does your company do?"

David explains, "We sell office furniture and equipment to businesses across the UK. Your products sound interesting. Do you have a catalog I could look at?"

Sarah nods. "Yes, of course. Our stand is number 47. Would you like to come over and see our latest products?"

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, let's check your understanding. Answer these questions with your partner."

1. Where are Sarah and David?
2. What does Sarah's company sell?
3. What does David's company do?
4. What does Sarah give to David?

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**
"Good! Now discuss these questions:"

1. Why is it important to introduce yourself well in business?
2. Have you ever been to a trade fair or business event? What happened?
3. What do you think Sarah and David will do next?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent discussion! Now let's look at some key phrases for professional introductions. We use these to introduce ourselves, ask about someone's work, and show interest."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**1. Introducing yourself:**
- "I'm [name] from [company]."
- "I work for [company]."
- "I'm a [job title] at [company]."

**Examples:**
- "I'm Maria Santos from Global Trading."
- "I work for Samsung Electronics."
- "I'm a marketing manager at ABC Corporation."

**2. Talking about your company:**
- "We specialize in [product/service]."
- "We sell/produce/provide [product/service]."
- "We're based in [location]."

**Examples:**
- "We specialize in software development."
- "We produce medical equipment."

**3. Showing interest:**
- "Pleased to meet you."
- "Nice to meet you."
- "What exactly does your company do?"
- "That sounds interesting."

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now let's practice! Here's a short dialogue. I'll read Person A, and you all read Person B together first."

**Person A:** Good morning. I'm Carlos Rodriguez from Import Export Solutions.  
**Person B:** Nice to meet you, Carlos. I'm Anna Lee. I work for Fashion Direct.  
**Person A:** Pleased to meet you, Anna. What exactly does your company do?  
**Person B:** We sell clothing to retail stores. We're based in Hong Kong. And your company?  
**Person A:** We specialize in helping companies import products from Asia.  
**Person B:** That sounds interesting!

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice this dialogue in pairs. Partner A reads A, Partner B reads B. Then swap roles."

*Give 2 minutes.*

"Excellent! Now, change the dialogue. Use your own name, company, and job. Practice with your partner."

*Give 3-4 minutes. Monitor and help.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great work! Now let's practice the language more. Complete these sentences with the correct words."

### **Gap-Fill Activity:**

**Instructions (on board or handout):**
Complete the sentences with: *specialize / based / pleased / exactly / work for / business card*

1. "Nice to meet you. I'm John Smith. I _______ Microsoft."
2. "_______ to meet you, John."
3. "Our company is _______ in Frankfurt, Germany."
4. "We _______ in medical equipment."
5. "What _______ does your company do?"
6. "Here's my _______. Please contact me anytime."

**Answers:** 1. work for, 2. Pleased, 3. based, 4. specialize, 5. exactly, 6. business card

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's check together. Number 1... Paulo, what's the answer?"

*Check answers together.*

### **Sentence Completion:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, complete these sentences about YOU and your company. Use full sentences."

1. I work for _______.
2. We specialize in _______.
3. We're based in _______.
4. My job title is _______.

**Teacher Script:**
"Okay, ask your partner these questions and listen to their full answers. Partner A starts: 'Who do you work for?' Partner B answers with a full sentence."

*Monitor and correct errors.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now it's time for a real role-play. You are all at an international business conference. You need to walk around, meet new people, and introduce yourself. I'll give each of you a role card."

### **Role Cards:**

**Prepare 8 cards with different roles:**

**Card 1:** You're a sales manager from a car manufacturing company in Germany.  
**Card 2:** You're a marketing director from a coffee export company in Brazil.  
**Card 3:** You're a purchasing manager from a clothing retail company in the UK.  
**Card 4:** You're a CEO from a software development company in India.  
**Card 5:** You're a logistics coordinator from a shipping company in Singapore.  
**Card 6:** You're a product designer from a furniture company in Italy.  
**Card 7:** You're a business consultant from a consulting firm in the USA.  
**Card 8:** You're an operations manager from a food processing company in Thailand.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Everyone, stand up! You're at a networking coffee break at a big conference. Walk around, meet at least 3 different people. Introduce yourself, ask about their company, exchange information. Remember to use the phrases we learned today. You have 8 minutes. Go!"

*Monitor the activity. Listen for good language use and errors.*

*After 8 minutes:*
"Okay, please sit down. Great work! Let me ask: Who did you meet? What do their companies do? Ana, tell us about someone you met."

*Take 2-3 responses.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"You all did an excellent job today! I heard many of you using our key phrases. For example, I heard Marco say 'We specialize in furniture design' – perfect! I also heard Julia say 'Pleased to meet you' – very professional!

Let me correct two things I noticed:
1. Remember, we say 'I work FOR a company,' not 'I work IN a company.'
2. When someone gives you their business card, say 'Thank you' and look at it. Don't put it away immediately – that's polite.

Now, think about this question: How can you use today's language in your real business life? Discuss with your partner."

*Give 1 minute for discussion.*

"Good! Remember, a strong introduction creates a good first impression. Practice these phrases this week!"

---

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

### Teacher Script:
"For homework, I have two options. Choose one:

**Option 1 (Writing):** Write an email introducing yourself to a new business contact. Write 80-100 words. Include: your name, company, job, what your company does, and why you're writing.

**Option 2 (Speaking):** Record yourself (on your phone) introducing yourself in a business situation. Speak for 1 minute. Include all the key information we practiced today.

Bring your homework next lesson. Any questions?"
