# Lesson 30: Final Business Trip Simulation (Advanced)

**Level:** B2 (Upper Intermediate - Advanced)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Comprehensive integration of Lessons 1-29 – complete international business trip

---

## **Lesson Overview**

This is the culminating lesson for Lessons 1-30. Students integrate ALL skills learned in a complex, realistic business trip simulation involving multiple countries, cultural challenges, and business tasks.

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Course Review (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Congratulations! You've completed 29 lessons! Today is your final integration. You'll experience a complete international business trip involving: travel, cultural navigation, presentations, negotiations, problem-solving, and relationship building. This brings together everything we've learned!"

**Quick Review (call out answers):**
- "How do you introduce yourself professionally?" (Lesson 1)
- "How do you handle a customer complaint?" (Lessons 14, 27)
- "How do you negotiate prices?" (Lesson 8)
- "What do you do if there's a cultural misunderstanding?" (Lessons 17, 29)

"Great! You remember! Now let's put it all into practice!"

---

## **Stage 2: Simulation Setup (5 min)**

### **The Scenario:**

**Your Company:** Premium Fashion Imports (UK-based)  
**Your Role:** Sourcing Manager  
**Your Mission:** 10-day business trip to Asia and Morocco to:
1. Meet textile suppliers in China
2. Visit fabric factory in Vietnam
3. Negotiate with packaging supplier in Morocco
4. Present new collection to Middle Eastern client in Dubai

**Challenges you'll face:**
- Travel problems
- Cultural differences
- Negotiations
- Presentations
- Complaints
- Tight deadlines

---

## **Stage 3: Simulation Phase 1 – China (12 min)**

### **Day 1-3: Shanghai, China**

**Teacher Script:**
"You're in Shanghai to meet textile suppliers. Remember Chinese business culture from Lesson 29!"

**Scenario A: Airport Problem**
Your luggage with product samples was lost. You have a supplier meeting in 3 hours.
*Student handles this crisis (Lesson 23)*

**Scenario B: Supplier Meeting**
Meet with Li Wei from Shanghai Textiles. They're showing you their new sustainable fabric line.
- Navigate indirect communication
- Understand "face" concept
- Build relationship (guanxi)
- Negotiate prices
- Handle cultural expectations

**Role-play cards:** Student A = You, Student B = Li Wei

*8 minutes for scenarios*

"What cultural adaptations did you make? What was challenging?"

---

## **Stage 4: Simulation Phase 2 – Vietnam (10 min)**

### **Day 4-5: Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam**

**Scenario A: Factory Visit**
Visit textile factory. Give feedback diplomatically about quality issues you noticed (Lesson 19).

**Scenario B: Trade Fair**
Attend Vietnam Textile Fair. Network with potential suppliers (Lesson 22).
- Start conversations
- Qualify suppliers
- Exchange information
- Follow up

**Role-play:** Students walk around room, networking with multiple "suppliers"

*10 minutes*

---

## **Stage 5: Simulation Phase 3 – Morocco (12 min)**

### **Day 6-7: Casablanca, Morocco**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now Morocco! Remember Middle Eastern business culture – relationship first!"

**Scenario A: Client Dinner**
Your Moroccan packaging supplier invites you to dinner. Navigate hospitality customs, small talk, and business discussion (Lesson 7).

**Scenario B: Price Negotiation**
Supplier quoted $5,000. You have $4,000 budget. Negotiate while respecting relationship (Lesson 8, 29).
- Be patient
- Build trust
- Find win-win solution
- Respect cultural approach

**Role-play pairs:** Student A = You, Student B = Moroccan supplier

*12 minutes*

"How was this negotiation different from China?"

---

## **Stage 6: Simulation Phase 4 – Dubai (10 min)**

### **Day 8-9: Dubai, UAE**

**Scenario: Client Presentation**
Present your new fabric collection to Al-Rashid Fashion Group (major client).

**Requirements:**
- Professional presentation structure (Lesson 21)
- Describe fabric qualities (Lesson 28)
- Handle questions
- Navigate Arab business culture
- Close the deal

**Each student presents 2 minutes to the "client" (other students)**

*10 minutes total*

---

## **Stage 7: Simulation Phase 5 – Crisis Management (10 min)**

### **Day 10: Return Journey**

**Multiple Challenges Hit Simultaneously:**

**Problem 1:** Client from Dubai just called – they received samples and found quality issues. They're considering canceling the order.
*Handle complaint professionally (Lesson 27)*

**Problem 2:** Chinese supplier emailed – they need to increase prices by 15% due to material costs.
*Negotiate solution (Lesson 8)*

**Problem 3:** You're at airport. Flight cancelled. You have critical meeting tomorrow.
*Solve travel crisis (Lesson 23)*

**Problem 4:** Your boss emails asking for complete trip report by tomorrow.
*Plan and prioritize (Lesson 20)*

**Students work in small groups to solve all 4 problems simultaneously**

*10 minutes*

"How did you prioritize? What was most urgent?"

---

## **Stage 8: Debrief & Reflection (7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Outstanding work! You handled a complex international business trip!

**You successfully:**
✅ Navigated 4 different cultures  
✅ Solved travel crises  
✅ Conducted negotiations  
✅ Built relationships  
✅ Gave presentations  
✅ Handled complaints  
✅ Managed time pressure  
✅ Stayed professional throughout

**Reflection Questions:**
1. What was most challenging?
2. What skills from Lessons 1-29 were most useful?
3. What cultural awareness helped you?
4. What would you do differently?
5. How confident do you feel now for real international business?

*Students discuss in pairs 2 minutes, then share with class.*

**Teacher's Final Message:**
"You started Lesson 1 learning basic introductions. Now you've completed a complex international business trip in English! This shows your incredible progress!

Remember these lessons as you continue your business English journey. You have the skills, vocabulary, and confidence to succeed in international business.

**Key Takeaways from 30 Lessons:**
1. Preparation is everything
2. Cultural awareness is as important as language
3. Professional relationships matter more than transactions
4. Clear communication solves most problems
5. Stay calm and professional under pressure

**Congratulations on completing Lessons 1-30!** You're now ready for advanced management and strategic communication in Lessons 31-45!"

## **Teacher Notes:**

**This lesson is the culmination of 30 lessons.** It should feel celebratory while also challenging. Students should leave feeling accomplished and confident.

**Adaptations:**
- **For smaller groups:** Combine some roles or phases
- **For shorter time:** Focus on 2-3 countries instead of 4
- **For specific industries:** Adapt products/scenarios to students' actual work

**Assessment Opportunity:**
This lesson provides excellent opportunity to assess students' progress across all skill areas. Take notes on individual performance for feedback or certificates.

**Celebration:**
Consider ending with certificates of completion for Lessons 1-30, or a class discussion about favorite lessons and biggest improvements.
