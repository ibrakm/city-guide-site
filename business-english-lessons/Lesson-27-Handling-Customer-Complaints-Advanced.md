# Lesson 27: Handling Customer Complaints (Advanced)

**Level:** B2 (Upper Intermediate - Advanced)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Complex complaints, angry customers, service recovery, turning complaints into opportunities

**Note:** Builds on Lesson 14 with more advanced scenarios.

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome! We covered basic complaint handling in Lesson 14. Today we tackle advanced scenarios: very angry customers, unreasonable demands, and complex problems."

**Warm-up Questions:**
1. Have you dealt with an extremely angry customer? How did it go?
2. What do you do when a customer's request is unreasonable?
3. How do you stay calm when customers are aggressive?
4. Can complaints be opportunities?

*Discuss 4 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 2-8: [Following standard structure]**

### **Key Language:**

**ADVANCED COMPLAINT HANDLING:**

**1. Dealing with Very Angry Customers:**
- "I can hear you're very frustrated. Let me help."
- "I completely understand why you're upset."
- "Let me make this right for you."
- "I'm going to personally handle this."

**2. When You Can't Meet Demands:**
- "I understand what you're asking, but what I can do is..."
- "That's outside our policy, but here's what I can offer..."
- "While I can't [X], I can [Y]."

**3. De-escalation:**
- "Let's take this one step at a time..."
- "Help me understand exactly what happened..."
- "I'm on your side. Let's solve this together."

**4. Service Recovery:**
- "We failed your expectations. Here's how we'll make it right..."
- "This isn't the service you deserve. Let me fix this."
- "You're a valued customer. We'll ensure this doesn't happen again."

**Role-play Scenarios:**
- Customer received wrong product 3 times
- Customer demanding refund outside policy
- Customer threatening social media complaints
- VIP customer with major issue

*Advanced role-plays – 15 minutes.*
