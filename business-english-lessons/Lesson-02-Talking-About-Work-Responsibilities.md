# Lesson 2: Talking About Work & Responsibilities

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Describing job duties, daily tasks, and professional responsibilities

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon! Welcome back! Today we're going to talk about our work and responsibilities. Let's start with some warm-up questions. Think about your answers first."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. What do you do in your job every day?
2. What's the most important part of your job?
3. What do you enjoy most about your work?
4. What's the most challenging thing you do at work?

**Instructions:**
"Now, discuss these questions in pairs. You have 4 minutes. Partner A, start with question 1 and 2. Partner B, answer questions 3 and 4 first. Then swap!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear from some of you. Roberto, what's the most important part of your job?"

*Take 3-4 responses from different students.*

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now, I'm going to write 'WORK RESPONSIBILITIES' on the board. In pairs, think about different jobs and what people do. Brainstorm as many work activities and responsibilities as you can. For example: answer emails, manage a team, make phone calls. You have 2 minutes!"

*Write "WORK RESPONSIBILITIES" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Stop! Let's collect your ideas. Team 1, give me one."

*Collect vocabulary on board. Expected answers: answer emails, attend meetings, prepare reports, manage staff, deal with customers, handle complaints, make presentations, organize schedules, supervise, negotiate, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do managers do?"
- "What about people who work with customers?"
- "What about administrative tasks?"

---

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent vocabulary! Now, let's listen to three people talking about their jobs. I'll read each description twice. Listen and take notes."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Person 1 – Elena**
"I'm Elena and I'm a project manager at a construction company. My main responsibility is to make sure projects finish on time and within budget. Every day, I check the progress of different building sites. I also manage a team of ten engineers and workers. I'm responsible for solving problems when they happen. Sometimes I negotiate with suppliers to get better prices for materials. I also prepare weekly reports for my boss."

**Person 2 – David**
"My name's David and I work as a customer service representative for an airline. I'm in charge of helping passengers with their problems and questions. I answer phone calls and emails from customers every day. When there's a flight delay or cancellation, I have to find solutions quickly. I also deal with complaints and try to make unhappy customers satisfied. Sometimes I process refunds or help people change their bookings."

**Person 3 – Yuki**
"I'm Yuki, and I'm an export coordinator for a tea company. My job involves organizing shipments to different countries. I'm responsible for all the export documentation – invoices, certificates, customs forms. I also communicate with overseas customers and answer their questions. Every week, I attend meetings with our sales team to discuss new orders. I make sure everything is ready before the products leave our warehouse."

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, discuss these questions with your partner:"

1. What does Elena do? What are her main responsibilities?
2. What problems does David solve?
3. What documents does Yuki prepare?
4. Which job sounds the most interesting to you? Why?

*Give 3 minutes for pair discussion.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

"Now discuss:"
1. How is your job similar to or different from these three jobs?
2. Do you prefer working with people or working with documents? Why?
3. What responsibilities do you have in your job?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now let's look at some key language for describing our work responsibilities. We'll learn different ways to talk about what we do."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**1. Talking about your main responsibility:**
- "I'm responsible for [noun/-ing]"
- "My main responsibility is to [verb]"
- "I'm in charge of [noun/-ing]"

**Examples:**
- "I'm responsible for customer service."
- "My main responsibility is to manage the team."
- "I'm in charge of organizing events."

**2. Describing daily/regular tasks:**
- "Every day, I [verb]"
- "I [verb] on a daily basis"
- "My job involves [verb+-ing]"

**Examples:**
- "Every day, I answer customer emails."
- "I check inventory on a daily basis."
- "My job involves meeting new clients."

**3. Talking about what you do:**
- "I deal with [noun]"
- "I handle [noun]"
- "I take care of [noun]"

**Examples:**
- "I deal with customer complaints."
- "I handle all the export documents."
- "I take care of the company's social media."

### **Grammar Note:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Notice: After 'responsible for,' 'in charge of,' and 'involved in,' we use the -ING form, not infinitive.
- CORRECT: I'm responsible for managING the budget.
- WRONG: I'm responsible for TO MANAGE the budget."

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now let's practice! Read this dialogue with me first."

**Person A:** So, what exactly do you do at your company?  
**Person B:** I'm a logistics coordinator. I'm responsible for organizing deliveries.  
**Person A:** That sounds busy! What does that involve?  
**Person B:** Well, every day I contact suppliers and check delivery schedules. I also handle all the shipping documents.  
**Person A:** And do you work with customers?  
**Person B:** Yes, sometimes I deal with customer questions about their orders.

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice this in pairs. Then, change it to talk about YOUR real job. Use the phrases we learned."

*Give 4-5 minutes. Monitor and assist.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's practice with some exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Complete the Sentences**

**Instructions:**
Choose the correct word: *responsible / involves / deal / in charge / handle*

1. I'm _______ for training new employees.
2. My job _______ traveling to different countries.
3. I _______ with customer complaints every day.
4. She's _______ of the marketing department.
5. Can you _______ this problem for me?

**Answers:** 1. responsible, 2. involves, 3. deal, 4. in charge, 5. handle

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's check together. Number 1, what's the answer?"

*Check all answers together.*

### **Exercise 2: Sentence Building**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, make true sentences about YOUR job. Use these prompts:"

1. I'm responsible for _______.
2. Every day, I _______.
3. My job involves _______.
4. I deal with _______.

**Teacher Script:**
"Write your answers, then share with your partner. Ask your partner: 'What are you responsible for?' Listen carefully to their full answer."

*Give 4 minutes. Monitor and note good examples and errors.*

**Teacher asks follow-up questions:**
"Maria, what are you responsible for in your job?"
"Tom, what does your job involve?"

*Get 3-4 students to share with the whole class.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now we're going to do a networking activity. Imagine you're at a business breakfast meeting. You meet new people and talk about your work. I'll give you role cards."

### **Role Cards:**

**Prepare 8 cards:**

**Card 1:** Sales Manager – You manage 5 salespeople. Responsible for meeting monthly sales targets. Deal with key customers.

**Card 2:** HR Officer – In charge of recruitment. Interview candidates. Handle employee problems and questions.

**Card 3:** Accountant – Responsible for company finances. Prepare monthly financial reports. Deal with invoices and payments.

**Card 4:** Warehouse Supervisor – Manage 8 warehouse workers. In charge of inventory. Make sure orders are packed correctly.

**Card 5:** IT Support Specialist – Handle technical problems. Install new software. Take care of the company's computer network.

**Card 6:** Marketing Coordinator – Responsible for social media. Organize promotional events. Create marketing materials.

**Card 7:** Quality Control Inspector – Check products before shipping. Deal with quality problems. Prepare quality reports.

**Card 8:** Executive Assistant – Manage boss's schedule. Organize meetings. Handle business travel arrangements.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Everyone, take a role card. This is your job for today. You're at a business breakfast. Walk around and talk to 3-4 different people. Ask about their job, tell them about yours. Use the phrases we learned. You have 10 minutes!"

*Students mingle and talk.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Okay, sit down please. Great conversations! Let me ask: Whose job sounded the most interesting? Why? Who has a lot of responsibility?"

*Take 3-4 responses.*

"Did anyone meet someone with a similar job to yours?"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Fantastic work today! I heard excellent use of our key phrases. For example, I heard Sofia say 'I'm in charge of the warehouse' – perfect! And I heard Chen say 'My job involves checking quality' – great!

Let me correct a couple of mistakes I heard:

1. Remember: 'I'm responsible FOR,' not 'I'm responsible OF.' We say: 'I'm responsible for sales,' not 'I'm responsible of sales.'

2. After 'involves,' use the -ING form: 'My job involves TRAVELING,' not 'My job involves TO TRAVEL.'

Now, think about this: Why is it important to explain your responsibilities clearly in English? Discuss with your partner for 30 seconds."

*Brief discussion.*

"Good! When you can explain your job well, people understand your expertise and can work with you better. Practice describing your real job this week!"

---

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

### Teacher Script:
"For homework, choose one option:

**Option 1 (Writing):** Write a paragraph (80-100 words) describing your job and responsibilities. Use at least 4 phrases from today's lesson.

**Option 2 (Speaking):** Record yourself (1 minute) explaining your job to someone who doesn't know your company. Describe your main responsibilities.

Any questions? See you next time!"
