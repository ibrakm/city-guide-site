# Lesson 4: Making & Receiving Phone Calls

**Level:** B1 (Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Professional telephone language, leaving messages, handling calls

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Good morning/afternoon everyone! Today we're learning about business phone calls in English. Let me ask you some questions to start."

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Do you make phone calls in English at work? How often?
2. What's difficult about phone calls in English?
3. What's the difference between a phone call and an email?
4. Have you ever had a problem during a phone call in English? What happened?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss these questions with your partner. 4 minutes. Partner A starts with questions 1 and 2. Go!"

*After 4 minutes:*
"Let's hear some answers. What's difficult about phone calls in English?"

*Take 3-4 responses. Expected answers: can't see the person, speaking speed, accent, understanding, pronunciation.*

"Yes, exactly! Phone calls are challenging because you can't see the person. But today we'll learn how to sound confident on the phone!"

---

## **Stage 2: Vocabulary/Context Brainstorm (5–7 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now, let's think about business phone calls. I'll write 'BUSINESS PHONE CALLS' on the board. With your partner, brainstorm: What phrases do you use? What situations need phone calls? What problems can happen? 2 minutes!"

*Write "BUSINESS PHONE CALLS" on the board.*

*After 2 minutes:*
"Stop! Let's collect your ideas. Give me one phrase or situation."

*Collect on board. Expected answers: Hello, this is..., Can I speak to..., hold on, leave a message, call back, wrong number, bad connection, appointment, order, complaint, etc.*

**Useful prompts:**
- "What do you say when you answer the phone?"
- "What if the person isn't available?"
- "What problems can happen on a call?"

---

## **Stage 3: Listening Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now I'm going to read two phone conversations. Listen carefully. I'll read each one twice. After, you'll answer some questions."

### **Listening Text (Teacher reads aloud):**

**Call 1: Making an Appointment**

*Phone rings*

**Receptionist:** Good morning, Thompson & Partners. How can I help you?

**Caller:** Good morning. This is Maria Santos from Global Exports. Could I speak to Mr. Thompson, please?

**Receptionist:** I'm afraid he's in a meeting right now. Can I take a message?

**Caller:** Yes, please. Could you ask him to call me back? It's about our meeting next week. My number is 555-0123.

**Receptionist:** Certainly. So that's Maria Santos from Global Exports, regarding the meeting next week, and your number is 555-0123. Is that correct?

**Caller:** Yes, that's right. Thank you very much.

**Receptionist:** You're welcome. I'll make sure he gets your message.

**Caller:** Thank you. Goodbye.

**Receptionist:** Goodbye.

---

**Call 2: Ordering Products**

*Phone rings*

**Supplier:** Hello, Office Supplies Direct. This is Tom speaking. How may I help you?

**Customer:** Hello Tom. This is Sarah Chen calling from TechPro Solutions. I'd like to place an order, please.

**Supplier:** Certainly! What would you like to order?

**Customer:** We need 50 boxes of printer paper and 20 toner cartridges for HP printers.

**Supplier:** Okay, let me just confirm that. 50 boxes of paper and 20 HP toner cartridges. Is that correct?

**Customer:** Yes, exactly.

**Supplier:** And when do you need these delivered?

**Customer:** Could we have them by Friday this week?

**Supplier:** Let me check... Yes, that's no problem. We can deliver on Friday morning.

**Customer:** Perfect! Thank you very much.

**Supplier:** You're welcome. I'll send you a confirmation email shortly. Is there anything else?

**Customer:** No, that's all. Thank you. Goodbye!

**Supplier:** Goodbye!

### **Comprehension Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Now, answer these questions with your partner:"

1. In Call 1, who does Maria want to speak to?
2. Why can't Maria speak to Mr. Thompson?
3. What message does Maria leave?
4. In Call 2, what does Sarah want to order?
5. When does Sarah need the delivery?

*Give 3 minutes.*

**Open-ended Discussion Questions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now discuss:"

1. Were these phone calls polite? How do you know?
2. What did the receptionist and supplier do to check information?
3. Which call was more formal? Why?

*Take feedback from 2-3 pairs.*

---

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's learn the key phrases for business phone calls. These will help you sound professional and confident."

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**1. Starting a Call:**
- "Good morning/afternoon, [company name]."
- "This is [name] speaking."
- "This is [name] from [company]."
- "How can/may I help you?"

**Examples:**
- "Good morning, ABC Corporation."
- "This is John speaking."
- "This is Maria from Global Trading."

**2. Asking to Speak to Someone:**
- "Could I speak to [name], please?"
- "I'd like to speak to [name], please."
- "Is [name] available?"
- "Could you put me through to [department/name]?"

**Examples:**
- "Could I speak to Mr. Kim, please?"
- "Could you put me through to the sales department?"

**3. The Person is Not Available:**
- "I'm afraid he's/she's not available at the moment."
- "He's/She's in a meeting right now."
- "Can I take a message?"
- "Would you like to leave a message?"

**4. Leaving a Message:**
- "Could you ask him/her to call me back?"
- "Could you tell him/her that [message]?"
- "My number is [number]."

**5. Checking Information:**
- "Let me confirm that..."
- "Could you repeat that, please?"
- "Sorry, could you speak more slowly?"
- "Is that correct?"

**6. Ending a Call:**
- "Thank you for your help."
- "I'll call back later."
- "Thank you. Goodbye."

### **Useful Tip:**

**Teacher Script:**
"When you answer the phone in business, don't just say 'Hello.' Say the company name or your name: 'Good morning, ABC Company' or 'This is John speaking.' This sounds professional!"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Let's practice! I'll be Person A, you all read Person B together first."

**Person A:** *Ring ring*  
**Person B:** Good afternoon, Global Sales. This is Anna speaking.  
**Person A:** Hello Anna. This is David from Tech Solutions. Could I speak to Ms. Johnson, please?  
**Person B:** I'm afraid she's not in the office today. Can I take a message?  
**Person A:** Yes, please. Could you ask her to call me back? It's about our order. My number is 555-7890.  
**Person B:** Certainly. Let me confirm – David from Tech Solutions, regarding your order, 555-7890. Is that correct?  
**Person A:** Yes, perfect. Thank you!  
**Person B:** You're welcome. Goodbye!

**Teacher Script:**
"Good! Now practice this in pairs. Sit back-to-back so you can't see each other – this is more like a real phone call! Partner A is A, Partner B is B. Then swap."

*Give 3 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Excellent! Now let's practice with some exercises."

### **Exercise 1: Put the Phone Call in Order**

**Teacher Script:**
"These sentences are mixed up. Put them in the correct order for a phone call."

**Instructions:**
Number these sentences 1-8 in the correct order:

___ a) Could I speak to Mr. Park, please?  
___ b) Goodbye!  
___ c) Yes, please ask him to call me back. My number is 555-3344.  
___ d) Good morning, Samsung Electronics.  
___ e) I'm afraid he's in a meeting. Can I take a message?  
___ f) Thank you very much. Goodbye.  
___ g) This is Lisa Chen from Import Export Services.  
___ h) Certainly. I'll give him your message.

**Correct Order:** d, g, a, e, c, h, f, b

**Teacher Script:**
"Work with your partner. Put them in order. Then practice reading the conversation!"

*Give 4 minutes. Check answers together.*

### **Exercise 2: Complete the Phone Phrases**

**Instructions:**
Complete with: *speaking / message / through / available / confirm / call back*

1. "This is Tom _______."
2. "Could you put me _______ to the manager?"
3. "I'm afraid she's not _______ right now."
4. "Can I take a _______?"
5. "Could you ask him to _______ _______?"
6. "Let me _______ that information."

**Answers:** 1. speaking, 2. through, 3. available, 4. message, 5. call back, 6. confirm

**Teacher Script:**
"Complete the phrases. Then practice saying them with your partner!"

*Give 3 minutes.*

---

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Role-play (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Great! Now it's time for real phone practice! You'll work in pairs. Sit back-to-back like you're on the phone. I'll give you situation cards. Do both situations."

### **Role-play Cards:**

**Prepare 4 sets (8 cards total):**

**Situation 1A:**
You work for ABC Trading Company. Answer the phone professionally. Take a message for your manager, Mr. Wilson (he's not available – in a meeting). Confirm all details.

**Situation 1B:**
Call ABC Trading Company. You need to speak to Mr. Wilson about a late delivery. Your name is Karen Lee from Retail Solutions. Your number: 555-8899.

---

**Situation 2A:**
Call Tech Support Services. You need to speak to someone in the IT department. You have a problem with your computer system. Your name is Marco Rossi from Fashion Direct.

**Situation 2B:**
You work at Tech Support Services reception. Answer the phone. The IT department is busy. Offer to take a message or ask the caller to call back in 30 minutes.

---

**Situation 3A:**
You work for Office Supplies Plus. Answer the phone. A customer wants to order products. Take the order: ask what they need, how many, and when they need delivery. Confirm all information.

**Situation 3B:**
Call Office Supplies Plus. Order: 100 notebooks, 50 pens, 20 folders. You need them delivered next Monday. Your company is Global Enterprises. Ask about delivery.

---

**Situation 4A:**
Call International Hotel. You want to book a meeting room for 20 people next Thursday afternoon. Your name is Yuki Tanaka from Tech Innovations. Ask about price and equipment (projector, WiFi).

**Situation 4B:**
You work at International Hotel reception. Answer the phone. You have meeting rooms available. Meeting room price: $200 for half day. All rooms have projector and WiFi. Take the booking details.

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Each pair, take your situation cards. Sit back-to-back. Do your first role-play. Then swap cards and do the second one. Use the phrases we learned! Remember to sound professional! You have 10 minutes."

*Monitor and listen. Take notes of good language and errors.*

*After 10 minutes:*

**Teacher Script:**
"Excellent work! Let's hear one phone call. Pair 3, can you demonstrate situation 4?"

*One pair demonstrates.*

"Great! What professional phrases did you hear?"

*Elicit key phrases from students.*

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Wonderful phone calls today! I heard excellent professional language. For example, I heard Ahmed say 'Could I speak to Mr. Wilson, please?' – very polite! And I heard Natalia say 'Let me confirm that' – professional!

Let me correct two mistakes I heard:

1. When someone asks 'Can I speak to John?' don't say 'Wait.' Say 'One moment, please' or 'Hold on, please.' It's more polite.

2. Don't forget to confirm phone numbers! Repeat them back: 'So that's 555-1234, correct?'

Now think: What will you do next time you get a business phone call in English? Discuss with your partner."

*30 seconds discussion.*

"Good! Remember: speak clearly, speak slowly, and don't be afraid to ask people to repeat. Practice makes perfect!"
