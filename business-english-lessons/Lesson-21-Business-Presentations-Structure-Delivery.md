# Lesson 21: Business Presentations – Structure & Delivery

**Level:** B1+ (Upper Intermediate)  
**Students:** 8 adult learners  
**Duration:** 60–75 minutes  
**Focus:** Presentation structure, signposting language, engaging delivery, handling Q&A

---

## **Stage 1: Warm-up & Lead-in (10 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Welcome to Module 4: Professional Travel & Trade! Today we're focusing on business presentations – an essential skill for introducing products, services, or ideas. Have you ever given a presentation in English? Let's discuss!"

**Warm-up Questions (write on board):**
1. Have you ever given a business presentation? In what language?
2. What makes a presentation effective? What makes it boring?
3. What's difficult about presenting in a foreign language?
4. What's your biggest fear about giving presentations?

**Instructions:**
"Discuss with your partner. 4 minutes. Share your presentation experiences – good and bad!"

### Teacher Script:

*Collect vocabulary: introduction, agenda, main points, visual aids, conclusion, Q&A, signposting, transition, engage audience, etc.*

## **Stage 3: Reading Input (10–12 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Let's read about presentation structure and see a model presentation outline."

### **Reading Text:**

**The Universal Presentation Structure**

Every effective business presentation follows a clear structure:

**1. OPENING (10% of time)**
- Greet the audience
- Introduce yourself and your topic
- State the purpose
- Give an overview (agenda)
- Set expectations (timing, questions)

**Example:**
"Good morning everyone. My name is Sarah Chen, and I'm the Product Manager at TechSolutions. Today I'm going to present our new cloud storage solution. I'll cover three main points: first, the current challenges businesses face with data storage; second, how our solution addresses these challenges; and third, our pricing and implementation process. The presentation will take about 20 minutes, and I'll be happy to answer questions at the end."

**2. BODY (70-80% of time)**
- Present 3-5 main points (3 is ideal)
- Use clear transitions between points
- Provide evidence, examples, data
- Use visual aids effectively

**Structure each main point:**
- State the point
- Explain/elaborate
- Provide example or evidence
- Link to next point

**3. CONCLUSION (10% of time)**
- Summarize key points
- Restate main message
- Call to action (if appropriate)
- Invite questions

**Example:**
"To summarize, we've seen that traditional storage methods are costly and inefficient. Our cloud solution offers secure, scalable storage at 40% lower cost. Implementation takes just 2 weeks. I encourage you to try our free 30-day trial. Thank you for your attention. I'm now happy to answer any questions."

**Sample Presentation Outline:**

**Topic: Introducing a New Product Line**

**Opening (2 minutes)**
- Greeting and introduction
- Purpose: "I'm here to introduce our new sustainable fabric line"
- Agenda: market trends, product features, benefits, pricing

**Main Point 1 (5 minutes)**
- Market demand for sustainable products
- Statistics and trends
- Customer feedback

**Transition:** "Now that we understand the market demand, let me show you our solution..."

**Main Point 2 (5 minutes)**
- Product features: organic cotton, bamboo fiber, recycled materials
- Production process
- Certifications

**Transition:** "These features translate into important benefits for your business..."

**Main Point 3 (5 minutes)**
- Benefits: appeal to eco-conscious consumers, competitive pricing, reliable supply
- Case study example
- Testimonials

**Transition:** "Now let's look at the investment required..."

**Main Point 4 (3 minutes)**
- Pricing structure
- Minimum order quantities
- Delivery timeline

**Conclusion (2 minutes)**
- Summary of 4 main points
- Call to action: "I invite you to review our sample book and place a trial order"
- Thank audience
- Q&A

**Total: 22 minutes + Q&A**

### **Comprehension Questions:**

1. What are the three main parts of a presentation?
2. How much time should each section take?
3. Why is it important to use transitions?
4. What should you include in your conclusion?

*Discuss 3 minutes.*

## **Stage 4: Language Focus (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now let's learn essential presentation language – signposting phrases that guide your audience through your presentation!"

### **Key Expressions:**

Write on board:

**OPENING:**

**1. Greeting & Introducing Yourself:**
- "Good morning/afternoon everyone."
- "Thank you for coming today."
- "My name is [name], and I'm [position] at [company]."
- "I'm here today to..."

**2. Stating the Purpose:**
- "Today I'm going to present/talk about..."
- "The purpose of this presentation is to..."
- "I'm here to introduce/explain/demonstrate..."
- "My objective today is to..."

**3. Giving the Agenda:**
- "I'll cover three main points: first... second... third..."
- "My presentation is divided into four parts."
- "I'll begin by... then I'll move on to... and finally..."
- "First, I'll talk about X. Then we'll look at Y. Finally, I'll discuss Z."

**4. Setting Expectations:**
- "This will take approximately 20 minutes."
- "Please feel free to ask questions at any time."
- "I'll take questions at the end."
- "Please hold your questions until the end."

**BODY (SIGNPOSTING):**

**5. Starting a New Point:**
- "Let's begin with..."
- "First, I'd like to talk about..."
- "To start, let me show you..."
- "Let me begin by..."

**6. Transitions Between Points:**
- "Now let's move on to..."
- "Let me now turn to..."
- "This brings me to my next point..."
- "Having looked at X, let's now consider Y..."

**7. Referring to Visuals:**
- "As you can see in this slide/chart..."
- "This graph shows..."
- "Take a look at this..."
- "Let me draw your attention to..."

**8. Giving Examples:**
- "For example..."
- "Let me give you an example..."
- "To illustrate this point..."
- "A case in point is..."

**9. Emphasizing Key Points:**
- "I'd like to emphasize that..."
- "The key point here is..."
- "What's really important is..."
- "It's worth noting that..."

**CONCLUSION:**

**10. Signaling the End:**
- "To conclude..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "Let me summarize the main points..."

**11. Summarizing:**
- "We've seen that..."
- "We've covered three main areas..."
- "The key takeaways are..."

**12. Call to Action:**
- "I encourage you to..."
- "I invite you to..."
- "The next step is..."
- "I recommend that you..."

**13. Thanking & Inviting Questions:**
- "Thank you for your attention."
- "I'm now happy to answer your questions."
- "Does anyone have any questions?"
- "I'd be pleased to answer any questions."

**HANDLING Q&A:**

**14. Responding to Questions:**
- "That's a good question."
- "Thank you for asking that."
- "Let me clarify..."
- "To answer your question..."
- "I'm glad you brought that up."

**15. When You Don't Know:**
- "That's an interesting question. I'll need to check and get back to you."
- "I don't have that information right now, but I'll find out for you."

**DELIVERY TIPS:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Remember these presentation tips:

✅ **Speak clearly and slowly** – Not too fast!  
✅ **Make eye contact** – Look at your audience  
✅ **Use gestures naturally** – Don't stand frozen  
✅ **Pause between points** – Give audience time to process  
✅ **Vary your tone** – Don't speak in monotone  
✅ **Show enthusiasm** – Your energy affects the audience  
✅ **Practice beforehand** – Know your content  
✅ **Time yourself** – Don't run over time"

### **Practice Dialogue:**

"Let's practice a presentation opening:"

"Good morning everyone. Thank you for being here today. My name is David Kim, and I'm the Sales Director at Global Fabrics. Today I'm going to present our new collection of sustainable textiles. I'll cover three main areas: first, current market trends in sustainable fashion; second, our product range and features; and third, pricing and ordering process. The presentation will take about 15 minutes, and I'll be happy to answer questions at the end. So, let's begin with market trends..."

"Practice this opening in pairs. Then create your own presentation opening for a product or service you know!"

*Give 5 minutes for practice.*

## **Stage 5: Guided Practice (10 min)**

### **Exercise 1: Put the Presentation in Order**

Number these parts 1-10:

___ Invite questions  
___ State your purpose  
___ Introduce yourself  
___ Greet the audience  
___ Present main point 1  
___ Give overview/agenda  
___ Conclude and summarize  
___ Present main point 2  
___ Thank the audience  
___ Transition between points  

**Answer:** 4, 6, 5, 1, 7, 2, 10, 8, 9, 3

### **Exercise 2: Choose the Correct Signposting Phrase**

1. _______ everyone. (Good morning / Hello there)
2. Today I'm going to _______ our new product. (present / say)
3. I'll cover _______ main points. (three / some)
4. _______, let me show you the features. (First / One)
5. _______ to my next point. (This brings me / I go now)
6. _______, we've seen that our product offers great value. (To conclude / Finally to say)

*Students practice in pairs – 4 minutes.*

### **Exercise 3: Create a Presentation Outline**

"Choose a topic and create a simple presentation outline:
- Opening (what will you say?)
- 3 main points
- Conclusion

Topics: Your company / A product you sell / Your city / A service you offer"

*Students work individually then share with partner – 6 minutes.*

## **Stage 6: Communicative Task / Mini-Presentations (15 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Now it's time to present! Each of you will give a 2-3 minute presentation. Don't worry about perfection – focus on structure and using signposting language!"

### **Presentation Topics (assign to students):**

1. **Introduce your company** (what you do, products/services, why customers choose you)
2. **Introduce a product** (features, benefits, why it's special)
3. **Describe a successful project** (what you did, challenges, results)
4. **Explain a process** (how something works in your company)
5. **Propose a new idea** (a suggestion for improvement)
6. **Compare two products** (similarities, differences, recommendation)
7. **Describe market trends** (in your industry, what's changing)
8. **Explain your department's role** (what your team does, importance)

### **Instructions:**

**Teacher Script:**
"Everyone: you have 5 minutes to prepare a 2-3 minute presentation. Use the structure we learned:
- Opening (greet, introduce self, state purpose, give agenda)
- 2-3 main points (use transitions!)
- Conclusion (summarize, thank, invite questions)

Don't write a script – just make bullet points! You'll present standing up, facing the class."

*5 minutes preparation time.*

**Teacher Script:**
"Okay! Presentations begin! Remember: speak clearly, make eye contact, use signposting phrases. After each presentation, the audience will ask 1-2 questions. Presenter 1, you're up!"

*Each student presents 2-3 minutes + 1 minute Q&A = approximately 24-32 minutes for 8 students. If short on time, divide into two groups of 4, each group presents to each other simultaneously.*

**After presentations:**

"Excellent work everyone! What signposting phrases did you hear? What made the presentations effective?"

---

## **Stage 7: Wrap-up & Reflection (5 min)**

### Teacher Script:
"Outstanding presentations! You all used great structure and signposting!

**REMEMBER THE PRESENTATION FORMULA:**

**Opening:** Greet → Introduce → Purpose → Agenda → Set expectations  
**Body:** Point 1 → Transition → Point 2 → Transition → Point 3  
**Conclusion:** Summarize → Thank → Invite questions

**THREE KEYS TO GREAT PRESENTATIONS:**

1. **Structure** – Clear beginning, middle, end
2. **Signposting** – Guide your audience through your presentation
3. **Practice** – Rehearse! Know your content!

One more tip: The #1 presentation mistake is trying to cover too much. Better to cover 3 points well than 10 points poorly!

What's one thing you'll work on for your next presentation? 30 seconds."

## **Stage 8: Homework (Optional)**

**Option 1 (Speaking):** Prepare a 5-minute presentation about your company or a product. Record yourself. Use all the signposting language from today.

**Option 2 (Writing):** Write a complete presentation script (200-250 words) including opening, 3 main points, and conclusion. Use signposting phrases.

**Option 3 (Analysis):** Watch a TED talk or business presentation online. Identify the structure and list the signposting phrases used.
