## 📈 Curriculum Progression

### **Unit 1: Foundations (A1 Beginning)**
Students learn absolute basics: colors, numbers, classroom language, time, weather

**Key Skills:**
- Name colors, count 1-100
- Identify classroom objects
- Say days/months
- Describe weather
- Follow basic instructions

---

### **Unit 2: Personal Information (A1)**
Students learn to talk about themselves and others

**Key Skills:**
- Introduce themselves
- Describe family members
- Describe physical appearance
- Express personality traits
- Talk about school and hobbies

**Grammar:** Present simple, to be, have/has, possessive adjectives

---

### **Unit 3: Everyday Life (A1-A2 Bridge)**
Students describe daily activities and routines

**Key Skills:**
- Describe daily routines
- Talk about food preferences
- Shop for items
- Describe home and city
- Ask for and give directions

**Grammar:** Present simple for habits, prepositions, there is/are

---

### **Unit 4: Experiences & Opinions (A2)**
Students express opinions and discuss past/future

**Key Skills:**
- Express likes and dislikes
- Talk about past events (simple past)
- Discuss future plans (going to)
- Describe celebrations and traditions
- Talk about friends and technology

**Grammar:** Simple past (regular/irregular), going to future, like + -ing

---

### **Unit 5: Integration & Real Life (A2)**
Students handle practical, real-world scenarios

**Key Skills:**
- Order food at restaurants
- Shop at markets/stores
- Describe medical symptoms
- Make polite requests and apologies
- Make small talk and meet new people

**Grammar:** Review and integration of all previous grammar

**Final Assessment:** Comprehensive speaking test integrating all 30 lessons

## 💡 Teaching Tips

### **Before You Start:**
1. Read the entire lesson before class
2. Prepare board space for vocabulary lists
3. Print any handouts (optional - lessons designed for minimal printing)
4. Arrange seating for pair/group work

### **During the Lesson:**
1. Follow the timing guidelines (use a timer!)
2. Stick to the teacher script (especially for beginners)
3. Encourage maximum student speaking time
4. Monitor all pair/group work actively
5. Correct errors gently during fluency tasks
6. Praise all attempts at communication
7. Use gestures, demonstrations, and board visuals

### **After the Lesson:**
1. Note what worked well and what didn't
2. Identify students needing extra support
3. Record assessment scores
4. Prepare for next lesson

### **Managing Large Classes (12-14 students):**
- Use pair work frequently (6-7 pairs easier to monitor than 12-14 individuals)
- Rotate partners for variety
- Group activities with 3-4 students per group
- Clear instructions before starting activities
- Time limits for each activity
- Positive reinforcement for on-task behavior

## 🌍 Cultural Sensitivity Notes

### **Islamic Practices:**
- Respect prayer times (5 daily prayers for Muslim students)
- Ramadan awareness (students may be fasting - adjust energy levels)
- Halal dietary restrictions (don't use pork in examples)
- Modest dress codes (appropriate in examples)
- Gender sensitivity (some activities may need same-gender pairs)

### **Moroccan Context:**
- Use Moroccan cities in examples (Casablanca, Marrakech, Fes, Rabat, Tangier)
- Reference Moroccan food (tajine, couscous, harira, msemen, mint tea)
- Mention Moroccan money (dirhams)
- Include Moroccan celebrations (Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Mawlid)
- Acknowledge multilingual background (Arabic/Darija + French)

### **Western Cultural References:**
- Introduce but don't impose (McDonald's, hamburgers, Western holidays)
- Explain cultural differences respectfully
- Compare rather than judge
- Use as learning opportunities about other cultures
