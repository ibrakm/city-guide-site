# Lesson 30: Final Speaking Test - Integration of All Skills

**Level**: A1-A2 (Comprehensive Assessment)  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Comprehensive Speaking Assessment - All 30 Lessons  

---

## OVERVIEW

This is the culminating lesson of the 30-lesson program! Students will demonstrate everything they've learned from Lesson 1 (Colors) through Lesson 29 (Meeting New People). This is primarily a speaking assessment, but also celebrates student progress and builds confidence for continued English learning.

---

## LESSON OBJECTIVES

By the end of this final lesson, students will:
- Demonstrate speaking skills from A1-A2 level
- Integrate vocabulary, grammar, and communication skills from all 30 lessons
- Participate in authentic conversational tasks
- Receive constructive feedback on progress
- Celebrate their achievement!

---

## ASSESSMENT OVERVIEW

### Test Components (60 minutes total):

1. **Warm-up Celebration (5 min)** - Building confidence
2. **Part 1: Quick-Fire Questions (10 min)** - Whole class
3. **Part 2: Individual Speaking Tasks (35 min)** - One-on-one or pairs
4. **Part 3: Group Communication Task (10 min)** - Small groups
5. **Wrap-up & Certificates (5 min)** - Celebration!

### Scoring System:

**Total Points: 100**
- Part 1: Quick-Fire Questions (20 points)
- Part 2: Individual Tasks (60 points)
- Part 3: Group Task (20 points)

### Grading Scale:
- **90-100**: Outstanding (A)
- **80-89**: Excellent (B)
- **70-79**: Good (C)
- **60-69**: Satisfactory (D)
- **Below 60**: Needs more practice

---

## LESSON PLAN

### WARM-UP CELEBRATION (5 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning, everyone! Today is a VERY special day! We've been together for 30 lessons! Do you remember Lesson 1? We learned COLORS! Blue, red, green... Then we learned numbers, days, weather, introducing ourselves, family, daily routines, food, past tense, future tense, and SO much more!

Today, you will SHOW me everything you learned! This is not scary - this is EXCITING! This is your chance to show how much your English improved! You should be proud! Let's start!"

**Teacher asks:**
"Who feels confident about their English now? Raise your hand!"

[Encourage everyone - celebrate!]

"Remember: Making mistakes is OK! The important thing is you TRY and you COMMUNICATE! Let's go!"

---

### PART 1: QUICK-FIRE QUESTIONS (10 minutes, 20 points)

**Teacher Instructions:**

"I will ask each person 2-3 quick questions. Answer quickly! Don't think too much - just speak naturally!"

**Question Bank (Teacher chooses 2-3 per student):**

#### Unit 1 Questions (Foundation):
1. "What's your favorite color? Why?"
2. "Count from 1 to 20."
3. "What day is today? What day was yesterday?"
4. "What's the weather like today?"
5. "Name 5 classroom objects."
6. "What month is your birthday?"

#### Unit 2 Questions (Personal Information):
7. "Introduce yourself: name, age, from where."
8. "Tell me about your family. How many people?"
9. "Describe your best friend. What does he/she look like?"
10. "Are you shy or outgoing? Describe your personality."
11. "What's your favorite school subject? Why?"
12. "What do you do in your free time?"

#### Unit 3 Questions (Everyday Life):
13. "What time do you wake up on school days?"
14. "What do you eat for breakfast?"
15. "Do you like shopping? What do you usually buy?"
16. "Describe your bedroom. What's in it?"
17. "What places are near your house?"
18. "How do you get to school?"

#### Unit 4 Questions (Experiences & Opinions):
19. "What food do you like? What food do you hate?"
20. "What did you do last weekend?"
21. "What are you going to do next weekend?"
22. "Tell me about a Moroccan celebration you like."
23. "Who is your best friend? Why do you like them?"
24. "Do you use a smartphone? What do you use it for?"

#### Unit 5 Questions (Real Life):
25. "What's your favorite food to order at a restaurant?"
26. "What did you buy last time you went shopping?"
27. "Have you ever been to the doctor? Why?"
28. "How do you apologize when you're late to class?"
29. "How do you start a conversation with someone new?"

**Scoring Rubric (per question):**
- **3-4 points**: Clear, complete answer with good vocabulary/grammar
- **2 points**: Understandable answer with some errors
- **1 point**: Attempted answer, many errors, hard to understand
- **0 points**: No answer or completely incorrect

---

### PART 2: INDIVIDUAL SPEAKING TASKS (35 minutes, 60 points)

**Teacher Instructions:**

"Now, each student will have 3-4 minutes with me for individual speaking tasks. While I test one student, the others will do a written reflection activity."

**Written Activity for Waiting Students:**

**Teacher writes on board:**

"While you wait for your speaking test, write about your English learning journey. Answer:
1. What was your favorite lesson (1-30)?
2. What was the most difficult topic?
3. What did you improve most: speaking, listening, reading, or writing?
4. What do you want to learn next in English?
5. How will you use English in your future?

Write 100-150 words. Be honest!"

---

#### Individual Speaking Tasks (Choose 4 out of 6)

**Teacher gives each student 4 tasks (15 points each = 60 total)**

---

**TASK 1: Self-Introduction (Lessons 1-2, 7)**

**Teacher says:**
"Introduce yourself to me. Pretend we're meeting for the first time. Tell me:
- Your name
- Your age  
- Where you're from
- Your family
- Your hobbies
- Why you're learning English"

**Time:** 1 minute minimum

**Scoring:**
- **13-15 points**: Fluent, complete, natural, minimal errors
- **10-12 points**: Good introduction, some errors, understandable
- **7-9 points**: Basic information given, many errors
- **4-6 points**: Incomplete, very difficult to understand
- **0-3 points**: Cannot complete task

---

**TASK 2: Describe Your Day (Lessons 5, 13)**

**Teacher says:**
"Describe your typical school day from morning to night. Use time expressions and sequence words (first, then, after that, finally)."

**Time:** 1.5 minutes minimum

**Scoring:** Same rubric as Task 1

**Must include:**
- At least 6 daily activities
- At least 3 time expressions
- Sequence words

---

**TASK 3: Tell a Story (Lessons 20, 21)**

**Teacher gives options:**
"Choose one topic and tell me about it:
a) What you did last weekend (past tense)
b) What you're going to do next vacation (future - going to)
c) A memorable day from your past"

**Time:** 1.5 minutes minimum

**Scoring:** Same rubric

**Must include:**
- Clear beginning, middle, end
- Correct verb tenses (mostly)
- Time expressions

---

**TASK 4: Role-Play Scenario (Lessons 15, 25, 26)**

**Teacher gives a scenario:**

"You're in a restaurant/shop. I'm the waiter/shopkeeper. Have a complete conversation. You:
- Greet me
- Ask about items/prices
- Order/buy something
- Make a request or ask a question
- Pay and say goodbye"

**Time:** 2 minutes

**Scoring:** Same rubric

**Must include:**
- Polite language (please, thank you)
- Questions
- Complete transaction

---

**TASK 5: Express Opinions (Lessons 19, 23, 24)**

**Teacher asks:**
"Talk about your likes and dislikes. Tell me:
- Your favorite food and why
- Your favorite season and why
- Your favorite way to spend free time
- One thing you don't like and why"

**Time:** 1.5 minutes minimum

**Scoring:** Same rubric

**Must include:**
- "I like/love/don't like/hate..."
- Reasons (because...)
- At least 3 different topics

---

**TASK 6: Problem Solving (Lessons 27, 28, 18)**

**Teacher gives a problem:**

"Choose one situation and tell me what you would say/do:
a) You're feeling sick. Describe your symptoms to the doctor.
b) You're lost in a new city. Ask for directions to your hotel.
c) You forgot your homework. Apologize to your teacher and explain.
d) You accidentally broke your friend's pen. What do you say?"

**Time:** 1 minute minimum

**Scoring:** Same rubric

**Must include:**
- Appropriate language for situation
- Clear explanation
- Polite expressions

---

### PART 3: GROUP COMMUNICATION TASK (10 minutes, 20 points)

**Teacher Instructions:**

"Now everyone is back together! Final task! You will work in groups of 3-4 students. Your task is to have a NATURAL CONVERSATION in English for 5 minutes."

**Teacher assigns groups and gives topics:**

**Topic Options (each group gets ONE):**

**Topic 1: Planning a Class Party**
"Your class wants to have an end-of-year party. Discuss:
- When should it be?
- Where should it be?
- What food should you have?
- What activities?
Everyone must contribute ideas!"

**Topic 2: Describing Your Dream Vacation**
"If you could travel anywhere, where would you go? Take turns describing your dream vacation:
- Where would you go?
- Who would you go with?
- What would you do there?
- How long would you stay?
Ask each other questions!"

**Topic 3: Comparing Your Routines**
"Compare your daily routines! Discuss:
- What time do you each wake up?
- What do you eat for breakfast?
- What do you do after school?
- What time do you go to bed?
Find similarities and differences!"

**Topic 4: Moroccan Culture for Tourists**
"Imagine a tourist asks you about Morocco. Discuss together and prepare to explain:
- Best places to visit in Morocco
- Traditional Moroccan food tourists should try
- Moroccan customs and traditions
- Useful Arabic/French phrases for tourists"

**Teacher's Role:**
- Walk around and listen to each group
- Take notes on fluency, vocabulary, interaction
- Don't interrupt unless absolutely necessary

**Scoring Rubric (20 points per student):**

**18-20 points:** Active participation, natural conversation, good turn-taking, helps others, uses varied vocabulary and grammar

**15-17 points:** Participates well, mostly natural, occasional errors don't block communication

**12-14 points:** Participates but hesitant, several errors, basic vocabulary

**9-11 points:** Minimal participation, many errors, very simple language

**0-8 points:** Does not participate meaningfully, cannot maintain conversation

## FOLLOW-UP RECOMMENDATIONS

**Teacher provides each student with personalized next steps:**

### For Strong Students (90+):
"Excellent work! You're ready for B1 level! Continue with:
- Reading English books for teens
- Watching English movies/shows without subtitles
- Conversing with English speakers online (safely!)
- Writing a journal in English daily
- Preparing for Cambridge A2 Key exam (optional)"

### For Good Students (70-89):
"Great progress! Keep practicing:
- Review any weak topics from these 30 lessons
- Practice speaking 10 minutes daily (even to yourself!)
- Listen to English podcasts for learners
- Join English club or find a study partner
- Aim for B1 in 6 months!"

### For Students Who Need More Practice (60-69):
"You're improving! Don't give up! Focus on:
- Reviewing A1-A2 foundations
- Practicing speaking without fear of mistakes
- Doing online English exercises (many free resources!)
- Watching English videos with subtitles
- Finding an English practice partner"

### For Students Below 60:
"Keep trying! English is hard, but you CAN do it! I recommend:
- One-on-one tutoring or extra help sessions
- Repeating some of these lessons
- Focusing on speaking and listening first
- Using apps like Duolingo for daily practice
- Not comparing yourself to others - everyone learns at their own pace!"
