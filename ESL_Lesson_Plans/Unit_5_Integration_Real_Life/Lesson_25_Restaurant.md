# Lesson 25: At the Restaurant

**Level**: A2  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Ordering Food, Menu Vocabulary, Restaurant Etiquette  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Read and understand a restaurant menu in English
- Order food and drinks politely
- Ask about menu items and prices
- Make special requests (no onions, extra sauce, etc.)
- Use restaurant-specific vocabulary and phrases
- Handle common restaurant situations

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Welcome to Unit 5 - Real Life English! We've learned so much: introducing yourself, daily routines, describing things, talking about the past and future. Now we put EVERYTHING together in real-life situations! Today: AT THE RESTAURANT! Have you ever been to a restaurant where they speak English? Maybe McDonald's? Maybe a hotel restaurant? Today you'll learn to order food in English like a pro!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Q1:** "What's your favorite restaurant in Morocco? What do you like to order there?"

**Q2:** "Imagine you're in a restaurant in London or New York. You're hungry! But the waiter only speaks English. How do you feel? Nervous? Excited? Scared?"

**Q3:** "What's the difference between eating at home and eating at a restaurant?"

#### Brainstorming Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What words do you know about restaurants? Tell me anything - food, people, objects!"

[Write: waiter, menu, table, food, drink, bill, tip, order, etc.]

#### Critical Thinking Question

"Why is it important to know restaurant English? When might you need this?"

[Tourism, traveling, working in hospitality, impressing friends, business situations, etc.]

---

### 2. VOCABULARY & READING (10-12 minutes)

#### Complete Restaurant Vocabulary

**Teacher teaches with gestures and demonstrations:**

| Category | Words | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **People** | waiter, waitress, chef, customer, guest | "The waiter brings the menu." |
| **Place** | restaurant, café, fast food, table, seat, counter | "Please sit at this table." |
| **Items** | menu, bill/check, plate, glass, fork, knife, spoon, napkin | "Here is your menu." |
| **Actions** | order, eat, drink, pay, tip, reserve, book | "I'd like to order, please." |
| **Menu Sections** | appetizers, main course, dessert, beverages, sides | "What would you like for the main course?" |

#### Essential Restaurant Phrases

**Teacher writes and drills:**

**Arriving:**
- "Table for two, please."
- "Do you have a reservation?"
- "Follow me, please."

**Ordering:**
- "Can I see the menu, please?"
- "What do you recommend?"
- "I'd like to order..."
- "Can I have...?"
- "I'll have the... / I'll take the..."

**Making Requests:**
- "Could I have extra sauce?"
- "No onions, please."
- "Is this spicy?"
- "Does this have meat?"
- "I'm allergic to..."

**During the Meal:**
- "How is everything?"
- "It's delicious!"
- "Can I have some water, please?"

**Ending:**
- "Can I have the bill, please?"
- "The check, please."
- "Is service included?"
- "Keep the change."

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Polite Ordering Patterns

**Teacher writes:**

```
VERY POLITE (Formal):
"Could I have..., please?"
"I would like..., please."
"May I have..., please?"

POLITE (Standard):
"Can I have..., please?"
"I'll have..., please."

CASUAL (Informal):
"I'll take the..."
"Give me the..." (can sound rude - be careful!)

QUESTIONS:
"What do you recommend?"
"What's good here?"
"What's in this dish?"
"Is this spicy/sweet/salty?"
"Does this have...?"
```

#### Complete Restaurant Dialogue

**Teacher acts out (use props if possible):**

**Dialogue: "Ordering Lunch"**

```
Waiter: Good afternoon! Welcome to Casa English Café. Table for one?
Customer: Yes, please. Just me.
Waiter: Right this way. [Shows to table] Here's your menu. I'll be right back.

[2 minutes later]

Waiter: Are you ready to order?
Customer: Yes, I think so. What do you recommend?
Waiter: The grilled chicken is very popular. And the tajine is delicious!
Customer: OK, I'll have the chicken tajine, please.
Waiter: Excellent choice! And to drink?
Customer: Can I have fresh orange juice, please?
Waiter: Of course! Anything else?
Customer: Yes, could I also have a garden salad as an appetizer?
Waiter: Certainly! So that's one garden salad, one chicken tajine, and one 
        orange juice. Is that correct?
Customer: Yes, perfect!
Waiter: Great! Your salad will be ready in about 5 minutes.

[15 minutes later, after eating]

Waiter: How was everything?
Customer: It was delicious! Very good!
Waiter: Wonderful! Would you like dessert or coffee?
Customer: No, thank you. Can I have the bill, please?
Waiter: Sure! I'll bring it right away.

[Waiter returns with bill]

Waiter: Here you are. The total is 115 dirhams.
Customer: Here you go. [Gives 120 DH] Keep the change.
Waiter: Thank you very much! Have a great day!
Customer: You too! Goodbye!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. Teacher reads both roles
2. Teacher = Waiter, Students = Customer (all together)
3. Students = Waiter, Teacher = Customer
4. Pair work: Practice full dialogue
5. Add variations: vegetarian request, allergy, complaint, etc.

**Teacher says:**
"Practice with partners! One is waiter, one is customer. Use the menu on board! Switch roles! 5 minutes!"

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Complete the Dialogue

Fill in the blanks:

```
Waiter: Good evening! ______ (1) for two?
Customer: Yes, please.
Waiter: Follow me. Here's your ______ (2).
Customer: Thank you. Can I ______ (3) some water, please?
Waiter: Of course! Are you ready to ______ (4)?
Customer: Yes, I'll ______ (5) the fish and chips.
Waiter: Excellent! Anything else?
Customer: No, that's all. ______ (6).
```

**Answers:** (1) Table (2) menu (3) have (4) order (5) have/take (6) Thank you

#### Exercise 2: Match Situations to Phrases

Match the situation to the correct phrase:

Situations:
1. You want to ask for the bill
2. You're allergic to peanuts
3. You want to know what's good
4. The food is delicious
5. You want extra ketchup

Phrases:
a) "Could I have extra ketchup, please?"
b) "What do you recommend?"
c) "Can I have the bill, please?"
d) "I'm allergic to peanuts. Does this have peanuts?"
e) "This is delicious!"

**Answers:** 1-c, 2-d, 3-b, 4-e, 5-a

#### Exercise 3: Calculate Your Order

**Teacher says:**
"Look at the menu. You have 100 dirhams. What will you order? Calculate the total!"

[Students plan orders and calculate]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Activity: Restaurant Role-Play

**Teacher sets up:**

"Now we transform our classroom into a RESTAURANT! I will divide you into groups of 3:
- 1 Waiter
- 2 Customers

Waiters: Stand here and get a 'menu' (use the board menu).
Customers: Sit at your 'tables' (desks).

You will have real conversations! Waiters, walk to tables and take orders! Customers, order food! Try to make it realistic - ask questions, make requests, chat a little!

You have 6 minutes! Then we switch roles!"

**Round 1:** Waiter-Customer role-play (6 minutes)

[Teacher monitors, takes notes of good examples and errors]

**Round 2:** Switch roles (3 minutes - shorter)

#### Follow-up Reporting

**Teacher asks:**

"What did you order?"
"Waiters, what was the most popular dish?"
"Did anyone make a special request?"
"What was difficult? What was easy?"

### 6. WRAP-UP & MINI-TEST (5 minutes)

#### Mini-Test Questions

**Teacher asks randomly:**

**Q1:** "You're in a restaurant. How do you ask for the menu?"
**A:** "Can I see the menu, please?"

**Q2:** "How do you order chicken tajine politely?"
**A:** "I'd like chicken tajine, please." / "Can I have chicken tajine, please?"

**Q3:** "The food was excellent. What do you say to the waiter?"

**Q4:** "You want to pay. What do you say?"

**Q5:** "You're allergic to eggs. How do you ask if a dish has eggs?"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Common Errors to Address:
- "I want..." (too direct) → "I'd like..." / "Can I have..."
- Forgetting "please" and "thank you"
- "Give me" (sounds rude) → "Can I have"
- Wrong question form: "How much costs?" → "How much does it cost?"

### Cultural Notes:
- In English-speaking countries, tipping is expected (15-20% in USA, less in UK)
- Waiters come to ask "How is everything?" - this is normal
- In Morocco, service is sometimes included in bill
- Western restaurants often split bills easily
- Waiters don't rush customers - you can sit and talk after eating

### Pronunciation Focus:
- "I'd like" /aɪd laɪk/ (not "I like")
- "Bill" vs. "beer" (short i vs. long ee)
- "Waiter" /ˈweɪtə/

### Safety & Respect:
- Teach students to ask about ingredients if they have religious dietary restrictions (halal, no pork)
- Emphasize politeness - it's culturally important everywhere
- "Please" and "Thank you" are essential!
