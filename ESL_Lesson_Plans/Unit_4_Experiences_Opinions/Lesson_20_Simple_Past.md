# Lesson 20: Talking About Past Days (Simple Past)

**Level**: A2  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Simple Past Tense (was/were, regular past verbs)

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Use "was/were" to talk about past states
- Form regular past tense verbs (add -ed)
- Describe what they did yesterday or last week
- Use past time expressions (yesterday, last week, ago)
- Ask and answer "What did you do...?" questions

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Students' own memories of recent past events

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning, class! Today we're learning to talk about the PAST - things that already happened! Yesterday, last week, last month, last year... We use PAST TENSE in English. This is very important because we talk about past events all the time! Let's start!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Think about yesterday. What did you do? Did you go to school? Did you stay home? Did you visit someone? Think about your day yesterday!"

[Give thinking time]

**Teacher:** "Today you'll learn to describe yesterday in English!"

**Question 2:**
"Last weekend - Saturday and Sunday - what did you do? Something fun? Something boring? Did you rest? Did you work? Think!"

[Let 2-3 students share in any language]

**Question 3:**
"Where were you at 8 PM last night? Were you at home? Were you at a friend's house? Were you studying?"

[Students think and respond]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What did you do LAST WEEK? Any activities? I will write them on the board!"

[Write student suggestions. Add these if not mentioned:]
- Studied
- Played
- Watched TV
- Helped at home
- Visited family
- Talked with friends
- Went to school
- Did homework

**Teacher says:**
"Great! All of these are PAST actions! Notice: we don't say 'go to school' - we say 'WENT to school'! 'Play' becomes 'PLAYED'! Today we learn all these past forms!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Why is it important to talk about the past? Why do we need to describe things that already happened?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: sharing experiences, learning from mistakes, telling stories, keeping memories, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Exactly! The past makes us who we are! We learn from the past. We share our experiences. Stories are about the past! Today you'll be able to tell your stories in English!"

| Listen | Listened | /ˈlɪsnd/ | "I listened to music." |
| Study | Studied | /ˈstʌdid/ | "I studied for the test." |
| Help | Helped | /helpt/ | "I helped my mother." |
| Clean | Cleaned | /kliːnd/ | "I cleaned my room." |
| Cook | Cooked | /kʊkt/ | "My mother cooked dinner." |

| Start | Started | /ˈstɑːtɪd/ | "School started at 8 AM." |
| Finish | Finished | /ˈfɪnɪʃt/ | "I finished my homework." |

| Need | Needed | /ˈniːdɪd/ | "I needed help." |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "Listen! WALK in the past is WALKED. Repeat: WALKED."  
**Students:** "Walked."  
**Teacher:** "I walked to school yesterday. Repeat!"  
**Students:** "I walked to school yesterday."

[Continue with each verb]

**Important Pronunciation Note:**

**Teacher explains:**
"The -ED ending has THREE sounds:
1. /t/ after voiceless sounds: walked, cooked, watched
2. /d/ after voiced sounds: played, cleaned, listened
3. /ɪd/ after T or D sounds: wanted, needed, started

Listen carefully!" [Practice pronunciation 3-4 times]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar: Was/Were

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
BE in the Past: WAS / WERE

I was at school yesterday.
You were at school yesterday.
He/She/It was at school yesterday.
We were at school yesterday.
They were at school yesterday.

NEGATIVE:
I wasn't (was not) at home.
You weren't (were not) at home.
He/She/It wasn't at home.
We weren't at home.
They weren't at home.

QUESTIONS:
Were you at school yesterday? - Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.
Was he sick? - Yes, he was. / No, he wasn't.
Where were you last night? - I was at home.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"'Was' and 'were' are the past forms of 'am/is/are':
- I AM → I WAS
- He/She IS → He/She WAS  
- You/We/They ARE → You/We/They WERE

We use was/were for states, locations, and descriptions in the past!"

#### Regular Past Tense: -ED Rule

**Teacher writes:**

```
RULE: Add -ED to make past tense

walk → walked
play → played
help → helped

SPELLING RULES:

1. If verb ends in E, just add D:
   like → liked
   love → loved

2. If verb ends in consonant + Y, change Y to IED:
   study → studied
   try → tried

3. If short verb ends in consonant-vowel-consonant, double last letter:
   stop → stopped
   plan → planned
```

#### Question Forms in Past

**Teacher writes:**

```
DID + base verb

What did you do yesterday?
Did you watch TV last night?
Where did you go?
Who did you see?

SHORT ANSWERS:
Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"For questions and negatives in past, we use DID + base verb (NOT the past form!):
- 'Did you play?' ✅ (NOT 'Did you played?' ❌)
- 'I didn't go.' ✅ (NOT 'I didn't went.' ❌)"

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes:**

1. "Yesterday was Monday. Today is Tuesday."
2. "I was at school yesterday. I wasn't at home."
3. "Last week, I visited my grandparents."
4. "Did you do your homework? - Yes, I did."
5. "I watched a movie last night. It was interesting."
6. "Where were you yesterday? - I was at the library."

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Let's learn a conversation about yesterday! Listen!"

**Dialogue: "What Did You Do Yesterday?"**

```
A: Hi, Sara! I didn't see you yesterday. Where were you?
B: Hi! I was at home. I was sick.
A: Oh no! Are you OK now?
B: Yes, I'm fine now. Thank you! What about you? What did you do yesterday?
A: I had a normal day. I went to school in the morning.
B: Did you have a test?
A: Yes, I had a math test. It was difficult!
B: Did you study for it?
A: Yes, I studied on Sunday. I hope I did well!
B: I'm sure you did! What did you do after school?
A: I played football with my friends. It was fun! Then I went home and 
   helped my mother cook dinner.
B: Nice! What did you cook?
A: We cooked couscous. It was delicious! What did YOU do at home?
B: I rested a lot. I watched TV and slept. It was boring!
A: Well, rest is important when you're sick!
B: True! I'm happy to be back at school today!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts**
2. **Teacher = A, All Students = B**
3. **All Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students create their own conversation about yesterday**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, interview your partner about THEIR yesterday! Use the dialogue as a model. Change the details! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor and help with past tense]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs:**

"Who wants to share? Come perform your dialogue!"

[Praise: "Excellent use of past tense!" / "Great questions!"]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Detective Game - Where Were You?"

**Teacher says:**
"Now we play a game! Imagine something happened at school yesterday - someone drew on the board, someone took the teacher's pen, something funny! You are all DETECTIVES! Ask each other: 'Where were you at [time] yesterday?' 'What did you do?'"

**Activity Setup (2 minutes):**

**Teacher creates scenario:**
"Yesterday at 2:00 PM, someone put a funny picture on my desk! It was during break time. I want to know who did it! You are all suspects! Ask each other questions to find out who could have done it!"

**Teacher assigns students alibis (where they were):**
- Some students were in the classroom
- Some were in the cafeteria
- Some were in the library
- Some were playing outside

[Give 1 minute for students to remember their alibis]

**Activity (6 minutes):**

**Teacher says:**
"Walk around! Ask classmates:
- Where were you at 2:00 PM yesterday?
- What did you do?
- Who were you with?

Try to find out who was in the classroom! You have 6 minutes!"

[Monitor as students question each other in English]

#### Follow-up Solving (2 minutes)

**Teacher asks:**
"Who do you think did it? Why?"

[Students give their theories]

**Teacher reveals:**
"Actually, it was ME! I put the picture there to make you practice past tense! Surprise! Did you use good English questions?"

[Class laughs and discusses]

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Past tense is challenging - be patient and practice extensively
- Focus on pronunciation of -ed endings
- Display regular/irregular verb charts throughout lesson
- Encourage storytelling - makes past tense natural and memorable
- Use gestures to show "past" (pointing backward)

### Common Errors to Address:
- Using past with 'did': "Did you went?" ❌ → "Did you go?" ✅
- Double past: "I didn't went" ❌ → "I didn't go" ✅
- Irregular verbs: "I goed" ❌ → "I went" ✅
- Forgetting 'was/were': "I at school" ❌ → "I was at school" ✅
- Confusing present and past: "Yesterday I go..." ❌ → "Yesterday I went..." ✅

### Cultural Notes:
- Moroccan weddings as examples of past events
- Family visits and gatherings common in Moroccan culture
- School schedules and daily life routines
- Traditional vs. modern life comparisons

### Pronunciation Focus:
- -ED endings: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/
- "Yesterday" /ˈjestədeɪ/
- "Ago" /əˈɡəʊ/
- Irregular verbs (often different vowel sounds)

### Grammar Note:
- This is students' introduction to past tense
- Focus on simple past (not present perfect yet)
- Start with regular verbs, gradually introduce irregular
- Was/were are often easier - start there if needed

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on was/were and 5-6 regular verbs
- **Average students**: Regular verbs + common irregulars (go, have, do)
- **Advanced students**: Extended irregular verb list, storytelling, writing

### Connection to Previous Lessons:
- Uses daily routine vocabulary from Lesson 13 (but in past!)
- Time expressions build on previous time vocabulary
- Can discuss past hobbies, family activities, etc.

### Future Lessons Connection:
- Next lesson introduces future tense (going to)
- Past + future = telling complete life stories
- Foundation for narrative writing
