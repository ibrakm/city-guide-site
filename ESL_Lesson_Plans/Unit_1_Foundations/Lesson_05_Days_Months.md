# Lesson 5: Days of the Week & Months

**Level**: A1  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Days of the Week, Months, and Dates  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Name all 7 days of the week in order
- Name all 12 months of the year
- Say and write dates correctly
- Ask and answer "What day is it?" and "When is your birthday?"
- Talk about weekly routines using days

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Calendar (or draw one on board)
- Students' own knowledge of birthdays

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning, everyone! Quick question: What day is today? [Students may answer in Arabic/French]. Yes! Today is [day]. But how do we say it in English? Today you will learn ALL the days of the week AND all the months of the year! These are very important for talking about time, schedules, birthdays, and plans!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Think about your favorite day of the week. Is it Friday because there's no school? Is it Saturday because you can relax? Think about YOUR favorite day!"

[Give thinking time]

**Teacher:** "OK, some of you like Friday, some like Saturday... In English, we will learn to say: 'My favorite day is Friday' or 'I love Saturday!'"

**Question 2:**
"When is your birthday? What month? Some of you have birthdays in summer, some in winter... We will learn to say this in English!"

[Let 2-3 students share their birthday months in Arabic/French]

**Question 3:**
"How many days are in one week? Let's count together on our fingers!"

[Count together: "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven! Seven days!"]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's think! What do you do on different days? I will write days on the board, and you tell me activities!"

**Teacher writes:**
- Saturday: ?
- Sunday: ?
- Monday: ?
- Friday: ?

[Students suggest: school, sleep, play, study, go out, etc.]

**Teacher says:**
"Great! Now let's learn the English names for ALL the days!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Why do we have 7 days in a week? Why not 5 days? Or 10 days? What do you think?"

[Accept creative answers. Then explain:]

**Teacher guides:**
"Many cultures use 7 days because of old traditions. In English, some day names come from old gods and planets! For example, 'Sunday' = Sun day, 'Monday' = Moon day! Interesting, right?"

---

### 2. VOCABULARY & READING (10-12 minutes)

#### Complete Vocabulary List: Days of the Week

**Teacher says:**
"Now I will teach you the 7 days of the week. In English, we ALWAYS write day names with a CAPITAL letter! Listen and repeat."

| Day | Pronunciation | Abbreviation |
|-----|---------------|--------------|
| Monday | /ˈmʌndeɪ/ | Mon. |
| Tuesday | /ˈtjuːzdeɪ/ | Tue. / Tues. |
| Wednesday | /ˈwenzdeɪ/ | Wed. |
| Thursday | /ˈθɜːzdeɪ/ | Thu. / Thur. / Thurs. |
| Friday | /ˈfraɪdeɪ/ | Fri. |
| Saturday | /ˈsætədeɪ/ | Sat. |
| Sunday | /ˈsʌndeɪ/ | Sun. |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "Listen carefully. MONDAY. Repeat: MONDAY."  
**Students:** "Monday."  
**Teacher:** "MONDAY is the first day of the week in English. After Sunday, we start Monday. Repeat: MONDAY."  
**Students:** "Monday."

[Continue with each day. Repeat each day 3 times]

**Special Pronunciation Notes:**

**Teacher emphasizes:**
"Listen carefully to WEDNESDAY! We spell it W-E-D-N-E-S-D-A-Y, but we say 'WENZ-day,' not 'wed-nes-day.' The 'D' is silent!

And THURSDAY: we say 'THURZ-day,' with the 'TH' sound - tongue between teeth!"

[Practice Wednesday and Thursday 5 extra times]

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's say all 7 days in order. Follow me!"

[Say together rhythmically, like a chant:]
"Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday!"

[Repeat 5 times, faster each time]

#### Complete Vocabulary List: Months of the Year

**Teacher says:**
"Now, the 12 months! Remember: CAPITAL letters for months too!"

| Month | Pronunciation | Abbreviation | Days |
|-------|---------------|--------------|------|
| January | /ˈdʒænjuəri/ | Jan. | 31 |
| February | /ˈfebruəri/ | Feb. | 28/29 |
| March | /mɑːtʃ/ | Mar. | 31 |
| April | /ˈeɪprəl/ | Apr. | 30 |
| May | /meɪ/ | May | 31 |
| June | /dʒuːn/ | Jun. | 30 |
| July | /dʒuˈlaɪ/ | Jul. | 31 |
| August | /ˈɔːɡəst/ | Aug. | 31 |
| September | /sepˈtembə/ | Sep. / Sept. | 30 |
| October | /ɒkˈtəʊbə/ | Oct. | 31 |
| November | /nəʊˈvembə/ | Nov. | 30 |
| December | /dɪˈsembə/ | Dec. | 31 |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "JANUARY. This is the first month of the year. Repeat: JANUARY."  
**Students:** "January."

[Continue with each month, repeating 2 times each]

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's say all 12 months in order!"

[Say together, rhythmically:]
"January, February, March, April, May, June, July, August, September, October, November, December!"

[Repeat 3 times]

**Memory Tip:**

**Teacher says:**
"To remember which months have 30 or 31 days, we have a rhyme:
'Thirty days has September, April, June, and November. All the rest have thirty-one, except February alone!'"

[Teach the rhyme, repeat together 2 times]

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read about someone's week. Listen first."

**Text: "My Busy Week"**

```
My name is Salma. I am 13 years old. Let me tell you about my week!

On Monday, I go to school. Monday is hard because I have math and science.
On Tuesday, I have English class. I love Tuesday! On Wednesday, I have art 
class in the afternoon. Thursday is my busiest day - I have six subjects!
On Friday, I finish school at 2 PM. I go to the mosque with my family.
On Saturday, I relax at home and do homework. On Sunday, I visit my 
grandmother. I love weekends!

My birthday is in July. July is a summer month. When is your birthday?
```

**Teacher reads slowly. Then says:**
"Let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "Why does Salma love Tuesday?"  
**Answer:** "Because she has English class."

**Q2:** "What day is Salma's busiest day?"  
**Answer:** "Thursday is her busiest day."

**Q3:** "What does Salma do on Friday afternoon?"  
**Answer:** "She goes to the mosque with her family."

**Q4:** "What does Salma do on Sunday?"  
**Answer:** "She visits her grandmother."

**Q5:** "What month is Salma's birthday?"  
**Answer:** "Her birthday is in July."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "What is YOUR busiest day of the week? Why?"

[Let 3-4 students answer]

**Q2:** "What is your favorite day? Why?"

[Encourage full sentences: "My favorite day is... because..."]

**Q3:** "What month is your birthday? Is it in summer, winter, spring, or fall?"

[Let several students share]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! One person reads the text. The other listens. Then, ask each other:
1. What's your busiest day?
2. What's your favorite day?
3. When is your birthday?

You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: What day is it (today)?
Answer: It's [day].
Example: What day is it? - It's Monday.

PATTERN 2: On [day], I [activity].
Example: On Friday, I go to the mosque.

PATTERN 3: My birthday is in [month].
Example: My birthday is in March.

PATTERN 4: My birthday is on [month + date].
Example: My birthday is on March 15th.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"We use 'ON' with days:
- On Monday, On Friday, On the weekend

We use 'IN' with months:
- In January, In July, In summer

When we say dates, we write: March 15 or March 15th
We SAY: 'March fifteenth' or 'the fifteenth of March'"

#### Date Format Examples

**Teacher writes:**

**Written Format:**
- January 1, 2025 OR 1st January 2025
- July 20, 2024 OR 20th July 2024

**Spoken Format:**
- "January first, twenty twenty-five"
- "July twentieth, twenty twenty-four"

**Ordinal Numbers for Dates (1st-31st):**

**Teacher says:**
"For dates, we use special numbers:
- 1st = first
- 2nd = second
- 3rd = third
- 4th = fourth
- 5th = fifth
...
- 21st = twenty-first
- 22nd = twenty-second
- 30th = thirtieth
- 31st = thirty-first"

[Write 1st-10th on board, students repeat]

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes:**

1. "Today is Wednesday, October 2nd."
2. "On Monday, I study English."
3. "My birthday is in December."
4. "My birthday is on December 25th."
5. "I love Fridays because there's no school tomorrow!"

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat each sentence]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Let's learn a conversation about days and birthdays. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "When's Your Birthday?"**

```
A: Hi! What day is today?
B: Today is Thursday.
A: Oh, good! Tomorrow is Friday!
B: Yes! I love Fridays. What's your favorite day?
A: My favorite day is Saturday. I can sleep late!
B: Ha ha! Me too! By the way, when is your birthday?
A: My birthday is in June. June 10th. What about you?
B: Mine is in March. March 22nd.
A: Cool! March is a nice month.
B: Thanks! June is nice too - it's summer!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts**
2. **Teacher = A, Students = B (together)**
3. **Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice with REAL information about their birthdays**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice with your partner. Use YOUR real favorite day and YOUR real birthday! Make it personal! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor and help]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs to perform:**

"Who wants to show their dialogue? Come up!"

[Praise: "Excellent pronunciation!" / "Good job remembering your partner's birthday!"]

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Days in Order

**Teacher says:**
"Fill in the missing days of the week!"

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. Monday, Tuesday, __________, Thursday
2. Friday, __________, Sunday
3. __________, Monday, Tuesday
4. Wednesday, __________, Friday, __________

**Answers:**
1. Wednesday
2. Saturday
3. Sunday
4. Thursday, Saturday

#### Exercise 2: Months in Order

**Teacher says:**
"Fill in the missing months!"

**Teacher writes:**

1. January, February, __________, April
2. __________, August, September
3. October, __________, December
4. May, June, __________

**Answers:**
1. March
2. July
3. November
4. July

#### Exercise 3: Complete the Sentences

**Teacher says:**
"Complete these sentences about YOU!"

**Teacher writes:**

1. Today is __________.
2. My favorite day is __________.
3. My birthday is in __________.
4. My birthday is on __________ [date].
5. On __________, I go to school.

[Students write their own answers, then share]

**Teacher asks 6-7 students to read their sentences**

#### Exercise 4: Match the Activity to the Day

**Teacher says:**
"I will tell you activities. You tell me what day Moroccans usually do this!"

**Teacher asks:**

1. "What day do many people go to the mosque for Jumu'ah prayer?"  
   **Answer:** "Friday."

2. "What day do Moroccan schools usually close?"  
   **Answer:** "Friday afternoon and Saturday." [accept either]

3. "What day is the start of the school/work week?"  
   **Answer:** "Monday."

4. "What days are the weekend in Morocco?"  
   **Answer:** "Saturday and Sunday."

#### PAIR-WORK Calendar Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work with your partner! Ask each other:
- What's your favorite day and why?
- What month is your birthday?
- What's your favorite month and why?
- Name 3 things you do on Mondays.

You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Class Birthday Calendar"

**Teacher says:**
"Now we create a CLASS BIRTHDAY CALENDAR! Everyone will share their birthday, and we will write all birthdays on the board!"

**Activity Setup (2 minutes):**

**Teacher draws on board:**

```
JANUARY: [students' names]
FEBRUARY: [students' names]
MARCH: [students' names]
...
```

**Teacher says:**
"One by one, come tell me: 'My birthday is on [month + date].' I will write your name under that month. Then, we will see which month has the most birthdays!"

**Activity (5 minutes):**

[Each student comes to the front and says their birthday]

**Student example:** "My birthday is on April 12th."
**Teacher writes:** "Ahmed - April 12"

[Continue for all students]

**Follow-up Analysis (3 minutes):**

**Teacher asks:**

1. "Which month has the MOST birthdays in our class?"
2. "Which month has NO birthdays?"
3. "Who has a birthday in the same month? Raise your hands!"
4. "Who has a summer birthday? Winter birthday?"

[Discuss together]

#### Follow-up Prompts

- "How do you celebrate your birthday?"
- "Do you have a birthday party?"
- "What's the best birthday gift you ever got?"

#### Extension: "Weekly Schedule Sharing"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, tell your partner about your week! Use: 'On Monday, I... On Tuesday, I...' Talk about your real school schedule!"

**Example:**
"On Monday, I have math and science. On Tuesday, I have English and Arabic. On Wednesday, I have PE. On Thursday, I have geography. On Friday, I finish school early. On Saturday, I rest. On Sunday, I study."

[Give 3 minutes for pair sharing]

### 6. WRAP-UP & MINI-TEST (5 minutes)

#### Mini-Test Questions

**Teacher says:**
"Quick test! Answer clearly!"

**Q1:** "Say all 7 days of the week in order. Go!"  
**Expected Answer:** [Student recites: Monday through Sunday]

[Ask 2 students]

**Q2:** "What day is today?"  
**Expected Answer:** "Today is [current day]."

**Q3:** "When is your birthday? Use a full sentence."  
**Expected Answer:** "My birthday is on [month + date]." OR "My birthday is in [month]."

**Q4:** "Name the 12 months in order. You can do it!"  
**Expected Answer:** [Student recites January through December]

[Ask 1-2 confident students]

**Q5:** "How do you spell 'Wednesday'?"  
**Expected Answer:** "W-E-D-N-E-S-D-A-Y."

[Ask 1 student]

#### Quick Written Test

**Teacher says:**
"Write down today's date in English! Write: [Day], [Month] [Number]."

**Example:** "Thursday, October 2nd"

[Students write, then check together]

#### Reflection Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Today we learned days and months. When will you use these words in real life?"

[Accept: talking about plans, birthdays, schedules, appointments, holidays, etc.]

**Teacher concludes:**
"Exactly! Days and months help you organize your life and make plans. Now you can talk about time in English! Great job today!"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Keep days and months visible on board throughout lesson
- Use a real calendar or draw one on the board for visual reference
- Encourage daily use: start each class with "What day is today?"
- Make the birthday calendar activity fun and inclusive

### Common Errors to Address:
- Pronouncing Wednesday as "wed-nes-day" → Correct: "WENZ-day"
- Forgetting capital letters for days and months
- Confusing "in" and "on" → ON days, IN months
- Wrong date format: saying "March 15" as "March fifteen" → Correct: "March fifteenth"

### Cultural Notes:
- Friday (Jumu'ah) is special in Morocco - acknowledge this
- Moroccan weekend (Saturday-Sunday)
- Birthday celebrations may vary by family
- Islamic calendar months can be referenced for advanced students

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on 7 days and 6-8 most important months
- **Average students**: All days and months, simple dates
- **Advanced students**: Complete all challenges, including complex date formats and detailed routines
