# Lesson 6: Weather & Seasons

**Level**: A1  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Weather Conditions and Four Seasons  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Name the four seasons
- Describe 10+ weather conditions
- Ask and answer "What's the weather like?"
- Talk about weather in different seasons
- Use weather vocabulary in context

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Pictures of weather/seasons (optional)
- Window view (to describe current weather)

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Hello, everyone! Look outside the window. What do you see? Is it sunny? Cloudy? Hot? Cold? Today we learn about WEATHER and SEASONS! Weather is very important. It changes how we dress, what we do, and how we feel. Let's start!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Look outside right now. How is the weather today? Is it hot or cold? Sunny or cloudy? Try to describe it!"

[Let students try in English or Arabic/French]

**Teacher:** "Good! Today I will teach you how to say all of this in English!"

**Question 2:**
"Think about summer in Morocco. What is the weather like? Is it hot or cold?"

[Students respond: hot]

**Teacher:** "Yes! Very hot! And winter? What about winter in Morocco?"

[Students respond: cold/cool]

**Question 3:**
"What is your favorite season? Summer? Winter? Spring? Autumn? Think about which one you like best and why!"

[Give thinking time]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What words do you know about weather? In any language! I will write them on the board in English."

[Write student suggestions. Add these if not mentioned:]
- Sun, rain, hot, cold, wind, snow, cloud, storm

**Teacher says:**
"Great start! We already have [number] weather words! Today we will learn many more!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"How does weather affect your life? What happens when it rains? What happens when it's very hot? Give me examples!"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: we stay inside when it rains, we go to beach when hot, we wear jacket when cold, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Exactly! Weather controls many things: our clothes, our activities, our food, even our mood! That's why weather vocabulary is so important!"

### 2. VOCABULARY & READING (10-12 minutes)

#### Complete Vocabulary List: Seasons

**Teacher says:**
"First, let's learn the four seasons! In English, we write season names with capital letters."

| Season | Pronunciation | Months | Typical Weather |
|--------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Spring | /sprɪŋ/ | March-May | Warm, flowers bloom |
| Summer | /ˈsʌmə/ | June-August | Hot, sunny, dry |
| Autumn/Fall | /ˈɔːtəm/ /fɔːl/ | September-November | Cool, leaves fall |
| Winter | /ˈwɪntə/ | December-February | Cold, sometimes rain |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "SPRING. This is when flowers grow and weather becomes warm. Repeat: SPRING."  
**Students:** "Spring."

**Teacher:** "SUMMER. This is the hottest season! Repeat: SUMMER."  
**Students:** "Summer."

**Teacher:** "AUTUMN or FALL. We can say both! This is when leaves fall from trees. Repeat: AUTUMN."  
**Students:** "Autumn."

**Teacher:** "WINTER. This is the coldest season. Repeat: WINTER."  
**Students:** "Winter."

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's say all four seasons together!"

[Repeat together 3 times: "Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter"]

"Now, let's learn weather words! I will show you, and you repeat."

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher demonstrates each:**

"SUNNY - [Point to sun or draw sun, make happy face] - It's sunny! Repeat!"  
**Students:** "It's sunny."

"RAINY - [Make rain motion with fingers] - It's rainy! Repeat!"  
**Students:** "It's rainy."

[Continue with each weather word, with gestures]

#### Additional Weather Expressions

**Teacher writes on board:**

- What's the weather like? (asking about weather)
- The weather is nice/bad/terrible/beautiful.
- It's raining. / It's snowing.
- The sun is shining.
- There are clouds in the sky.

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read about weather in Morocco. Listen first."

**Text: "Weather in Morocco Through the Seasons"**

```
Morocco has four seasons, and each season is different! 

In spring (March to May), the weather is warm and pleasant. Flowers bloom 
everywhere. Sometimes it rains, but not too much. Spring is beautiful!

In summer (June to August), Morocco is very hot! It's sunny every day. 
In cities like Marrakech, it's sometimes 40 degrees! People go to the 
beach or mountains to escape the heat. Summer is dry with no rain.

In autumn (September to November), the weather becomes cooler. The hot 
summer ends. Some days are warm, some are cool. It's a comfortable season!

In winter (December to February), it's cold, especially at night. In the 
mountains, it snows! In cities, it sometimes rains. People wear jackets 
and warm clothes. Winter in Morocco is mild compared to Europe.

I love all four seasons in Morocco! Each season is special!
```

**Teacher reads slowly. Then says:**
"Let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "What is the weather like in spring in Morocco?"  
**Answer:** "It's warm and pleasant. Sometimes it rains."

**Q2:** "Which season is the hottest?"  
**Answer:** "Summer is the hottest season."

**Q3:** "Does it rain in summer in Morocco?"  
**Answer:** "No, summer is dry with no rain."

**Q4:** "What happens in the mountains in winter?"  
**Answer:** "It snows in the mountains."

**Q5:** "According to the text, how is Moroccan winter compared to Europe?"  
**Answer:** "It's mild compared to Europe."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "What is YOUR favorite season? Why?"

[Let 4-5 students answer with reasons]

**Q2:** "What do you do in summer when it's very hot?"

[Accept: go to beach, stay inside, drink cold water, swim, etc.]

**Q3:** "Have you ever seen snow? Where?"

[Let students share experiences]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! Take turns reading the text. Then discuss:
1. Which season do you like best?
2. What do you wear in each season?
3. What activities do you do in different seasons?

You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: What's the weather like?
Answer: It's [weather condition].
Example: What's the weather like? - It's sunny and hot.

PATTERN 2: It's [weather adjective].
Example: It's rainy. / It's cold. / It's windy.

PATTERN 3: It's [verb]-ing.
Example: It's raining. / It's snowing.

PATTERN 4: In [season], the weather is [description].
Example: In summer, the weather is hot and dry.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"When we describe weather, we usually start with 'IT'S...'

We can say:
- 'It's sunny.' (adjective)
- 'It's raining.' (verb + -ing)
- 'It's a beautiful day!' (noun phrase)

We use 'IN' with seasons: in spring, in summer, in autumn, in winter."

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and explains:**

1. "It's sunny and warm today. Perfect weather!"
2. "What's the weather like in Casablanca? - It's hot and humid."
3. "In winter, it's cold and sometimes rainy."
4. "It's raining cats and dogs!" (idiom = raining very heavily)
5. "I love summer because the weather is always nice."
6. "It's freezing outside! Wear a warm jacket!"

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Let's learn a weather conversation. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "Planning the Weekend"**

```
A: Hi! What are you doing this weekend?
B: I'm not sure yet. What's the weather forecast?
A: I checked! Saturday will be sunny and warm.
B: Perfect! Let's go to the beach!
A: Great idea! What about Sunday?
B: Sunday will be cloudy and maybe rainy.
A: Oh no! Then let's stay home on Sunday.
B: Yeah. We can watch movies and relax.
A: Sounds good! I hope Saturday stays sunny!
B: Me too! I love sunny days!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts**
2. **Teacher = A, Students = B (together)**
3. **Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice, then create their own plans based on weather**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice with your partner. Change the weather and the activities! What will you do if it's rainy? Sunny? Cold? Be creative! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor and help with vocabulary]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs to perform:**

"Who wants to show their dialogue? Come up!"

[Praise: "Creative idea!" / "Good use of weather words!"]

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Match Season to Weather

**Teacher says:**
"Match the season to the typical weather in Morocco!"

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. Spring → a) Very hot and dry
2. Summer → b) Cold and sometimes rainy
3. Autumn → c) Warm with some rain
4. Winter → d) Cool and comfortable

**Answers:**
1. c
2. a
3. d
4. b

#### Exercise 2: Complete the Sentences

**Teacher says:**
"Fill in the blanks with weather words!"

**Teacher writes:**

1. In summer, it's very _____ in Morocco. (hot/sunny)
2. When it's _____, I need an umbrella. (rainy/raining)
3. In winter, it's _____ at night. (cold/cool)
4. I love _____ days because I can see the sun! (sunny)
5. It's _____ today. The wind is strong! (windy)
6. In the mountains, it's _____ in winter. (snowy/freezing)

[Students write answers, then check together]

**Possible Answers:**
1. hot/sunny
2. rainy/raining
3. cold
4. sunny
5. windy
6. snowy/freezing

#### Exercise 3: Describe Current Weather

**Teacher says:**
"Look outside! Describe today's weather using 3-4 weather words. Write a sentence!"

**Example:** "Today is sunny and warm. It's a beautiful day!"

[Students write their own sentences, then share]

**Teacher asks 5-6 students to read their sentences**

#### Exercise 4: Season Activities

**Teacher says:**
"What activities do people do in each season? I'll say a season, you tell me activities!"

**Teacher asks:**

1. "What do people do in summer?"  
   **Answers:** "Go to the beach, swim, wear light clothes, drink cold water, eat ice cream..."

2. "What do people do in winter?"  
   **Answers:** "Wear warm clothes, stay inside, drink hot tea, wear jackets..."

3. "What do people do in spring?"  
   **Answers:** "Look at flowers, go on picnics, enjoy nice weather..."

4. "What do people do in autumn?"  
   **Answers:** "Go back to school, wear medium clothes, prepare for winter..."

#### PAIR-WORK Weather Interview

**Teacher says:**
"Interview your partner! Ask these questions and write their answers:
1. What's your favorite season? Why?
2. What's your least favorite season? Why?
3. Do you like rainy days or sunny days?
4. What do you do when the weather is bad?

You have 3 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Weather Reporter"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, you will be WEATHER REPORTERS! Like on TV! You will report the weather for different Moroccan cities!"

**Activity Setup (2 minutes):**

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
Cities:
- Casablanca
- Marrakech  
- Fes
- Tangier
- Agadir

Weather conditions:
[Students can choose or teacher assigns]
```

**Teacher demonstrates:**

"Good morning! This is the weather report! In Casablanca, it's cloudy and cool today. The temperature is 20 degrees. In Marrakech, it's sunny and very hot - 35 degrees! In Fes, it's rainy this morning. Bring an umbrella! That's all for today's weather. Have a great day!"

**Activity (5 minutes):**

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! One person is the weather reporter. Report the weather for 3-4 Moroccan cities. The other person listens and asks one question at the end. Then switch roles! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs, help with vocabulary]

#### Follow-up Reporting

**Teacher invites 3 students to give weather reports to the class:**

"Who wants to be our weather reporter? Come to the front!"

[Student reports, class listens]

**Teacher:** "Excellent report! Very professional!"

#### Extension: "Weather and Clothes"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, new activity! I will say weather conditions. You tell me what clothes to wear!"

**Teacher asks:**

1. "It's sunny and hot. What should I wear?"  
   **Answers:** "T-shirt, shorts, sandals, sunglasses, hat..."

2. "It's rainy and cold. What should I wear?"  
   **Answers:** "Jacket, boots, umbrella, long pants..."

3. "It's freezing! What should I wear?"  
   **Answers:** "Heavy coat, scarf, gloves, warm hat, boots..."

[Do 4-5 examples with full class participation]

### 7. HOMEWORK (Optional)

#### Option A - Writing Task (80-100 words)

**Teacher writes on board:**

"Write about your favorite season in Morocco. Include:
- Which season it is
- What the weather is like
- What you wear
- What activities you do
- Why you like it"

**Example:**
```
My favorite season is spring. In spring, the weather in Morocco is beautiful! 
It's warm and sunny, but not too hot. Sometimes it rains a little, and flowers 
grow everywhere. The weather is perfect - not hot like summer, not cold like 
winter. In spring, I wear light clothes like T-shirts and jeans. I don't need 
a heavy jacket. I love going to the park in spring and looking at all the 
flowers. My family sometimes has picnics outside. The air is fresh and clean. 
Spring makes me happy! I wish it could be spring all year!
```

#### Option B - Recording Task

**Teacher says:**
"Record yourself. You are a weather reporter! Give a weather report for 5 Moroccan cities. For each city, say:
- The city name
- The weather condition
- The temperature (if you know it)
- What people should wear or do

Make it sound like a real TV weather report! Record for 1-2 minutes."

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Weather Idioms

**Teacher says:**
"English has many idioms about weather! Learn these and use them in sentences:"

**Idioms:**
1. "It's raining cats and dogs" = Raining very heavily
2. "Under the weather" = Feeling sick
3. "Break the ice" = Start a conversation with someone new
4. "Every cloud has a silver lining" = Every bad situation has something good
5. "Save it for a rainy day" = Save something for when you need it later

**Task:** Write 5 sentences using these idioms!

**Example:** "Yesterday it was raining cats and dogs! I got completely wet!"

#### Challenge 2: Weather in Different Countries

**Teacher says:**
"Research and write about weather in 3 different countries. Compare with Morocco."

**Example:**
```
Weather in England: In England, it rains a lot! Even in summer, it's often 
cloudy and rainy. It's not very hot. Winter is cold and wet. England has 
more rain than Morocco!

Weather in Egypt: In Egypt, it's very hot and dry, like Morocco. But Egypt 
has even less rain! It's sunny most of the year. The desert is very hot!

Weather in Norway: In Norway, winter is very, very cold! It snows a lot. 
Summer is cool and pleasant. Norway is much colder than Morocco!
```

#### Challenge 3: Season Poem

**Teacher says:**
"Write a short poem (4-8 lines) about your favorite season!"

**Example:**
```
Summer, summer, hot and bright,
Sunny days and warm nights,
Swimming, playing, having fun,
Dancing under the golden sun,
Ice cream melting in my hand,
Building castles in the sand,
Summer, summer, don't go away,
I want you here every day!
```

#### Challenge 4: Weather Survey

**Teacher says:**
"Survey 5 people (family or classmates). Ask:
1. What's your favorite season?
2. What's your favorite weather?
3. Do you prefer hot or cold weather?

Write a report with the results. Which season is most popular?"

#### Challenge 5: Climate Change Discussion

**Teacher says:**
"Advanced topic: Climate change. Write 6-8 sentences answering:
- What is climate change?
- How is weather changing in Morocco?
- What can we do to help?"

**Example:**
```
Climate change is when Earth's weather patterns change over time. The planet 
is getting warmer. In Morocco, summers are getting hotter and longer. There 
is less rain in some areas. This affects farmers and water supply. We can 
help by saving water, using less electricity, planting trees, and recycling. 
Everyone must work together to protect our planet. Weather is important for 
all living things!
```

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Use gestures and actions for weather words (this makes it memorable!)
- Reference current weather outside whenever possible
- Encourage students to check weather forecasts in English
- Display season/weather vocabulary posters if available

### Common Errors to Address:
- Saying "The weather is sun" ❌ → "It's sunny" ✅
- Confusing "It's hot" (weather) with "I'm hot" (personal feeling)
- Forgetting "It's..." at the start of weather sentences
- Mixing up seasons with weather conditions

### Cultural Notes:
- Moroccan climate varies greatly (coastal vs. mountain vs. desert)
- Snow in Atlas Mountains is relevant to many students
- Summer heat in cities like Marrakech (can exceed 40°C)
- Lack of rain in summer months
- Regional differences in weather across Morocco

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on basic weather words (sunny, rainy, hot, cold) and four seasons
- **Average students**: All weather vocabulary, can describe weather in seasons
- **Advanced students**: Complete all challenges, including idioms and climate discussions

### Connection to Previous Lessons:
- Review numbers (temperatures)
- Review colors (gray clouds, blue sky, white snow)
- Review months/seasons from Lesson 5
