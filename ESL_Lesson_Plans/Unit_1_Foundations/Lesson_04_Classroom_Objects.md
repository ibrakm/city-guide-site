# Lesson 4: Classroom Objects & Instructions

**Level**: A1  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Classroom Objects and Instructions  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Name 20+ classroom objects in English
- Use "I have / I need / Can I borrow...?"
- Understand and follow classroom instructions
- Ask for permission and make requests in class

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Real classroom objects (students' own materials)
- Flashcards of classroom objects (optional)

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning, class! Look around this classroom. What do you see? We have desks, chairs, boards, books... so many things! Today, we will learn to name ALL classroom objects in English. This is very important because you use these things every day!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Touch something on your desk right now. Don't tell me what it is yet! Just touch it."

[Students touch objects]

**Teacher:** "Now, in your language, what is it called? [Student says in Arabic/French]. OK, today you will learn the English word!"

**Question 2:**
"Look in your school bag. How many different things do you have? Count them quickly!"

[Let students count]

**Teacher:** "Who has more than 5 things? Raise your hand! Who has more than 10? You have many things! Let's learn their English names!"

**Question 3:**
"What is the most important thing in your bag? Why?"

[Accept answers like: notebook (to write), pen (to do homework), book (to study), etc.]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's make a list! Tell me ANY object in this classroom. I will write it on the board. Even if you say it in Arabic or French, I will write the English word. Ready?"

[Write student suggestions on board with English words:]
- Chair, desk, board, pen, pencil, book, bag, door, window, clock, etc.

**Teacher says:**
"Excellent! We already have [number] words! Today we will learn 20 more!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Imagine you forget your pen at home. You're in English class. How do you ask for a pen in English? What do you say?"

[Give thinking time. Some may try: "Give me pen" or "I need pen"]

**Teacher guides:**
"Good try! In English, we say: 'Can I borrow a pen, please?' or 'Do you have an extra pen?' We will learn these polite phrases today!"

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read about a student's school bag. Listen first."

**Text: "What's in My School Bag?"**

```
My name is Hassan. I have a big blue school bag. Let me show you

pencils, one eraser, and one ruler. I also have a math book and an

```

**Teacher reads slowly. Then says:**
"Let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

**Teacher asks:**

[Let 3-4 students share]

**Q2:** "What is the heaviest thing in your bag? Why?"

[Encourage: "The heaviest thing is... because..."]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! Take turns reading the text. Then empty your pencil case or bag on your desk. Tell your partner what you have. Use: 'I have...' You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: I have a/an [object].
Example: I have a pen. / I have an eraser.

PATTERN 2: I need a/an [object].
Example: I need a pencil.

PATTERN 3: Can I borrow [object], please?
Example: Can I borrow your ruler, please?

PATTERN 4: Do you have an extra [object]?
Example: Do you have an extra pen?
```

**Teacher explains:**
"In English, we use 'a' or 'an' before singular objects:
- Use 'a' before consonant sounds: a pen, a book, a desk
- Use 'an' before vowel sounds: an eraser, an apple, an umbrella

When we want to borrow something, we ask POLITELY: 'Can I borrow...please?' We don't say 'Give me!' - that's rude!"

#### Classroom Instructions (Bonus Vocabulary)

**Teacher says:**
"Teachers give instructions in class. Let's learn important commands!"

**Teacher writes and demonstrates:**

| Instruction | Meaning | Action |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Open your book | Open your book | [Demonstrate opening book] |
| Close your book | Close your book | [Demonstrate closing book] |
| Take out your notebook | Get your notebook | [Demonstrate taking out notebook] |
| Put away your things | Pack up | [Demonstrate putting things away] |
| Stand up | Stand up | [Stand] |
| Sit down | Sit down | [Sit] |
| Listen carefully | Pay attention | [Cup ear] |
| Look at the board | Watch the board | [Point to board] |
| Raise your hand | Put hand up | [Raise hand] |
| Work in pairs | Work with partner | [Point to two students] |

**Teacher:** "Now, I will say the instruction, and you DO it! Ready?"

[Teacher says each instruction, students perform action - repeat 2-3 times]

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes:**

1. "I have a red notebook and three pens."
2. "I need an eraser. I made a mistake."
3. "Can I borrow your ruler, please? I forgot mine."
4. "Do you have an extra pencil? Mine is broken."
5. "Please open your books to page 25."

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat each sentence]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Let's learn a classroom conversation. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "Can I Borrow...?"**

```
Student A: Oh no! I forgot my pen.
Student B: What's wrong?
Student A: I don't have a pen. Can I borrow yours, please?
Student B: Sorry, I only have one pen. But I have an extra pencil.
Student A: That's OK. Can I borrow the pencil, please?
Student B: Sure! Here you are.
Student A: Thank you so much!
Student B: You're welcome. You can keep it.
Student A: Really? Thank you! You're very kind!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts**
2. **Teacher = A, Students = B (together)**
3. **Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice, then change objects (eraser, ruler, etc.)**

**Teacher says:**
"Practice with your partner. Change the object! Maybe you need an eraser, or a ruler, or scissors. Make it your own! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor and help]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs to perform:**

"Let's see your dialogues! Who wants to show?"

[Praise attempts: "Excellent politeness!" / "Very natural!"]

**Teacher says:**

- 'You erase mistakes with this. It's usually white or pink.' → Answer: 'Eraser!'

Take turns! Do 5 objects each. You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Lost and Found"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, we play 'Lost and Found'! Imagine you lost something in class, OR you found something and want to return it."

**Setup:**

**Teacher explains:**
"Close your eyes. I will take ONE object from 3-4 students' desks. Don't look!"

[Teacher takes 3-4 objects: a pen, eraser, ruler, etc.]

**Teacher says:**
"Open your eyes! If you notice something is missing, stand up and ask the class: 'Has anyone seen my [object]? I lost my [object].'

If you found something (I will give objects to different students), say: 'I found a [object]. Is this yours?'"

**Activity (6 minutes):**

**Teacher distributes taken objects to different students, then says:**

"Now, figure out who lost what! Ask around! Use English! Go!"

[Monitor as students circulate asking: "Did you lose a pen?" / "Is this your eraser?" / "I'm looking for my ruler."]

#### Follow-up Prompts

**Teacher uses while monitoring:**
- "Describe your object. What color is it?"
- "Where did you lose it?"
- "Thank the person who found it!"

#### Extension: "Classroom Commands Game"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's play Simon Says with classroom instructions! I will give commands. You follow them ONLY if I say 'Simon says' first. If I don't say 'Simon says,' don't do it! If you do it, you're out!"

**Teacher plays:**
- "Simon says, stand up!" [Students stand]
- "Simon says, take out your notebook!" [Students take out notebooks]
- "Sit down!" [Students should NOT sit - those who do are out]
- "Simon says, open your book!" [Students open books]
- "Close your book!" [Students should NOT close - those who do are out]

[Play for 3-4 minutes, then declare winners]

**Teacher concludes:**
"Exactly! You use these words EVERY DAY in school! Now you can talk about your classroom in English! Excellent work today!"

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Categories

**Teacher says:**
"Organize these classroom objects into categories. Create at least 3 categories."

**Objects:** pen, pencil, ruler, scissors, desk, chair, board, eraser, glue, book, notebook, door, window

**Example categories:**
- Writing tools: pen, pencil, eraser
- Furniture: desk, chair, board
- Cutting/sticking tools: scissors, glue
- Reading materials: book, notebook
- Room features: door, window

**Task:** Create your own categories and add more objects!

#### Challenge 2: Object Descriptions

**Teacher says:**
"Describe 5 classroom objects in detail. Include: color, size, use, and why it's important."

**Example:**
"A ruler is a long, thin object. It's usually 30 centimeters. My ruler is yellow and transparent. I use it to draw straight lines and measure things. It's important because I need it for math and geometry."

#### Challenge 3: Classroom Instructions Chain

**Teacher says:**
"Create a series of 8 classroom instructions for a partner to follow in order. Be creative!"

**Example:**
1. Stand up.
2. Walk to the board.
3. Pick up a marker.
4. Write your name on the board.
5. Put down the marker.
6. Turn around.
7. Wave to the class.
8. Go back to your seat and sit down.

**Task:** Write your instructions. Then test them on a partner!

#### Challenge 4: Mini-Dialogue Creation

**Teacher says:**
"Create a longer dialogue (10-12 lines) between two students. Include:
- Asking to borrow 2 different objects
- Describing objects

- Offering to share something"

#### Challenge 5: Classroom Problem Solving

**Teacher says:**
"Read these problems. What do you say in English?"

**Problems:**
1. You spilled water on your notebook. What do you tell the teacher?
2. Someone is sitting in your chair. What do you say politely?

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Use real objects throughout - show, touch, demonstrate
- Encourage students to use "please" and "thank you" consistently
- For Lost and Found game, ensure it stays organized - return objects quickly
- Practice classroom commands daily for reinforcement

### Common Errors to Address:
- Forgetting "please" when making requests
- Using "give me" instead of "can I borrow"
- Confusing a/an usage
- Saying "borrow me" instead of "can I borrow" or "lend me"

### Cultural Notes:
- In Morocco, sharing materials is common - emphasize this positively
- Respect for objects and keeping materials organized
- Politeness is valued in Moroccan culture - reinforce with "please" and "thank you"

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on 10 basic objects, simple "I have/I need" sentences
- **Average students**: All 22 objects, polite requests
- **Advanced students**: Complete all challenges, create complex dialogues
