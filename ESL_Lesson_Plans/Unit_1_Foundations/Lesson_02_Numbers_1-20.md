# Lesson 2: Numbers 1-20

**Level**: A1 (Absolute Beginner)  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Numbers 1-20  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Count from 1 to 20 in English
- Say and write numbers 1-20
- Ask and answer "How many...?" questions
- Use numbers in simple sentences about age, quantity, and phone numbers

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Students' own objects (pens, books, fingers for counting)
- Optional: Number flashcards 1-20

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning/afternoon, everyone! Today we are learning about NUMBERS in English. We use numbers every day! When do we use numbers? Think..."

[Pause for thinking time]

**Teacher continues:**
"Yes! We use numbers for: counting, telling age, phone numbers, prices, addresses, time, dates... Numbers are very important! Let's start!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Hold up your hands! Count your fingers with me in English. How many fingers do you have on both hands?"

[Count together: "One, two, three... ten!"]

**Teacher:** "Excellent! You have ten fingers. Now, how many students are in this classroom today? Let's count together!"

[Point to each student and count: "One student, two students, three students..."]

**Question 2:**
"How old are you? In Morocco, we say our age with numbers. In English, we say: 'I am [number] years old.' Can you guess my age? I am [teacher's age] years old."

[Ask 2-3 confident students: "How old are you?"]
[Help with: "I am... years old."]

**Question 3:**
"Look at your school bag. How many books do you have inside? Count them in your mind. Don't tell me yet!"

[Let students think/count]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! Where do we see numbers every day? I will write your ideas on the board. Who has an idea?"

[Write student suggestions. Add these if not mentioned:]
- Phone numbers
- Ages
- Prices (in shops)
- House numbers
- Bus numbers
- Classroom numbers
- Test scores
- Time

**Teacher says:**
"Wow! We use numbers everywhere! That's why we must learn them in English."

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Imagine you are in England or America. You cannot speak Arabic or French. You only know numbers in English. What can you do? How can numbers help you?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: buy things, tell age, ask for help, give phone number, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Exactly! Numbers are a universal language. Even if you don't speak much English, knowing numbers helps you survive!"

**Teaching Script:**

**Students:** "One."  
**Teacher:** "Again. ONE."

**Teacher:** "One more time. ONE."  
**Students:** "One."  

[Continue this pattern for ALL numbers 1-20]

**Special Pronunciation Notes (Teacher emphasizes):**

**Teacher says:**
"Listen carefully to these tricky sounds:
- THREE - Put your tongue between your teeth: 'th-th-three'
- THIRTEEN - This ends with '-teen': thir-TEEN
- FIFTEEN - This is FIF-teen, not 'five-teen'
- Listen to the difference: THIRTEEN (13) vs. THIRTY (30) - we will learn 30 next lesson!"

[Practice these difficult ones 5 times each]

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read a story with numbers. Listen first."

**Text: "My Day with Numbers"**

```
My name is Youssef. I am thirteen years old. I wake up at seven o'clock. 
I have two brothers and one sister. My older brother is sixteen years old. 
My younger sister is nine years old. I go to school five days a week. 
In my classroom, there are fourteen students. I have six books in my bag. 
My favorite number is seven because it's lucky. I have ten fingers and 
ten toes, just like everyone! Numbers are in my life every day.
```

**Teacher reads slowly and clearly. Then says:**
"Now, let's read together. Ready? One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "How old is Youssef?"  
**Answer:** "He is thirteen years old."

**Q2:** "How many brothers does Youssef have?"  
**Answer:** "He has two brothers."

**Q3:** "How old is Youssef's older brother?"  
**Answer:** "His older brother is sixteen years old."

**Q4:** "How many students are in Youssef's classroom?"  
**Answer:** "There are fourteen students."

**Q5:** "What is Youssef's favorite number?"  
**Answer:** "His favorite number is seven."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "How old are YOU? Use a full sentence: 'I am _____ years old.'"

[Ask 4-5 students]

**Q2:** "Do you have a favorite number? Why is it your favorite?"

[Encourage explanations: "My favorite number is 5 because..."]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Now, work in pairs! One person reads the text out loud. The other person listens. Then, ask each other the comprehension questions. You have 4 minutes. Go!"

[Monitor and help pairs. Ensure both partners take turns reading and asking]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: I am [number] years old.
Example: I am fourteen years old.

PATTERN 2: I have [number] [thing].
Example: I have three pens.

PATTERN 3: There are [number] [things].
Example: There are twelve students.

PATTERN 4: How many [things] ...?
Example: How many books do you have?
```

**Teacher explains:**
"In English, we use numbers with nouns (things). When we have MORE than one thing, we add 's' or 'es' to the noun. Look:
- One book → Two books
- One pen → Three pens
- One student → Ten students

We say: 'I have five books.' NOT 'I have five book.' The 's' is very important!"

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and explains:**

1. "I am twelve years old." (Talking about age)
2. "I have seven pencils." (Possessions - plural!)
3. "There are fifteen students in my class." (Quantity)
4. "My phone number has ten numbers." (Count)
5. "How many chairs are there?" (Question)

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's learn a conversation about numbers. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "Getting to Know You"**

```
A: Hi! What's your name?
B: My name is Salma. What's your name?
A: I'm Hassan. How old are you, Salma?
B: I'm fourteen years old. How old are you?
A: I'm thirteen. Do you have brothers or sisters?
B: Yes, I have one brother and two sisters. What about you?
A: I have one sister. She is ten years old.
B: Nice! How many students are in your class?
A: There are sixteen students. What about your class?
B: We have fifteen students.
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts (students listen)**
2. **Teacher = A, Students = B (all together)**
3. **Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Left side = A, Right side = B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice in pairs**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice with your partner. Use your REAL information - your real age, your real brothers and sisters, your real class! Make it true! You have 5 minutes."

[Monitor pairs. Help with numbers and grammar]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3-4 pairs to perform:**

"Who wants to show their dialogue? [Names], please come to the front and perform!"

[After each pair, praise: "Excellent! Good job using numbers!" / "Perfect pronunciation!"]

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Gap-Fill Exercise

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's practice numbers in sentences. Fill in the blank with the correct number IN WORDS. Write in your notebook."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. I have __________ fingers on one hand. (5)
2. There are __________ days in one week. (7)
3. I am __________ years old. (your age)
4. A dozen eggs is __________ eggs. (12)
5. Two weeks is __________ days. (14)
6. After nineteen comes __________. (20)

**Teacher says:**
"You have 4 minutes. Write the numbers as WORDS, not symbols! Then we check together."

[Give time, then check]

**Answer Key:**
1. five
2. seven
3. (varies: twelve, thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, etc.)
4. twelve
5. fourteen
6. twenty

#### Sentence Transformation

**Teacher says:**
"Now, I will give you numbers as digits (1, 2, 3...). You write them as words AND use them in a sentence about you!"

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. 2 → "I have __________ eyes."
2. 10 → "I have __________ fingers."
3. (your age) → "I am __________ years old."
4. (number of books) → "I have __________ books in my bag."
5. (number of friends) → "I have __________ friends."

**Example for #1:**
"I have TWO eyes."

**Teacher says:**
"Write all 5 sentences. You have 4 minutes."

[After time:]

**Teacher asks questions and students answer orally:**

"Maria, read your sentence number 1."
"Karim, what about sentence number 3?"

[Call on 6-8 students to share different sentences]

#### Quick Oral Practice

**Teacher says:**
"Now, I will point at objects in the room. You count them and answer! Ready?"

**Teacher points and asks:**

1. [Points to pens on desk] "How many pens are there?"  
   **Students:** "There are [number] pens."

2. [Points to students in row] "How many students are in this row?"  
   **Students:** "There are [number] students."

3. [Points to windows] "How many windows do we have?"  
   **Students:** "There are [number] windows."

4. [Holds up fingers] "How many fingers am I showing?"  
   **Students:** "You are showing [number] fingers."

#### PAIR-WORK Checking

**Teacher says:**
"Now, work with your partner. One person asks: 'How many pens do you have?' The other person counts and answers: 'I have [number] pens.' Take turns! Ask about:
- Pens
- Books
- Pencils
- Papers
- Bags

You have 3 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Class Survey"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, we do a class survey! You will walk around and ask 3 different classmates these questions. Write their answers in your notebook."

**Survey Questions:**
1. How old are you?
2. How many brothers do you have?
3. How many sisters do you have?
4. How many books are in your bag today?

**Teacher demonstrates:**
[Approaches a student]
"Excuse me, [name]. How old are you?"  
**Student:** "I am thirteen years old."  
**Teacher writes answer down.**  
"Thank you! Next question: How many brothers do you have?"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, YOU do this! Stand up, walk around, ask 3 different people. Write their names and answers. You have 5 minutes. Go!"

[Monitor the activity. Ensure students are speaking in English]

#### Follow-up Reporting

**Teacher says:**
"Sit down now. Let's share! Who wants to report?"

**Teacher asks:**
"[Name], who did you ask? What did you learn?"

[Student reports: "I asked Sara. She is fourteen years old. She has two brothers and one sister. She has six books in her bag."]

**Teacher:** "Excellent! Good sentences! Who else?"

[Call on 3-4 students to report]

#### Follow-up Prompts (Teacher uses during reporting)

- "Is that the same as you, or different?"
- "Who is the oldest person you asked?"
- "Who has the most brothers?"
- "How many total books did your 3 people have?"

#### Math Challenge Extension

**Teacher says:**
"Now, a challenge! Add up all the ages of the 3 people you asked. What is the total? Tell your partner!"

[Give 2 minutes for calculation and sharing]

### 6. WRAP-UP & MINI-TEST (5 minutes)

#### Mini-Test Questions

**Teacher says:**
"Test time! Answer these questions clearly. Use full sentences!"

**Q1:** "Count from 1 to 20 for me. Go!"  
**Expected Answer:** [Student counts 1-20]

[Ask 2 students]

**Q2:** "What number comes after fifteen?"  
**Expected Answer:** "Sixteen comes after fifteen." OR "Sixteen."

**Q3:** "Spell the number 7."  
**Expected Answer:** "S-E-V-E-N."

**Q4:** "How many students are in this classroom right now? Count and tell me."  
**Expected Answer:** "There are [number] students."

**Q5:** "I am [teacher's age] years old. How old are you?"  
**Expected Answer:** "I am [student's age] years old."

[Ask 6-8 different students different questions]

#### Quick Written Test (2 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Write these numbers as words in your notebook quickly!"

**Teacher says the numbers:**
1. 3
2. 11
3. 17
4. 20

[After 1 minute, check together]

**Answers:** three, eleven, seventeen, twenty

#### Reflection Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Today we learned numbers 1 to 20. When will you use these numbers in real life? Give me one example!"

[Accept answers like: telling age, buying things, phone numbers, addresses, etc.]

**Teacher concludes:**
"Yes! Numbers help you everywhere! Next lesson, we learn bigger numbers. Great work today!"

#### PAIR Testing Option

**Teacher says:**
"Turn to your partner. One person says a number in English (1-20). The other person writes it down as a WORD. Then switch! You have 1 minute!"

**Teacher writes on board:**

"Write about numbers in your life. Answer these questions:
- How old are you?
- How many people are in your family?
- How many rooms are in your house?
- How many friends do you have?
- What is your favorite number and why?"

**Example:**
```
My name is Leila. I am thirteen years old. I have four people in my family: 
my mother, my father, my brother, and me. My brother is ten years old. 
We have six rooms in our house. I have seven close friends at school.

```

#### Option B - Recording Task

**Teacher says:**
"Record yourself speaking. Say:
1. Your age
2. How many brothers and sisters you have
3. Count from 1 to 20
4. Your favorite number and why

Speak for at least 1 minute. Send me the recording."

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Number Patterns

**Teacher says to advanced students:**
"Look at these number patterns. What number comes next? Write the number in words."

1. 2, 4, 6, 8, ____ (ten)
2. 1, 3, 5, 7, ____ (nine)
3. 5, 10, 15, ____ (twenty)
4. 20, 18, 16, ____ (fourteen)

**Extension:** "Create your own number pattern and ask your partner to solve it!"

#### Challenge 2: Word Problems

**Teacher writes:**
"Solve these problems. Write your answer in words AND a full sentence."

1. "Sara has 8 pencils. Ahmed gives her 5 more pencils. How many pencils does Sara have now?"  
   **Answer:** "Sara has thirteen pencils now."

2. "There are 20 students in class. 3 students are absent today. How many students are in class?"  
   **Answer:** "There are seventeen students in class."

3. "Youssef is 12 years old. His brother is 4 years older. How old is his brother?"  
   **Answer:** "His brother is sixteen years old."

#### Challenge 3: Phone Number Practice

**Teacher says:**
"In English, we say phone numbers digit by digit. Practice saying these phone numbers:"

Example: 0612-345-678
We say: "zero, six, one, two... three, four, five... six, seven, eight"

**Task:** "Write your phone number (or a fake one). Practice saying it in English. Then say it to a partner. They write it down. Did they get it right?"

#### Challenge 4: Number Story

**Teacher says:**
"Write a short story (5-6 sentences) using at least 8 different numbers from 1-20."

**Example:**
```
My friend Fatima is fourteen years old. She has three cats and two dogs. 
She wakes up at six o'clock every morning. She walks to school in fifteen 
minutes. Her classroom is on the second floor. She has twelve subjects at 
school. Her favorite number is seven. She goes to bed at nine o'clock.
```

#### Challenge 5: Speed Challenge

**Teacher says:**
"Can you count from 1 to 20 in 10 seconds? Practice and try! Then, try counting BACKWARDS from 20 to 1!"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Write numbers 1-20 on the board at the start and keep them visible
- Use fingers, objects, and students themselves for counting demonstrations
- Encourage choral repetition - confidence builds with group practice
- For weaker students: Focus on 1-10 first, then gradually add 11-20
- Correct pronunciation gently and have the class repeat correct form

### Common Errors to Address:
- "Tree" instead of "three" → Show tongue placement
- "Tirteen" instead of "thirteen" → Emphasize the "th" sound
- Confusing "thirteen" (13) with "thirty" (30) → Stress the difference in sound
- Forgetting plural 's': "five book" ❌ → "five books" ✅
- Saying numbers in Arabic/French → Gently redirect to English

### Cultural Notes:
- Lucky number 7 is common in Moroccan culture - mention this!
- Number 5 (khomsa) has cultural significance - can reference
- Use familiar prices in dirhams for context (we'll use this next lesson)

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on 1-10 thoroughly, practice more slowly
- **Average students**: Master 1-20, use in simple sentences
- **Advanced students**: Complete all high achiever challenges, especially word problems

### Time Management:
- If running short, reduce mini-test to 3 questions
- If extra time, add more oral drilling (count by 2s, count backwards)

Students successfully completed this lesson if they can:
✅ Count from 1 to 20 with clear pronunciation
✅ Write at least 10 numbers as words (not digits)
✅ Say their age: "I am ___ years old"
✅ Use "How many...?" in questions
✅ Form plurals correctly with numbers (two books, five pens)
