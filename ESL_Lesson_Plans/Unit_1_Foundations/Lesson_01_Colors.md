# Lesson 1: Colors

**Level**: A1 (Absolute Beginner)  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Basic and Extended Colors  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Name 15+ colors in English
- Describe objects using color + noun
- Ask and answer "What color is...?"
- Express simple color preferences

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers (use colored markers if possible)
- Students' own objects (pens, bags, clothes)

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning/afternoon, everyone! Welcome to our first English lesson. Today we are going to learn about COLORS. Colors are everywhere! Look around the classroom. What do you see?"

[Wait for responses. Students may answer in Arabic/French initially]

**Teacher continues:**
"Yes! I see many things. Let me point. This is RED [point to something red]. This is BLUE [point to blue]. This is GREEN [point to green]. Colors help us describe the world!"

#### Warm-up Questions (Ask these one by one)

**Question 1:**
"Look at your clothes today. Point to one color you are wearing. Don't say it yet, just point!"

[Let students point. Then ask 2-3 students:]
"Ahmed, what color are you pointing to?" 
[Help: "It's... blue/red/green..."]

**Question 2:**
"Look around this classroom. How many RED things can you find? Let's count together!"

[Point and count together: "One red thing, two red things..."]

**Question 3:**
"What is your favorite color? Think about it... My favorite color is [teacher's choice]. What about you, Fatima?"

[Help weaker students: "My favorite color is..."]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's make a list! Tell me ANY color you know in English. I will write it on the board. Who wants to start?"

[Write student suggestions on board. Add these if not mentioned:]
- Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, Black, White, Pink, Orange, Purple, Brown

**Teacher says:**
"Excellent! We have [number] colors already!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Why are colors important in our life? Where do we use colors every day?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: clothes, flags, signs, food, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Yes! Colors help us choose clothes, find things, understand signs, enjoy food. Colors make life beautiful!"

#### Complete Vocabulary List

**Teacher says:**
"Now I will teach you 15 important colors. Listen and repeat after me."

| English | Pronunciation | Example |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| Red | /red/ | "The Moroccan flag is red" |
| Blue | /bluː/ | "The sky is blue" |
| Green | /griːn/ | "Mint tea leaves are green" |
| Yellow | /ˈjeləʊ/ | "Lemons are yellow" |
| Orange | /ˈɒrɪndʒ/ | "Oranges are orange" |
| Pink | /pɪŋk/ | "Some flowers are pink" |
| Purple | /ˈpɜːpl/ | "Grapes are purple" |
| Brown | /braʊn/ | "Wood is brown" |
| Black | /blæk/ | "The board is black" |
| White | /waɪt/ | "Paper is white" |
| Gray/Grey | /ɡreɪ/ | "Clouds are gray" |
| Gold | /ɡəʊld/ | "This ring is gold" |
| Silver | /ˈsɪlvə/ | "This coin is silver" |
| Light blue | /laɪt bluː/ | "Light blue is soft" |
| Dark green | /dɑːk griːn/ | "Dark green is strong" |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "Listen carefully. RED. Repeat: RED."  
**Students:** "Red."  
**Teacher:** "Good! RED. The Moroccan flag is RED. Repeat: The Moroccan flag is red."  
**Students:** "The Moroccan flag is red."

[Continue with each color, using the examples]

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read a short text about colors. First, I will read. Then, you read with me."

**Text: "Colors in My Classroom"**

```
My classroom is full of colors. The walls are white. The door is brown. 
The board is black. My bag is blue and red. My friend Sara has a green 
pencil case. Ahmed's jacket is orange. Our teacher has a yellow book. 
The chairs are gray. I love the pink poster on the wall. Colors make 
our classroom beautiful!
```

**Teacher reads slowly, then says:**
"Now, let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "What color are the walls?"  
**Answer:** "The walls are white."

**Q2:** "What color is the door?"  
**Answer:** "The door is brown."

**Q3:** "What color is the board?"  
**Answer:** "The board is black."

**Q4:** "What color is Sara's pencil case?"  
**Answer:** "Sara's pencil case is green."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "Is your classroom the same? What colors do you have in YOUR classroom?"

[Let 2-3 students answer]

**Q2:** "What is the prettiest color in this text? Why?"

[Encourage opinions]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Now, work in pairs! Sit with your partner. One person reads the text. The other person listens. Then, ask each other the comprehension questions. You have 3 minutes. Go!"

[Monitor and help pairs]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: The [object] is [color].
Example: The book is red.

PATTERN 2: I have a [color] [object].
Example: I have a blue pen.

PATTERN 3: What color is the [object]?
Answer: It is [color].
```

**Teacher explains:**
"In English, we describe things with colors. We say: 'The book is RED.' We DON'T say 'The red book is.' The color comes BEFORE the noun when we use 'a': 'a red book.' Let me show you..."

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and explains:**

1. "The sky is blue." (Subject + is + color)
2. "I have a green bag." (a + color + noun)
3. "Her eyes are brown." (Plural subject + are + color)
4. "What color is your phone?" (Question form)

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:
- The sky is blue.
- I have a green bag.
- Her eyes are brown.
- What color is your phone?"

[Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's learn a short conversation. I will be Person A. You all are Person B. Listen first."

**Dialogue:**

```
A: Hi! I like your bag. What color is it?
B: It's red and black.
A: Nice! Red is my favorite color.
B: Really? My favorite color is blue.
A: Blue is beautiful too!
B: Thank you!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts (students listen)**
2. **Teacher = A, Students = B (together)**
3. **Students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice in pairs**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice with your partner. Change the colors and objects. Make your own dialogue! You have 4 minutes."

[Monitor pairs. Help with vocabulary]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 2-3 pairs to perform for class:**

"Who wants to show their dialogue? [Name] and [Name], please come to the front."

[Praise all attempts: "Excellent work! Good pronunciation!"]

---

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Gap-Fill Exercise

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's practice! I will give you sentences with missing colors. Fill in the blank with a color. Write in your notebook."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. The Moroccan flag is __________ and green.
2. The sun is __________.
3. My school bag is __________.
4. Oranges are __________.
5. The night sky is __________.
6. My favorite color is __________.

**Teacher says:**
"You have 3 minutes. Write the answers. Then, we check together."

[Give time, then check]

**Answer Key:**
1. red
2. yellow
3. (student's own answer)
4. orange
5. black/dark blue
6. (student's own answer)

#### Sentence Completion

**Teacher says:**
"Now, complete these sentences about YOU. Use colors."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. My pen is __________.
2. My favorite color is __________.
3. My teacher's shirt is __________.
4. The classroom door is __________.
5. My friend's bag is __________.

**Teacher says:**
"Write your answers. Then, we will share."

[After 3 minutes:]

**Teacher asks:**
"Who wants to share sentence 1? [Name], what color is your pen?"

[Student answers: "My pen is blue."]

**Teacher:** "Perfect! Full sentence. Very good!"

[Ask 5-6 different students to share different sentences]

#### PAIR-WORK Checking

**Teacher says:**
"Now, check your answers with your partner. Read your sentences to each other. Are they different? That's okay! You have 2 minutes."

[Monitor pairs]

---

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Describing Objects"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, we will play a game! It's called 'What Color Is It?' One student describes an object in this room using ONLY the color. Other students guess the object!"

**Example:**
**Teacher:** "I see something. It is black. What is it?"  
**Students guess:** "The board!" / "Your pen!" / "The door!"  
**Teacher:** "Yes! It's the board. Good job!"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, YOU try! Work in groups of 3-4 students. Take turns. One person thinks of an object and says the color. Others guess. You have 5 minutes!"

[Divide class into groups of 3-4. Monitor each group]

#### Follow-up Prompts (Teacher uses while monitoring)

- "Why did you choose that color?"
- "Can you think of other [color] things?"
- "What's your second favorite color?"
- "Do you like wearing this color?"

#### Role-Play Extension

**Teacher says:**
"Now, new task! Imagine you are shopping. Work in pairs. One person is the SELLER, one is the CUSTOMER. The customer asks about colors."

**Example dialogue:**
```
Customer: Excuse me, do you have a blue bag?
Seller: Yes, I have blue, red, and black bags.
Customer: I like the blue one. How much?
Seller: It's 100 dirhams.
Customer: Thank you!
```

**Teacher:** "Practice this for 3 minutes. Change roles after 90 seconds!"

[Monitor and help]

---

### 6. WRAP-UP & MINI-TEST (5 minutes)

#### Mini-Test Questions

**Teacher says:**
"Now, quick test! Answer these questions. I will ask, you answer. Ready?"

**Q1:** "What color is the Moroccan flag?"  
**Expected Answer:** "It is red (and green)."

**Q2:** "Name 5 colors in English."  
**Expected Answer:** "Red, blue, green, yellow, black..." [etc.]

**Q3:** "Look at [student name]'s shirt. What color is it?"  
**Expected Answer:** "It is [color]."

**Q4:** "What is your favorite color? Make a full sentence."  
**Expected Answer:** "My favorite color is [color]."

[Ask 6-8 different students different questions]

#### Reflection Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Today we learned about colors. How can you use colors in your real life? Where will you use this English?"

[Accept answers like: shopping, describing things, art class, talking about clothes, etc.]

**Teacher concludes:**
"Excellent! Colors are everywhere. Now you can talk about them in English! Very good work today!"

#### PAIR Testing Option

**Teacher says (if time allows):**
"Turn to your partner. Ask them: 'What color is your bag?' and 'What is your favorite color?' You have 1 minute!"

### 7. HOMEWORK (Optional)

#### Option A - Writing Task (80-100 words)

**Teacher writes on board:**

"Write about colors in your home. Describe 5-6 things and their colors."

**Example:**
```
In my home, there are many colors. The walls are white. My bed is brown. 
I have a blue blanket. The kitchen table is black. My mother's favorite 
cup is red. The bathroom door is green. I love the colors in my home!
```

#### Option B - Recording Task

**Teacher says:**
"Record yourself speaking for 1 minute. Describe the colors you are wearing today and your favorite color. Send the recording to me."

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Extended Descriptions

**Teacher says to advanced students:**
"You must describe 3 objects in your house. Use colors AND explain why you like those colors."

**Example:**
"I have a blue pillow in my bedroom. I like blue because it is calm and peaceful. My mother has a red tajine pot. Red is a strong color. It makes me think of Morocco. I have a green plant in my room. Green is fresh and natural."

#### Challenge 2: Color Combinations

**Teacher writes:**
"Create sentences using TWO colors for one object."

**Examples:**
- "My bag is red and white."
- "The Moroccan flag is red and green."
- "My pencil case is blue, yellow, and black."

**Task:** "Write 5 sentences with color combinations."

#### Challenge 3: Color Feelings

**Teacher asks:**
"Some colors make us feel different things. What do these colors make you feel?"

- Red = ? (strong, excited, love)
- Blue = ? (calm, sad, peaceful)
- Green = ? (natural, fresh, peaceful)
- Yellow = ? (happy, bright, sunny)
- Black = ? (serious, elegant, night)

**Task:** "Write 5 sentences like: 'Red makes me feel strong.'"

#### Challenge 4: Mini-Presentation (1-2 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Prepare a short talk about your favorite color. Tell us:
1. What is your favorite color?
2. Why do you like it?
3. Give 3 examples of things with this color.
4. How does this color make you feel?"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Use colored objects around the room to make learning visual
- Encourage students to use colors in full sentences, not just one-word answers
- Praise all attempts, even if pronunciation is imperfect
- For weaker students: Allow pointing + saying color word first, then build to full sentences
- For pair work: Walk around and ensure both partners are participating

### Common Errors to Address:
- Mixing up "blue" and "black" pronunciation
- Forgetting "is/are" in sentences ("The bag red" ❌ → "The bag is red" ✅)
- Saying "color green" instead of "green color" or just "green"

### Cultural Notes:
- Reference Moroccan flag colors (red and green) frequently
- Use familiar objects: tajine, djellaba, babouches, mint tea
- Respect that some students may wear specific colors for cultural/religious reasons

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on 8-10 basic colors, simple sentences
- **Average students**: All 15 colors, full sentence patterns
- **Advanced students**: Complete high achiever tasks, create longer descriptions

Students successfully completed this lesson if they can:
✅ Name at least 10 colors in English
✅ Say "The [object] is [color]" correctly
✅ Answer "What color is...?" questions
✅ Participate in pair-work color descriptions
✅ Express their favorite color in a full sentence
