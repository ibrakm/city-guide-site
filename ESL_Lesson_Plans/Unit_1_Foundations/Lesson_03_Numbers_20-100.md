# Lesson 3: Numbers 20-100

**Level**: A1  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Numbers 20-100 and Prices  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Count in tens from 20 to 100
- Say and understand numbers 20-100
- Ask and answer "How much...?" questions about prices
- Use numbers in shopping contexts

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Real objects with price tags (or pictures)
- Play money (optional)

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Hello, everyone! Last lesson, we learned numbers 1 to 20. Today, we learn BIGGER numbers: 20, 30, 40, 50... all the way to 100! When do we use big numbers?"

[Wait for responses]

**Teacher continues:**
"Yes! We use big numbers for: MONEY and PRICES! When you go shopping, you need big numbers. In Morocco, things cost 50 dirhams, 100 dirhams, sometimes more! Today you will learn to talk about prices in English!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Think about your school bag. How much did it cost? Was it 50 dirhams? 100 dirhams? 200 dirhams? Just think... don't tell me yet!"

[Let students think]

**Question 2:**
"Look at your friend's pencil case. Guess: How much does it cost? Maybe 20 dirhams? Maybe 30? Make a guess!"

[Let 2-3 students share guesses]

**Question 3:**
"When you go to the souk or the market with your family, what do you buy? And do you know the prices in dirhams?"

[Accept answers: bread, vegetables, fruit, clothes, etc.]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What things can you buy for these prices? I will write categories on the board."

**Teacher writes:**
- 10-30 dirhams: ?
- 40-60 dirhams: ?
- 70-100 dirhams: ?

[Students suggest items. Examples: candy, bread, juice, notebook, pen, sandwich, shirt, shoes, etc.]

**Teacher says:**
"Excellent! You know many things about prices! Now let's learn to say these numbers in English!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Imagine you are in a shop in England or America. You don't speak the language well. But you know NUMBERS. How can this help you? What can you do?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: understand prices, count money, pay correctly, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Exactly! If you know numbers, you can shop anywhere in the world! Numbers are international!"

---

### 2. VOCABULARY & READING (10-12 minutes)

#### Complete Vocabulary List: Tens (20-100)

**Teacher says:**
"First, let's learn the TENS. These are special numbers. Listen and repeat carefully."

| Number | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| 20 | twenty | /ˈtwenti/ |
| 30 | thirty | /ˈθɜːti/ |
| 40 | forty | /ˈfɔːti/ |
| 50 | fifty | /ˈfɪfti/ |
| 60 | sixty | /ˈsɪksti/ |
| 70 | seventy | /ˈsevnti/ |
| 80 | eighty | /ˈeɪti/ |
| 90 | ninety | /ˈnaɪnti/ |
| 100 | one hundred | /wʌn ˈhʌndrəd/ |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "Listen. TWENTY. Repeat: TWENTY."  
**Students:** "Twenty."  
**Teacher:** "Good. THIRTY. Repeat: THIRTY."  
**Students:** "Thirty."

[Continue for all tens]

**Special Pronunciation Notes:**

**Teacher says:**
"Listen carefully! 
- THIRTEEN (13) vs. THIRTY (30) - hear the difference?
  - THIRTEEN - stress at the END: thir-TEEN
  - THIRTY - stress at the START: THIR-ty
- FOURTEEN (14) vs. FORTY (40)
  - FOURTEEN - four-TEEN
  - FORTY - FOR-ty
- FIFTEEN (15) vs. FIFTY (50)
  - FIFTEEN - fif-TEEN
  - FIFTY - FIF-ty"

[Practice these pairs 5 times each with the class]

#### Numbers 21-99 (Pattern Learning)

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
Pattern: [TEN] + [ONE]
21 = twenty-one
22 = twenty-two
23 = twenty-three
35 = thirty-five
47 = forty-seven
68 = sixty-eight
99 = ninety-nine
```

**Teacher explains:**
"To make numbers 21-99, we combine! We say the TEN, then add a DASH (-), then add the ONE. Look:
- 34 = thirty (30) + four (4) = thirty-four
- 56 = fifty (50) + six (6) = fifty-six
- 87 = eighty (80) + seven (7) = eighty-seven

Easy, right?"

**Practice together:**
**Teacher:** "What is 41?"  
**Students:** "Forty-one!"  
**Teacher:** "What is 73?"  
**Students:** "Seventy-three!"

[Practice 10 random numbers together]

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read a shopping story. Listen first."

**Text: "Shopping at the Souk"**

```
My name is Amina. Today I went shopping with my mother. We went to 
the souk in the medina. I wanted to buy a new bag. The first bag was 
sixty dirhams. It was nice, but small. The second bag was eighty-five 
dirhams. It was big and blue. I liked it! My mother bought vegetables. 
Tomatoes were twenty dirhams per kilo. Onions were fifteen dirhams. 
We spent one hundred and fifty dirhams in total. It was a good day!
```

**Teacher reads slowly. Then says:**
"Now, let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "Where did Amina go shopping?"  
**Answer:** "She went to the souk in the medina."

**Q2:** "How much was the first bag?"  
**Answer:** "The first bag was sixty dirhams."

**Q3:** "How much was the second bag?"  
**Answer:** "The second bag was eighty-five dirhams."

**Q4:** "How much were tomatoes per kilo?"  
**Answer:** "Tomatoes were twenty dirhams per kilo."

**Q5:** "How much did they spend in total?"  
**Answer:** "They spent one hundred and fifty dirhams."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "Do you like shopping? Where do you usually shop - souk, supermarket, or small shops?"

[Let 3-4 students answer]

**Q2:** "What was the last thing you bought? Do you remember the price?"

[Encourage: "I bought... It was... dirhams."]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! Take turns reading the text. Then, ask each other the comprehension questions. One person asks, the other answers. Then switch! You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar/Expression

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: How much is [thing]?
Example: How much is this pen?

PATTERN 2: It is [number] dirhams.
Example: It is twenty dirhams.

PATTERN 3: That's expensive / cheap / good.
Example: Fifty dirhams? That's expensive!

PATTERN 4: I want to buy [thing].
Example: I want to buy a notebook.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"When we shop, we ask: 'How much is this?' or 'How much is it?' 
The shopkeeper answers: 'It is [price].'
We can respond: 'That's expensive!' or 'That's cheap!' or 'OK, I'll take it.'"

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and explains:**

1. "How much is this bag?" → "It's seventy dirhams."
2. "How much are these shoes?" → "They're one hundred dirhams."
3. "That's expensive! Do you have a cheaper one?" 
4. "This is cheap. I'll buy it!"
5. "I have fifty dirhams. Is that enough?"

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me:" [Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

**Important note:**
"Notice: ONE thing = 'How much IS it?' / MANY things = 'How much ARE they?'"

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's learn a shopping conversation. I will be the shopkeeper. You are the customers. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "At the Shop"**

```
Customer: Hello! How much is this notebook?
Shopkeeper: It's twenty-five dirhams.
Customer: And how much is this pen?
Shopkeeper: The pen is ten dirhams.
Customer: OK. I want to buy the notebook and the pen.
Shopkeeper: That's thirty-five dirhams, please.
Customer: Here you are.
Shopkeeper: Thank you! Have a nice day!
Customer: You too. Goodbye!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts (students listen)**
2. **Teacher = Shopkeeper, Students = Customer (all together)**
3. **Students = Shopkeeper, Teacher = Customer**
4. **Divide class: Half shopkeepers, Half customers**
5. **Pair work: Students practice in pairs**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice in pairs. Change the items and prices! Use your imagination. One is shopkeeper, one is customer. You have 5 minutes. Then switch roles!"

[Monitor pairs. Provide vocabulary if needed: pencil case, eraser, ruler, book, etc.]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs to perform:**

"Who wants to show their dialogue? Come to the front!"

[After each pair: "Excellent! Very good prices!" / "Great job asking!"]

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Write Numbers in Words

**Teacher says:**
"Write these numbers in English words. Write in your notebook."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. 30 = _______
2. 45 = _______
3. 67 = _______
4. 82 = _______
5. 50 = _______
6. 99 = _______

**Teacher says:**
"You have 3 minutes!"

[Check together]

**Answer Key:**
1. thirty
2. forty-five
3. sixty-seven
4. eighty-two
5. fifty
6. ninety-nine

#### Exercise 2: Price Questions

**Teacher says:**
"Now, answer these questions about prices. Make full sentences."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. A sandwich costs 25 dirhams. How much is it?  
   **Answer:** "It is twenty-five dirhams."

2. Shoes cost 90 dirhams. How much are they?  
   **Answer:** "They are ninety dirhams."

3. You have 50 dirhams. Can you buy a bag for 60 dirhams?  
   **Answer:** "No, I can't. I need ten more dirhams." OR "No, it's too expensive."

4. Bread costs 5 dirhams. Juice costs 15 dirhams. How much in total?  
   **Answer:** "The total is twenty dirhams."

**Teacher says:**
"Write the answers. You have 4 minutes."

[Check together by calling on students]

#### Exercise 3: Shopping Math

**Teacher says:**
"Let's practice shopping math! I will say prices. You add them and tell me the total IN ENGLISH!"

**Teacher says:**

1. "Thirty dirhams + twenty dirhams = ?"  
   **Students answer:** "Fifty dirhams!"

2. "Forty dirhams + fifteen dirhams = ?"  
   **Students answer:** "Fifty-five dirhams!"

3. "Sixty dirhams + thirty dirhams = ?"  
   **Students answer:** "Ninety dirhams!"

4. "Twenty-five dirhams + twenty-five dirhams = ?"  
   **Students answer:** "Fifty dirhams!"

[Do 6-8 quick calculations together]

#### PAIR-WORK Practice

**Teacher says:**
"Work with your partner! One person says a number (20-100). The other person writes it down as a WORD. Check together. Do 5 numbers each. You have 3 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Classroom Market"

**Teacher says:**
"Now, we create a CLASSROOM MARKET! Everyone has things on their desk. You will 'sell' your things to classmates!"

**Setup (2 minutes):**

**Teacher explains:**
"Look at your desk. Choose 3 things to 'sell': pen, notebook, eraser, pencil case, book... anything!

Now, decide the prices:
- Small things: 10-30 dirhams
- Medium things: 40-60 dirhams
- Big things: 70-100 dirhams

Write your prices on a paper!"

[Give students 2 minutes to prepare]

**Activity (6 minutes):**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, stand up! Walk around! You are shoppers AND shopkeepers! 
- When someone comes to you, you are the shopkeeper.
- When you go to someone else, you are the customer.

Use the dialogue we learned:
- 'How much is this?'
- 'It's [price] dirhams.'
- 'OK, I'll take it!' OR 'That's too expensive!'

You have 6 minutes! Go!"

[Monitor the activity. Note good examples]

#### Follow-up Reporting

**Teacher says:**
"Sit down! Let's share! Who bought something interesting?"

**Teacher asks:**
"[Name], what did you buy? How much was it?"

[Student: "I bought a pen from Sara. It was twenty dirhams."]

**Teacher:** "Was it expensive or cheap?"

[Student: "It was cheap!"]

[Call on 4-5 students to report]

#### Follow-up Prompts

- "Who had the most expensive item? How much?"
- "Who had the cheapest item?"
- "Did anyone bargain? Did you ask for a lower price?"

**Q5:** "Say this number: 78."

[Ask different students different questions]

**Teacher calls out:**

2. 66

4. 100

#### Challenge 1: Speed Counting

**Teacher says:**
"Can you count from 20 to 100 by tens in 5 seconds? Try!
Then, try counting BACKWARDS from 100 to 20!"

#### Challenge 2: Complex Prices

**Teacher says:**
"These items have prices. Calculate the totals and say them in English:"

   **Answer:** "Two hundred and five dirhams" (we'll learn 100+ next lessons, but challenge them!)

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Keep the tens (20-100) visible on board throughout lesson
- Use real objects with price tags for authentic practice
- For the classroom market activity, ensure students move around safely
- Encourage bargaining language if students are ready: "Can you make it cheaper?"

### Common Errors to Address:
- Confusing thirteen/thirty, fourteen/forty, fifteen/fifty
- Forgetting the hyphen in compound numbers (forty-five, not forty five)
- Saying "five-ten" instead of "fifty"
- Missing "dirhams" after prices

### Cultural Notes:
- Reference familiar Moroccan shops: souk, Marjane, Aswak Assalam, local hanout
- Use Moroccan dirham (DH) as currency (familiar to students)
- Mention bargaining culture in souks (negotiating prices)

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on tens (20, 30, 40...) and simple combinations (21, 32, 45)
- **Average students**: Master all numbers 20-100, use in prices
- **Advanced students**: Complete all challenges, including comparisons and calculations
