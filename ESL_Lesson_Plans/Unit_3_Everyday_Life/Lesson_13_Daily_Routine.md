# Lesson 13: Daily Routine

**Level**: A1-A2  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Daily Routines & Time Expressions with Present Simple  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Describe daily routine activities (wake up, brush teeth, go to school, etc.)
- Use present simple tense for routines (I wake up, He goes, She eats)
- Tell time in English (o'clock, half past, quarter past/to)
- Use time expressions (in the morning, at night, every day, usually, always)
- Ask and answer "What time do you...?"

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Clock (real or drawn on board)
- Students' own daily schedules

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning! What did you do this morning? Did you wake up? Did you eat breakfast? Did you brush your teeth? These actions are part of your DAILY ROUTINE. A routine is something you do every day, at the same time. Today, we will learn to describe our daily routines in English! This is very useful because we can talk about our lives, compare with friends, and understand each other better!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"What time did you wake up this morning? Think... Was it 6 AM? 7 AM? 8 AM?"

[Let 3-4 students share]

**Teacher responds:** "Interesting! Everyone wakes up at different times! Today we'll learn to say these times in English!"

**Question 2:**
"What is the FIRST thing you do when you wake up? Do you brush your teeth? Do you check your phone? Do you pray? Think about it..."

[Accept answers and write some on board]

**Question 3:**
"Are you a morning person or a night person? Do you like waking up early, or do you prefer staying up late?"

[Show hands: "Who likes mornings?" / "Who likes nights?"]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What activities do you do EVERY DAY? I will write them on the board!"

[Write student suggestions. Common ones:]
- Wake up
- Pray
- Eat breakfast/lunch/dinner
- Go to school
- Do homework
- Watch TV
- Go to bed
- Brush teeth
- Take a shower
- Play

**Teacher says:**
"Excellent! These are all routine activities! Today we'll learn to say all of them in English!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Why do we have routines? Why do we do the same things every day? Is it good or bad to have a routine?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: organization, habits, discipline, comfort, boring, predictable, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Routines help us organize our time and lives. They create good habits! But too much routine can be boring. Balance is important! In English, we can describe our routines to help people understand our lives!"

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar: Present Simple for Routines

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PRESENT SIMPLE: Regular Actions/Routines

I wake up
You wake up
He wakes up ← ADD 'S'!
She wakes up ← ADD 'S'!
We wake up
They wake up

PATTERN:
I/You/We/They + BASE VERB
He/She + BASE VERB + S

Examples:
✓ I go to school every day.
✓ He goes to school at 8 AM.
✓ She has breakfast at 7:00.
✓ We do homework together.
✓ They play football after school.

NEGATIVE:
I don't wake up at 5 AM.
He doesn't wake up at 5 AM.

QUESTIONS:
What time do you wake up?
What time does he wake up?
```

**Teacher explains:**
"When we talk about routines - things we do EVERY DAY or REGULARLY - we use PRESENT SIMPLE. 

Important rule: With HE, SHE, and IT, we ADD 'S' to the verb!
- I wake up → He wakes up
- I go → She goes
- I do → He does

This is very important! Listen for the 'S'!"

#### Time Expressions with Routines

**Teacher writes:**

```
PREPOSITIONS WITH TIME:

AT + specific time: at 7:00, at noon, at night
IN + parts of day: in the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening
ON + days: on Monday, on Friday, on the weekend
EVERY + period: every day, every morning, every week

FREQUENCY ADVERBS (how often):
always (100%)
usually (80%)
often (60%)
sometimes (40%)
rarely (20%)
never (0%)

Position: BEFORE main verb
- I always wake up early.
- She usually eats breakfast.
- They sometimes watch TV.
```

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and drills:**

1. "I always wake up at 6:30 in the morning."
2. "He usually has breakfast at 7:00."
3. "She goes to school at 8:00 every day."
4. "We sometimes watch TV in the evening."
5. "They never go to bed late."
6. "What time do you have lunch? - I have lunch at 1:00 PM."

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me!" [Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's learn a conversation about daily routines. Listen first."

**Dialogue: "What's Your Daily Routine?"**

```
A: Hi! What time do you usually wake up?
B: I wake up at 6:30. What about you?
A: I wake up at 7:00. I'm not a morning person!
B: Ha ha! What do you do first after waking up?
A: First, I brush my teeth and wash my face. Then I have breakfast.
B: Me too! What time do you go to school?
A: I go to school at 8:00. My school starts at 8:30.
B: Mine starts at 8:15, so I leave home at 7:45.
A: What time do you usually go to bed?
B: I usually go to bed at 10:00 PM. Sometimes 10:30 if I have a lot of homework!
A: Same! I'm always tired by 10:00!
B: Me too! Early mornings are hard!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts**
2. **Teacher = A, All students = B**
3. **All students = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Half A, Half B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice with REAL information about their own routines**

**Teacher says:**
"Practice with your partner! Use YOUR real wake-up time, YOUR real school time, YOUR real bedtime! Make it about YOUR life! You have 5 minutes!"

[Monitor and assist]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 3 pairs to perform:**

"Show us your dialogue! Come up!"

[Praise: "Great use of time expressions!" / "Good pronunciation of 'wakes up'!"]

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Routine Comparison

**Teacher says:**
"Write a comparison of YOUR routine vs. your mother's or father's routine. How are they different? Use present simple! (8-10 sentences)"

**Example:**
"I wake up at 7:00 AM, but my father wakes up at 5:30 AM. He prays and then goes to work. I go to school. I have lunch at school, but he has lunch at his office..."

#### Challenge 2: Interview an Adult

**Teacher says:**
"Interview an adult (parent, teacher, or neighbor) about their daily routine. Write a report about their typical day. Include:
- What time they wake up
- What they do in the morning
- Their work/job routine

Compare their routine to yours!"

#### Challenge 3: Weekend vs. Weekday Routine

**Teacher says:**
"Write two paragraphs:
1. Your weekday (school day) routine
2. Your weekend routine

**Teacher says:**
"When it's 3:00 PM in Morocco, what time is it in England? New York? Japan? Research time zones! Then write: 'When I am having dinner at 8:00 PM in Morocco, people in London are...' (Create 5 sentences about different time zones and activities!)"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Pantomiming activities makes vocabulary memorable - don't skip it!
- For "Find Someone Who" activity, set clear boundaries (stay in classroom)
- Time management: Be strict with pair work timing
- Create routine in YOUR class (students arrive, you greet in English, lesson begins)

### Common Errors to Address:
- Forgetting 'S' with he/she: "He wake up" ❌ → "He wakes up" ✅
- Wrong preposition: "at the morning" ❌ → "in the morning" ✅
- "I am wake up" ❌ → "I wake up" ✅ (no "to be" with present simple!)
- "I go to school in 8:00" ❌ → "I go to school at 8:00" ✅

### Cultural Notes:
- Prayer times are part of many Moroccan students' routines - respect and include
- Family dinner time is important in Moroccan culture
- Some students may have different routines during Ramadan - be aware
- Helping at home is common for both boys and girls

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on 10 key activities, basic times (7:00, 8:00), simple sentences
- **Average students**: All activities, varied time expressions, present simple accuracy
- **Advanced students**: Frequency adverbs, complex routines, comparisons
