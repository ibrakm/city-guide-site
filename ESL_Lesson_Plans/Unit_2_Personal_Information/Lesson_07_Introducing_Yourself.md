# Lesson 7: Introducing Yourself

**Level**: A1-A2  
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Target Group**: 12-14 Moroccan students (6th-9th grade)  
**Topic**: Introductions, Greetings, and Basic Personal Information  

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Introduce themselves with name, age, and nationality
- Use formal and informal greetings appropriately
- Ask and answer basic personal information questions
- Understand "to be" verb (am/is/are) in present tense
- Engage in simple introduction dialogues

---

## Materials Needed
- Board and markers
- Name tags or paper for students to write names
- Optional: World map showing Morocco and English-speaking countries

---

## LESSON PLAN

### 1. LEADING DISCUSSION (7-8 minutes)

#### Teacher Script - Opening

**Teacher says:**
"Good morning, everyone! Welcome to Unit 2! Today is very important - we learn how to INTRODUCE OURSELVES in English! When you meet someone new, what do you say? In Arabic, you say 'Salaam alaikum' or 'Labas?' But what about in English? Today you will learn!

Imagine: You travel to England or America. You meet someone new. You need to say: 'Hello! My name is...' This is the MOST important skill in English! Let's begin!"

#### Warm-up Questions

**Question 1:**
"Think about this: When was the last time you introduced yourself to someone new? Maybe a new student in class? A new neighbor? How did you feel?"

[Let 2-3 students share experiences]

**Teacher responds:** "Yes! Meeting new people can be exciting or nervous! In English, we have polite ways to introduce ourselves that make it easier!"

**Question 2:**
"When you meet someone, what information do you usually share? Think... your name? Your age? Where you live? What else?"

[Accept answers and write on board: name, age, from, school, etc.]

**Question 3:**
"Is there a difference between meeting an adult (like a teacher) and meeting a friend your age? How do you speak differently?"

[Guide students to understand formal vs. informal - important in English too!]

#### Brainstorming Activity (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Let's brainstorm! What English greeting words do you already know? Even if you're not sure, tell me!"

[Write student suggestions on board. Common ones they may know:]
- Hello
- Hi
- Good morning
- Goodbye
- Thank you

**Teacher says:**
"Excellent! You already know some English greetings! Today we will learn many more and how to use them correctly!"

#### Critical Thinking Question

**Teacher asks:**
"Why is it important to introduce yourself well? What impression do you make when you introduce yourself clearly and politely?"

[Give thinking time. Accept answers like: people remember you, you seem confident, you make friends, you show respect, etc.]

**Teacher guides:**
"Exactly! Your introduction is your 'first impression.' In English we say: 'You never get a second chance to make a first impression!' That's why we practice!"

### 2. VOCABULARY & READING (10-12 minutes)

#### Complete Vocabulary List: Greetings & Introductions

**Teacher says:**
"Let's learn all the important words for introductions! Listen and repeat carefully."

| English | Pronunciation | When to Use | Example |
|---------|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Hello | /həˈləʊ/ | Formal, any time | "Hello! Nice to meet you." |
| Hi | /haɪ/ | Informal, friends | "Hi! How are you?" |
| Good morning | /ɡʊd ˈmɔːnɪŋ/ | Before 12 PM | "Good morning, teacher!" |
| Good afternoon | /ɡʊd ˌɑːftəˈnuːn/ | 12 PM - 6 PM | "Good afternoon, sir!" |
| Good evening | /ɡʊd ˈiːvnɪŋ/ | After 6 PM | "Good evening, everyone!" |
| Good night | /ɡʊd naɪt/ | Bedtime/leaving at night | "Good night! Sleep well!" |
| How are you? | /haʊ ɑː juː/ | Asking wellbeing | "Hi! How are you?" |
| I'm fine/good | /aɪm faɪn/ /ɡʊd/ | Response | "I'm fine, thank you!" |
| What's your name? | /wɒts jɔː neɪm/ | Asking name | "Hello! What's your name?" |
| My name is... | /maɪ neɪm ɪz/ | Giving name | "My name is Ahmed." |
| I'm... | /aɪm/ | Short form | "I'm Fatima." |
| Nice to meet you | /naɪs tə miːt juː/ | First meeting | "Nice to meet you, Sara!" |
| Where are you from? | /weə ɑː juː frɒm/ | Asking origin | "Where are you from?" |
| I'm from Morocco | /aɪm frɒm məˈrɒkəʊ/ | Stating origin | "I'm from Morocco." |
| How old are you? | /haʊ əʊld ɑː juː/ | Asking age | "How old are you?" |
| I'm [age] years old | /aɪm [age] jɪəz əʊld/ | Stating age | "I'm 13 years old." |
| Goodbye | /ɡʊdˈbaɪ/ | Formal leaving | "Goodbye! See you tomorrow!" |
| Bye | /baɪ/ | Informal leaving | "Bye! See you later!" |
| See you later/soon | /siː juː/ | Casual leaving | "See you later!" |

**Teaching Script:**

**Teacher:** "First, greetings! HELLO. This is formal and polite. Repeat: HELLO."  
**Students:** "Hello."

**Teacher:** "HI. This is informal, for friends. Repeat: HI."  
**Students:** "Hi."

**Teacher:** "GOOD MORNING. We say this before noon - before 12 o'clock. Repeat: GOOD MORNING."  
**Students:** "Good morning."

[Continue with each phrase, repeating 2-3 times]

**Special Notes:**

**Teacher explains:**
"Important! In English:
- 'Good morning' = greeting when you arrive
- 'Good night' = saying goodbye at night (NOT a greeting!)
- 'How are you?' is a greeting! You answer: 'I'm fine, thanks. How are you?'
- Americans often say 'Good!' instead of 'Fine!' Both are correct!"

#### Reading Text

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's read about someone introducing themselves. This is Ahmed's first day at English club. Listen first."

**Text: "My Introduction"**

```
Hello! My name is Ahmed. I am 14 years old. I am from Morocco. I live 
in Casablanca with my family. I am a student at Al-Majd School. I am 
in the 8th grade.

Nice to meet you! I am happy to be here at English Club. I love learning 
English. My favorite subjects are English and math. I am good at sports. 
I play football with my friends.

I am excited to meet new people and practice English. What's your name? 
Where are you from? How old are you? Let's be friends!
```

**Teacher reads slowly and clearly. Then says:**
"Now, let's read together. One, two, three..."

[Read together 2 times]

#### Comprehension Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "What is the boy's name?"  
**Answer:** "His name is Ahmed."

**Q2:** "How old is Ahmed?"  
**Answer:** "He is 14 years old."

**Q3:** "Where is Ahmed from?"  
**Answer:** "He is from Morocco."

**Q4:** "Where does Ahmed live?"  
**Answer:** "He lives in Casablanca."

**Q5:** "What is Ahmed good at?"  
**Answer:** "He is good at sports. / He plays football."

#### Discussion Questions

**Teacher asks:**

**Q1:** "Is Ahmed's introduction good? What information does he give?"

[Accept: name, age, from, school, interests - good complete introduction!]

**Q2:** "How do YOU feel when you meet new people? Excited? Nervous? Shy?"

[Let 3-4 students share feelings]

**Q3:** "What would YOU add to your introduction? What makes you special?"

[Encourage: hobbies, family, dreams, skills]

#### PAIR-WORK Activity

**Teacher says:**
"Work in pairs! Take turns reading Ahmed's introduction. Then, tell your partner about YOURSELF using the same pattern! You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs, ensure both partners speak]

### 3. LANGUAGE FOCUS (15 minutes)

#### Key Grammar: "To Be" Verb (am/is/are)

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
Subject Pronouns + TO BE:

I am (I'm)
You are (You're)
He is (He's)
She is (She's)
It is (It's)
We are (We're)
They are (They're)

USAGE IN INTRODUCTIONS:
- I am Ahmed. / I'm Ahmed.
- You are from Morocco. / You're from Morocco.
- He is 14 years old. / He's 14 years old.
- She is my friend. / She's my friend.
- We are students. / We're students.
- They are Moroccan. / They're Moroccan.
```

**Teacher explains:**
"The verb 'TO BE' is the MOST IMPORTANT verb in English! We use it to say:
- WHO we are: 'I am Ahmed.'
- WHERE we are from: 'I am from Morocco.'
- HOW OLD we are: 'I am 14 years old.'
- OUR JOB/STATUS: 'I am a student.'

We can use the FULL form: 'I am' or the SHORT form (contraction): 'I'm'
In conversation, we usually use the short form!"

#### Introduction Patterns

**Teacher writes on board:**

```
PATTERN 1: Hello! My name is [name]. I am [age] years old.
Example: Hello! My name is Sara. I am 13 years old.

PATTERN 2: Hi! I'm [name]. I'm from [place].
Example: Hi! I'm Youssef. I'm from Marrakech.

PATTERN 3: Nice to meet you! I am [name]. I am a student.
Example: Nice to meet you! I am Leila. I am a student at English Center.

QUESTIONS:
- What's your name? / What is your name?
- How old are you?
- Where are you from?
```

#### Example Sentences

**Teacher writes and drills:**

1. "I am Karim. I am 14 years old. I am from Rabat."
2. "She is Fatima. She is my friend. She is 13 years old."
3. "We are students. We are from Morocco. We are learning English!"
4. "What's your name? - My name is Hassan. / I'm Hassan."
5. "How old are you? - I'm 15 years old."
6. "Where are you from? - I'm from Fes."

**Teacher says:**
"Repeat after me!" [Students repeat each sentence 2 times]

#### Scripted Dialogue

**Teacher says:**
"Now, let's learn a complete introduction dialogue. This is how English speakers meet for the first time. Listen!"

**Dialogue: "Meeting for the First Time"**

```
Person A: Hello! My name is Sara. What's your name?
Person B: Hi, Sara! I'm Hassan. Nice to meet you!
Person A: Nice to meet you too, Hassan! How are you?
Person B: I'm good, thanks! How are you?
Person A: I'm fine! Where are you from, Hassan?
Person B: I'm from Tangier. What about you?
Person A: I'm from Casablanca. How old are you?
Person B: I'm 14 years old. And you?
Person A: I'm 13. Are you a student?
Person B: Yes, I am! I'm in 8th grade. What about you?
Person A: I'm in 7th grade. Nice talking to you!
Person B: You too! See you later!
```

**Teaching Steps:**

1. **Teacher reads both parts (students listen)**
2. **Teacher = A, Students (all) = B**
3. **Students (all) = A, Teacher = B**
4. **Divide class: Left side = A, Right side = B**
5. **Pair work: Students practice with THEIR OWN real information**

**Teacher says:**
"Now, practice with your partner! Use YOUR real names, ages, and cities! Make it personal! You have 5 minutes. I will listen to everyone!"

[Monitor all pairs, help with pronunciation and grammar]

#### Role-Play Follow-up

**Teacher invites 4-5 pairs to perform for class:**

"Who wants to introduce themselves to the class? Come to the front!"

[After each pair: "Excellent introduction!" / "Very polite!" / "Clear pronunciation!"]

### 4. GUIDED PRACTICE (10 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Complete with AM, IS, or ARE

**Teacher says:**
"Fill in the blanks with the correct form of 'to be': am, is, or are."

**Teacher writes on board:**

1. I ______ from Morocco.
2. She ______ 13 years old.
3. They ______ students.
4. You ______ my friend.
5. He ______ from Casablanca.
6. We ______ in English class.
7. I ______ happy today.
8. It ______ a beautiful day.

**Students write answers. After 3 minutes, check together:**

**Answer Key:**
1. am
2. is
3. are
4. are
5. is
6. are
7. am
8. is

#### Exercise 2: Make Questions

**Teacher says:**
"Turn these sentences into questions!"

**Teacher writes:**

1. Your name is Ahmed. → What ________?
2. You are 14 years old. → How old ________?
3. You are from Fes. → Where ________?
4. You are a student. → ________ you a student?

**Answer Key:**
1. What is your name?
2. How old are you?
3. Where are you from?
4. Are you a student?

#### Exercise 3: Write About Yourself

**Teacher says:**
"Complete these sentences about YOU! Write TRUE information!"

**Teacher writes:**

1. My name is __________.
2. I am __________ years old.
3. I am from __________.
4. I am a __________.
5. I am __________ and __________. (two adjectives: happy, excited, smart, friendly, etc.)

[Students write their own answers, then share with class]

**Teacher calls on 8-10 students to read their sentences**

#### PAIR-WORK Information Gap

**Teacher says:**
"Interview your partner! Ask these questions and write their answers:"

**Questions:**
1. What's your name?
2. How old are you?
3. Where are you from? (which city?)
4. What grade are you in?
5. What do you like to do?

**Teacher says:**
"After interviewing, you will introduce your PARTNER to the class! You have 4 minutes!"

[Monitor pairs]

**Follow-up:**
"Now, introduce your partner to the class! Say: 'This is [name]. He/She is...' Go!"

[Each student introduces their partner to the class - great practice!]

### 5. COMMUNICATIVE TASK (10 minutes)

#### Real-Life Scenario: "Speed Meeting"

**Teacher says:**
"Now we play 'SPEED MEETING'! You will meet 5 different classmates in 5 minutes! Each meeting is 1 minute. You introduce yourself, ask questions, and say goodbye. Then, move to a new partner!"

**Activity Setup (1 minute):**

**Teacher explains:**
"I will say 'START!' You meet the person next to you. Introduce yourself! Ask 2-3 questions! When I say 'SWITCH!', say goodbye and move to meet someone new! Ready?"

**Activity (6 minutes):**

[Teacher times each meeting for 1 minute, then calls "SWITCH!"]

**Round 1**: Meet Partner 1 (1 min)  
**Round 2**: Meet Partner 2 (1 min)  
**Round 3**: Meet Partner 3 (1 min)  
**Round 4**: Meet Partner 4 (1 min)  
**Round 5**: Meet Partner 5 (1 min)

[During activity, monitor and encourage use of full introductions]

#### Follow-up Reporting (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
"Sit down! That was fun! Now tell me: Who did you meet? What did you learn?"

**Teacher asks:**

1. "Who met someone new today? What's their name?"
2. "Did anyone find someone with the same age? Same city?"
3. "What was the most interesting thing you learned about someone?"

[Let 4-5 students share]

#### Follow-up Prompts

- "How do you feel after meeting 5 people in English?"
- "Was it easier the second time? The third time?"
- "Which question was hardest to answer? Why?"

**Q2:** "How old are you?" [Ask 2 students]

**Teacher says:**
"Write three sentences introducing yourself! Use:
1. My name is...

[Students write, teacher checks quickly]

**Teacher asks:**
"Today we learned how to introduce ourselves in English. When will you use this skill in real life?"

**Teacher concludes:**

### 7. HOMEWORK (Optional)

#### Option A - Writing Task (80-100 words)

**Teacher writes on board:**

"Write a paragraph introducing yourself. Include:
- Your name
- Your age
- Where you're from
- Your grade/school
- Your family (briefly)
- Your hobbies or interests
- Why you're learning English"

**Example:**
```
Hello! My name is Amina. I am 13 years old. I am from Marrakech, Morocco. 
I am a student at Al-Amal School. I am in 7th grade. I live with my parents, 
my two brothers, and my grandmother. I love reading books and playing 
basketball. My favorite subject is English because I want to travel to 
England someday. I am also good at drawing. I am learning English because 
it's an international language. I want to communicate with people from 
different countries. I am excited to improve my English! Nice to meet you!
```

#### Option B - Recording Task

**Teacher says:**

1. Greeting (Hello/Hi)
2. Your name
3. Your age
4. Where you're from
5. Something about your family
6. Your hobbies
7. Why you're learning English
8. Closing (Nice to meet you!)

Speak clearly and with confidence! Send me the recording."

### 8. HIGH ACHIEVERS SECTION

#### Challenge 1: Extended Introduction

**Teacher says:**
"Write a longer, more detailed introduction (120-150 words). Include:
- All basic information
- Your personality (I am friendly/shy/funny/hardworking...)
- Your dreams and goals
- What makes you unique
- A question to the reader/listener"

**Example:**
```
Hello, everyone! Allow me to introduce myself. My name is Youssef, but my 
friends call me Youssi. I am 14 years old, and I am from the beautiful city 
of Fes, Morocco. I am currently in 8th grade at Ibn Khaldoun School.

I am a curious and ambitious person. I love learning new things, especially 
languages. Besides Arabic and French, I am now learning English! I am also 
passionate about science and technology. In my free time, I enjoy reading 
science fiction books and playing chess. I am quite good at chess - I even 
won a school tournament last year!

My dream is to become a software engineer and create apps that help people. 
I believe English will help me achieve this dream because it's the language 
of technology and innovation.

What about you? What are your dreams? I'd love to hear about them!
```

#### Challenge 2: Formal vs. Informal Introductions

**Teacher says:**
"Write TWO different introductions for yourself:
1. FORMAL introduction (meeting a teacher or adult)
2. INFORMAL introduction (meeting a classmate or friend your age)

Notice the differences in language!"

**Example:**

**Formal:**
```
Good morning. My name is Sara Hassan. I am 13 years old. I am a student at 
Al-Majd School in Casablanca. I am pleased to meet you. I am interested in 
English literature and mathematics. I am hardworking and respectful. Thank 
you for this opportunity to introduce myself.
```

**Informal:**
```
Hey! I'm Sara! I'm 13. I'm from Casa. I go to Al-Majd School. I love English 
and math - yeah, I know, kind of nerdy! 😊 I also love music and hanging out 
with my friends. What about you? What do you like to do?
```

#### Challenge 3: Cultural Comparison

**Teacher says:**
"Research: How do people introduce themselves in different cultures? Compare:
- Morocco (Arabic culture)
- England (British culture)
- USA (American culture)

Write 6-8 sentences about the differences and similarities."

**Example:**
```
In Morocco, when we meet someone, we often ask about family and health. We 
shake hands, and sometimes men kiss on the cheeks. In England, introductions 
are more formal. People shake hands firmly and maintain eye contact. They 
usually say "How do you do?" which is more formal than "How are you?" In 
the USA, introductions are friendlier and more casual. Americans often use 
first names immediately and may say "Nice to meet you!" with a big smile. 
All cultures value politeness, but the style is different!
```

#### Challenge 4: Interview Project

**Teacher says:**
"Interview an adult (parent, teacher, or family member) in English! Ask:
1. What's your name?
2. How old are you? (optional - some don't like to answer!)
3. Where are you from?
4. What is your job?
5. What do you like to do in your free time?

Write a report about the interview (100-120 words)."

#### Challenge 5: Create a Video Introduction

**Teacher says:**
"Make a 2-3 minute video introducing yourself in English! Be creative! You can:
- Show your room or favorite place
- Include photos of your family or hobbies
- Use props (objects that represent you)
- Add background music
- Make it fun and interesting!

This is like a YouTube 'About Me' video!"

## TEACHER NOTES

### Classroom Management Tips:
- Start class by greeting each student individually in English
- Model perfect introductions yourself before asking students
- Create a supportive, low-anxiety environment for speaking
- Praise all attempts, focus on communication over perfect grammar
- Use name tags for first few lessons to help students remember names

### Common Errors to Address:
- "I have 14 years" ❌ → "I am 14 years old" ✅
- "I am come from Morocco" ❌ → "I am from Morocco" OR "I come from Morocco" ✅
- Forgetting "to be" verb: "I Ahmed" ❌ → "I am Ahmed" ✅
- Wrong pronoun: "She is a boy" ❌ → "He is a boy" ✅

### Cultural Notes:
- In Morocco, asking about family is common and shows interest
- Eye contact may be less direct in Moroccan culture than in Western cultures
- Handshakes are common between same genders
- English speakers often introduce themselves with first name only (informal)
- "How are you?" is a greeting, not always a real question about health

### Differentiation:
- **Weaker students**: Focus on basic pattern "My name is... I am... years old. I am from..."
- **Average students**: Full introduction with all patterns
- **Advanced students**: Extended introductions, formal/informal differences, cultural comparisons

### Connection to Previous Lessons:
- Numbers (age) - Lesson 2
- Countries/places - can expand vocabulary
- Builds foundation for ALL future communication
