## UNIT 2: PERSONAL INFORMATION (Lessons 8-12)

### Lesson 8: Talking About Family

**Vocabulary (20+ words):**
- Family members: mother, father, sister, brother, grandmother, grandfather, aunt, uncle, cousin, parents, siblings, son, daughter, husband, wife
- Only child, eldest, youngest, twins
- Extended family

**Grammar Focus:**
- Possessive adjectives (my, your, his, her, our, their)
- "I have..." for family
- "My [family member] is..."

**Reading Text:**
A student describes their family - who lives in the house, ages, jobs, personalities

**Communicative Activity:**
- Draw your family tree and present it
- "Find someone who..." activity (has 3 sisters, lives with grandparents, is an only child, etc.)
- Family interview questions

**Dialogue Example:**
```
A: Tell me about your family!
B: I have a big family. I have two brothers and one sister.
A: Wow! Are you the oldest?
B: No, I'm the youngest. My oldest brother is 18 years old.
```

**High Achievers:**
- Write detailed family descriptions including personalities
- Compare family sizes in different cultures
- Create a family tree going back to grandparents

### Lesson 9: Describing People - Physical Appearance

**Vocabulary (25+ words):**
- Height: tall, short, medium height
- Build: thin, slim, fat, overweight, well-built, muscular
- Hair: long, short, curly, straight, wavy, bald, blonde, brown, black, red, gray
- Eyes: blue, brown, green, hazel, small, big
- Face: round, oval, square, handsome, beautiful, pretty, cute
- Other: beard, mustache, glasses, freckles, dimples

**Grammar Focus:**
- "He/She is..." + adjective
- "He/She has..." + noun
- "He/She looks like..."

**Reading Text:**
Description of different family members' appearances

**Communicative Activity:**
- "Guess who" game - describe a classmate, others guess
- Partner descriptions - describe your partner, they draw you!
- Celebrity description challenge

**Dialogue Example:**
```
A: What does your sister look like?
B: She's tall and slim. She has long, curly black hair.
A: What color are her eyes?
B: Her eyes are brown. She's very pretty!
A: Does she wear glasses?
B: Yes, she wears glasses for reading.
```

**High Achievers:**
- Detailed description using compound sentences
- Compare two people (my brother is taller than my sister)
- Write "wanted poster" descriptions

**Vocabulary (25+ words):**

A: What's your personality like?

A: Are you shy?
B: No, not really. But I'm nervous when I speak in front of many people.
A: Me too! That's normal!

**High Achievers:**

### Lesson 29: Meeting New People (Small Talk)

**Vocabulary (25+ words):**
- Conversation starters: "Hi! How are you?", "Nice weather today!", "Have we met before?", "What do you do?"
- Small talk topics: weather, hobbies, school, sports, current events (light)

**Reading Text:**

**Communicative Activity:**
- Speed meeting (like speed dating but for making friends!)
- Conversation practice with conversation cards
- Breaking the ice games

**Dialogue Example:**
```

B: Hi Hassan! I'm Sara. Nice to meet you!
A: Nice to meet you too! Are you new here?
B: Yes, I just started at this English center last week.
A: Cool! How do you like it so far?
B: I love it! Everyone is really friendly. What about you? How long have you been here?
A: About three months. It's great! So, what do you like to do for fun?
B: I love reading and playing basketball. What about you?
A: I like basketball too! We should play together sometime!
B: That would be great! Do you have Instagram? We can keep in touch!

```

**Topics for Small Talk:**
- Weather ("Beautiful day today!")
- Weekend/holiday plans
- School/studies

- Popular TV shows/movies
- Sports
- Local events

**Small Talk DON'Ts (cultural sensitivity):**
- Don't ask very personal questions immediately (money, politics, religion)
- Don't complain too much
- Don't dominate the conversation
- Don't use your phone while talking

**High Achievers:**
- Research small talk customs in English-speaking countries

## TEACHING TIPS FOR ALL LESSONS

### 1. Timing Management
- Use a timer! Stick to section timings
- If running over, cut from guided practice, not communicative tasks
- If running under, extend communicative tasks or add games

### 2. Pair/Group Work Tips
- Assign partners randomly or strategically (strong with weak)
- Walk around during all pair work - monitor and help
- Give time warnings: "You have 2 more minutes!"
- Have fast finishers help slower pairs

### 3. Error Correction
- During fluency tasks: note errors, correct after
- During accuracy practice: correct immediately but gently
- Use "teacher echo" - repeat student's sentence correctly
- Encourage peer correction

### 4. Differentiation Strategies
- **For weaker students:**
  - Seat near teacher for extra support
  - Provide sentence starters
  - Allow extra time
  - Pair with patient, helpful students
  - Simplify tasks (fewer items, shorter texts)

- **For stronger students:**
  - High achiever challenges (every lesson has these!)
  - Leadership roles (help explain to others)
  - Extended writing/speaking tasks
  - Research projects
  - Teaching assistant role

### 5. Moroccan Cultural Integration
- Use Moroccan examples consistently (cities, food, customs)
- Reference Islamic practices respectfully (prayer times, Ramadan, halal)
- Include Arabic/French cognates when helpful
- Discuss Moroccan-English differences positively
- Celebrate Moroccan identity while learning English

### 6. Engaging Activities
- Use games frequently (Bingo, Simon Says, Charades, Hangman, Board Race)
- Include movement (TPR activities, walk around interviews)
- Use technology when available (YouTube videos, online games)
- Create competitions (team points, individual rewards)
- Celebrate progress visibly (progress charts, certificates)

### 7. Homework Management
- Make homework optional but encouraged
- Provide two options (writing or recording)
- Give clear examples
- Check homework briefly at start of next lesson
- Praise students who complete homework

### 8. Assessment Balance
- Informal assessment every lesson (mini-tests)
- Formal assessment every 6 lessons (review tests)
- Focus on communication over perfection
- Celebrate attempts, not just accuracy
- Provide constructive feedback
