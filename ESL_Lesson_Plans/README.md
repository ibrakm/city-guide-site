# ESL Lesson Plans: A1-A2 Level (Moroccan Students, 6th-9th Grade)

## Overview
This collection contains **30 complete, classroom-ready lesson plans** designed for mixed-level groups of 12-14 Moroccan teenage students (A1в†’A2 proficiency). Each lesson is 60 minutes long and structured to maximize student speaking time (60-70%).

## Target Audience
- **Age**: 12-16 years (6th-9th grade)
- **Level**: Beginner to Pre-Intermediate (A1-A2)
- **Class Size**: 12-14 students
- **Context**: English Center in Morocco

## Lesson Structure (Every Lesson Follows This Format)

### 1. Leading Discussion (7-8 minutes)
- 3-4 warm-up questions
- Brainstorming activity
- 1 critical thinking question
- Full teacher script included

### 2. Vocabulary & Reading/Listening (10-12 minutes)
- Complete vocabulary lists with examples
- Short text or dialogue (6-10 sentences)
- Comprehension questions
- Pair-work activities

### 3. Language Focus (15 minutes)
- Grammar or expression explanation
- Example patterns
- Scripted dialogues
- Role-play activities

### 4. Guided Practice (10 minutes)
- Gap-fill exercises
- Sentence completion
- Pair checking activities

### 5. Communicative Task (10 minutes)
- Real-life scenario role-plays
- Pair or small group work
- Follow-up prompts

### 6. Wrap-up & Mini-test (5 minutes)
- 3-4 assessment questions
- Reflection question
- Peer testing option

### 7. Homework (Optional)
- Writing or recording tasks

### 8. High Achievers Section
- Extra challenges in every lesson
- Extension activities

## Units Overview

### Unit 1: Foundations (Lessons 1-6)
Building blocks: colors, numbers, classroom language, time concepts, weather

### Unit 2: Personal Information (Lessons 7-12)
Identity, family, descriptions, school life, hobbies

### Unit 3: Everyday Life (Lessons 13-18)
Routines, food, shopping, home, city navigation

### Unit 4: Experiences & Opinions (Lessons 19-24)
Preferences, past experiences, future plans, social topics

### Unit 5: Integration & Real Life (Lessons 25-30)
Practical scenarios, integration of all skills

## Review Tests
- Review Test 1 (After Lesson 6)
- Review Test 2 (After Lesson 12)
- Review Test 3 (After Lesson 18)
- Review Test 4 (After Lesson 24)
- Final Speaking Test (Lesson 30)

## How to Use These Lesson Plans

1. **Read the entire lesson** before class
2. **Prepare materials**: Board markers, any printed handouts (if desired)
3. **Follow the teacher script** word-for-word for consistency
4. **Use the timing guidelines** to stay on track
5. **Adapt pacing** based on student responses (but maintain structure)
6. **Monitor pair/group work** actively during activities
7. **Challenge high achievers** with extension tasks in each section
